# QCOM: persistent short vs. edge-AI turnaround

Two tracked publications genuinely disagree on Qualcomm as of mid-2026.

**Bear side — [[irrationalanalysis]] (persistent SHORT, ~2 years).** Thesis: Apple's in-house modem strips ~$1.2B+ of Qualcomm revenue by 2027-28; the QTL licensing cliff (April 2027) has no offset; data-center/CPU ambitions are vapor; talent exodus. Priced shorts at **$163.08 (Oct 2024)** and **$177 (Apr 2026)**.

**Constructive side — [[semidoped]] (NEUTRAL/cautiously constructive, 2026-06-29).** From the Investor Day: **High Bandwidth Compute (HBC)** (stacking LPDDR on logic, no CoWoS), the Alphawave interconnect IP, the Modular/Mojo open software stack, the C1000 CPU with **Meta** as customer, and a large **edge-AI/robotics** vision give Qualcomm real long-term optionality — even if the data-center entry is late and HBC is unproven slideware.

**Scoreboard so far (price):** the bear side is **losing**. QCOM sits ~$191.65 after popping ~23% on the June-29 Investor Day; both priced shorts ($163.08, $177) are underwater (✗/✗, resolved). The market is rewarding the optionality Semi Doped describes and ignoring the Apple-modem cliff.

**What resolves it:**
- Bull confirmed if HBC ships on schedule (AI250 in 2027) with credible hyperscaler data-center traction, and edge-AI/automotive revenue inflects.
- Bear confirmed if HBC slips / no data-center design wins by 2028 AND the Apple modem + QTL cliffs bite FY27-28 with no offset, re-rating the stock down.

Watch: AI200 sampling (2026) reception; any technical paper / Hot Chips disclosure proving HBC works; Apple modem ramp %; QTL renewal news.
