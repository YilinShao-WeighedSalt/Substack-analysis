[[irrationalanalysis]] covers the rumor — first reported by The Information — that Marvell is in advanced talks to acquire Celestial AI in a multi-billion-dollar deal. The author had heard the rumor from a single source roughly a month prior but held off writing until the story circulated widely.

The core thesis is that Marvell *needs* this acquisition. Their existing co-packaged optics (CPO) approach relies on DR-MZI (dual-ring Mach-Zehnder interferometer) silicon photonics modulators, which the author argues was effectively vaporware at the OCP Global Summit 2025 — Marvell presented no lane-turn-on data or post-FEC performance results, in stark contrast to Broadcom's CPO presentation which included performance, reliability, and power data. Circumstantially, Marvell's CPO marketing lead resigned shortly after the conference and announced joining Nvidia on the same day the Celestial rumor broke widely.

Celestial AI's technology is based on Germanium EAM (electroabsorption modulator) silicon photonics. The post provides a technical breakdown: EAMs are well-established in traditional EML transceivers (laser + EAM on monolithic Indium Phosphide substrate), and Celestial applies a Germanium-based variant within a SiPho platform. The author flags the key outstanding risk: Germanium content introduces reliability concerns — Germanium is present as photodiodes in all SiPho platforms and is sensitive to ESD damage and yield/reliability failure; Germanium EAMs additionally face degradation ("burning out") over time. The author explicitly does not take a position on whether this risk is solvable, but identifies it as the single most important open question. Several other technical risks are characterized as minor with clear engineering paths to resolution.

Bottom line: the author views the deal as a good strategic move for Marvell, a nice outcome for Celestial shareholders, and technically exciting — conditional on Celestial having a credible plan for the Germanium EAM reliability issue.
