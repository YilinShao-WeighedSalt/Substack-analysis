[[irrationalanalysis]] covers every major holding in the VanEck SMH semiconductor ETF, giving individual views on thirteen names. The post doubles as a portfolio update following a brutal drawdown in a leveraged trading account.

**Nvidia & Broadcom:** The author firmly rejects the prevalent buy-side thesis of "long AVGO, short/avoid NVDA." The Hyperscaler ASIC programs (Trainium 3, Maia 2) are viewed as largely failing — only Google TPU is rated as genuinely competitive, and Google itself still buys Nvidia GPUs internally and signed a new CoreWeave contract. Nvidia is described as at least 18 months ahead on engineering, with rack-scale systems and leading SerDes as durable new moats. Broadcom's ASIC franchise is respected but not seen as an existential threat to Nvidia.

**TSMC & Intel:** TSMC is called an "incredibly powerful earned monopoly." Intel Foundry is dismissed as non-viable until 2027 — Intel IR itself reportedly talked down 18A and distanced itself from Pat Gelsinger on a private investor call. Intel shares are expected to go nowhere for 12 months; long-dated call options are floated as the only viable structure.

**Qualcomm:** Rated as still overvalued despite appearing "cheap." Key risks: flagship Android OEMs (Xiaomi, Samsung) developing in-house SoCs, Samsung Foundry recovering and reducing Qualcomm's 100% Galaxy share, PC market share stuck below 0.5% despite heavy investment, and dilutive automotive margins. The author calls it a "slow burn excellent funding short."

**Applied Materials & KLA:** AMAT is avoided due to the ICAPS headwind (China fabs booking equipment in the ≥10nm bucket). KLA is the preferred semicap name; process control intensity scales disproportionately with complexity, giving KLA structural outperformance. KLA is described as outpacing Onto Innovation and Camtek in market share.

**AMD:** Not an AI story. AMD's MI-series is "competitive on paper" but software ecosystem is weak, SerDes lags Nvidia's NVLink, and AMD prioritized a $6B buyback over the software investment Semianalysis had urged. FPGAs (Xilinx) may have cyclically bottomed; PC/laptop share gains vs. Intel are acknowledged as a positive trade catalyst.

**Micron:** Described as "the crystal meth of semiconductor investing" — violently cyclical and dangerous for retail. Key risks include Samsung 12-Hi HBM3e qualification at Nvidia and CXMT flooding DDR5.

**Cadence & Synopsys:** Competently-run earned EDA duopoly. Cadence is better positioned in the chiplet/advanced packaging era (Clarity3D, analog/3D-EM simulation). Synopsys needs its Ansys acquisition to close to avoid a bundling disadvantage. Valuation discount for Synopsys reflects lumpier, lower-quality IP revenue (~30% of sales vs ~10% at Cadence).

**Marvell:** AI ASIC wins are at risk; Amazon has pitted Marvell against Alchip for Trainium 3 and Alchip currently has the upper hand. Microsoft has paid NRE to both Marvell and Broadcom — author views Marvell SerDes as materially inferior to Broadcom's, putting the perceived Maia 2 win in serious doubt. oDSP business also under pressure.

**Monolithic Power:** Makes best-in-class switching regulators but faces design-out risk given easy second-sourcing and price competition. Regained Nvidia content after competitors stumbled. Long-term decent; short-term volatile.
