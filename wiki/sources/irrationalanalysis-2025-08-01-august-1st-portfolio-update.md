[[irrationalanalysis]] publishes a candid mid-year portfolio update that combines full P&L transparency with brief thesis updates across a wide range of semiconductor and adjacent names. The author is upfront about being heavily biased — all profits derive from personal trading, with zero external monetization — and discloses a 2025 YTD trading account that recovered from a –47.5% / –$172K drawdown in late March to a strongly positive result by August 1st.

The author de-levered and withdrew cash ahead of what proved to be renewed tariff volatility, framing the exit as lucky timing. Broad market charts are described as "very bad," with little worth buying at current levels.

**Key position-level commentary:**

- **AEHR**: Sold half at $20.88 to book profit; original thesis (undisclosed product catalyst) has not yet played out. Remaining position kept with conviction; would size up 2–3x if risk appetite allowed.
- **SKYT (Skywater Technology)**: Speculative long on the only 100%-US logic foundry, valued for captive US military fab supply despite poor PDK and node quality. Paired with FREQ (Frequency Electronics) as a defense-timing niche idea — both viewed as pure military plays.
- **QCOM**: Active short. Author argues tariffs disproportionately hurt consumer electronics and that management is incompetent. Made ~$10K on puts around earnings. Frames a long/short pair: short QCOM, long almost any other large-cap semicon.
- **VZ (Verizon)**: Defensive long with undercovered AI upside from the Frontier acquisition. Teases undisclosed Apple 2026 modem read-through via C-band commentary.
- **BESI (BE Semiconductor)**: Long-term conviction buy, primary thesis is co-packaged optics and Broadcom's hybrid-bonding 3.5D XPU platform; HBM4 is upside, not the core case.
- **KLAC (KLA)**: Wants to re-enter but cautious — believes Intel capex cuts are not yet priced in.
- **ALAB (Astera Labs)**: Short. Disputes Semianalysis ASP estimates and calls the product engineering "shit" with probable re-timer dependencies; maintains spreadsheet model.
- **MRVL (Marvell)**: Ongoing short. Booked $12.4K in 2025 shorting RSUs and ~$88.4K on 2024 call options. Expects further negative catalyst from undisclosed channel checks.
- **SITM (SiTime)**: Long in long-only accounts; concerned about consumer-electronics exposure and Apple pull-forward. Waiting for chart improvement before re-entering in trading account.
- **TSEM / GFS pair**: Long Tower Semi, short GlobalFoundries. Author says GloFo's silicon-photonics process node is "dogshit" with unanimous negative engineering feedback; pair trade to be set up after broad market pullback.
- **KEYS (Keysight)**: One of few charts not broken; adding to long-only accounts as defensive position.
- **MU (Micron)**: Cautious. Notes Samsung may slash HBM3E-12 prices, putting Micron at a disadvantage as Nvidia plays both suppliers. Only $8.8K profit in 2025 despite heavy risk — author describes this as a mistake.
- **AAOI**: Short-term trading long. Engineering view is that IR claims are largely marketing; author is willing to hold for up to two weeks for a trade setup.
