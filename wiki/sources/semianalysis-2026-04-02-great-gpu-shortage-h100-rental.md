[[semianalysis]] launches a new H100 1-Year Rental Price Index and documents a sharp tightening in GPU rental markets through early 2026, arguing that prices are more likely to continue rising than to fall.

**The core data point** is a ~40% increase in H100 1-year rental prices — from roughly $1.70/hour in October 2025 to $2.35/hour by March 2026. The index tracks 1-year contracts as the most demand-sensitive segment of the market: short-term (on-demand/spot/<3 months) reflects residual capacity with infrequently updated pricing, while long-term (4-5 year) contracts are dominated by hyperscale AI labs locking up 50-100 MW clusters. The 1-year mid-tier is where marginal supply/demand clears.

**Supply constraints are severe.** All on-demand capacity across H100, H200, and early Blackwell is sold out. H100/H200 contracts signed in 2023 are renewing at the same rates through 2028. Blackwell deployment lead times extend into June-July 2026, with all capacity through September 2026 already booked. The article characterizes finding available GPU clusters as resembling "trying to buy drugs" rather than standard procurement — neocloud providers now dictate terms, demanding 20%+ prepayments and longer contract durations.

**Demand is structural, not cyclical.** Anthropic's annualized revenue reportedly tripled from $9B to over $30B in a single quarter on Claude model and Claude Code demand. Multi-agent workflows, native media generation (Seedance, Nano Banana), and broad enterprise AI deployment are compounding baseline token consumption. The article argues that "AI tools can deliver value an order of magnitude greater than the cost of using the tools," implying substantial pricing runway before demand destruction.

**Memory pricing is a secondary constraint.** LPDDR5 and DDR5 spot prices increased 4-5x year-on-year in Q1 2026, forcing OEMs to reprice servers beyond underlying component increases — withholding supply and compressing deployment economics at the margin.

**The public market disconnect** is flagged explicitly: despite these improving fundamentals, publicly traded neocloud providers CoreWeave (CRWV), Nebius (NBIS), and IREN trade near 6-12 month lows, as market sentiment remains anchored to eventual oversupply narratives. The article frames this as a mispricing opportunity, contingent on GB300 ramp timing, silicon supply (TSMC N3, HBM, DRAM, NAND), and AI lab ARR trajectory.
