[[semianalysis]] argues that AWS is uniquely positioned for margin expansion while Azure and Google Cloud stagnate, owing to three structural advantages: a Token-as-a-Service (TaaS) business model via Bedrock, superior datacenter capacity deployment, and proprietary custom silicon (Trainium/Graviton).

**Bedrock and Anthropic financials.** Bedrock revenue reached approximately $5.5B annualized run rate in Q1 2026, representing 37% of AWS AI revenue (up from 9% in Q1 2025), with sequential growth of 170% and 60% in Q1 2026 and Q4 2025 respectively. AWS AI revenue rose from 2% of total AWS revenue in Q1 2024 to 10% in Q1 2026, and AWS EBIT margins expanded 213 basis points quarter-over-quarter. Anthropic's ARR reached approximately $30B by Q1 2026 (up from $9B), with $21B net new ARR added in Q1 alone; inference gross margins improved to mid-60s from 38% in 2025 and negative 94% in 2024. Anthropic is projected to reach operating income profitability in Q2 2026 (ex-SBC), with potential ARR exceeding $100B by year-end. Claude Code adoption drove majority market share of net new enterprise customers versus OpenAI.

**Deal structure.** The Anthropic–Bedrock arrangement includes fixed IaaS infrastructure fees, revenue-sharing components, and performance-linked payments triggered at throughput thresholds. Authors model Bedrock achieving ~55% EBIT margins at current Anthropic volumes, versus AWS's blended low-60s inference gross margins on other AI products. Trainium chips now power more than 50% of Amazon Bedrock token usage, insulating margin from third-party GPU economics.

**Capacity edge.** AWS deployed nearly 2 GW of new datacenter capacity in Indiana and Mississippi through power contracts with Talen and Vistra, enabling demand fulfillment that peers cannot match. Azure took a year-long construction pause and concentrates capacity toward OpenAI, reducing availability for other customers. Microsoft's AI business mix remains 80%+ IaaS-focused, a lower-margin posture.

**Google Cloud accounting concern.** Google Cloud reported record margins with 60%+ revenue growth, but the authors argue that DeepMind training costs ($5.4B in Q1 2026, up from $3.0B year-over-year) are classified under "Alphabet-Level Activities" rather than GCP, effectively presenting what they call "EBTIT" (earnings before training, interest, taxes) rather than true operating performance.
