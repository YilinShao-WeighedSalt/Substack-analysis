[[irrationalanalysis]] covers a wide-ranging set of topics in this March 15, 2025 memo, spanning macro uncertainty, mobile/RFFE headwinds, several tactical shorts, and a detailed take on Intel's restructuring under new CEO Lip-Bu Tan.

**Macro.** The author acknowledges his macro limits but notes that the Trump administration's chaotic tariff policy is injecting dangerous uncertainty into the economy. The stated goals — reducing the 10-year Treasury yield and cutting government spending via DOGE — are partially sympathized with, but the erratic, hourly-changing trade policy is viewed as deeply damaging. A recession scenario is presented as one mechanism to force Treasury yields lower.

**Mobile / Teradyne / GloFo / Tower.** TER held an investor day that was badly received. The author flagged the divergence between GFS (sold off hard alongside TER) and TSEM (unaffected), interpreting Teradyne's stumble as a possible structural signal for the mobile/smartphone SoC market. Teradyne is characterized as the inferior member of the ATE duopoly, with poor engineering community reputation. GloFo is seen as more exposed to mobile RFFE than Tower.

**Tactical Shorts.** CRDO is shorted on patent-troll optics — the company sued copper cable competitors on the same day Amazon announced warrant agreements with AAOI and FN, signaling defensive panic given Amazon's ~86% share of Credo revenue. ANET is called a "sheet-metal shitco in disguise" — its gross margin premium rests on Broadcom switching silicon and a proprietary software stack, both being eroded by Meta going direct to white-box hardware and hiring internal software teams; additionally Cisco (CSCO) is on the verge of a silicon comeback and Nvidia is aggressively bundling Spectrum-X. MRVL is shorted on Trainium 2 yield (~25% backplane yield) and stability failures, with Amazon reportedly shifting volume to Trainium 3 via Alchip; the only bull case left is Microsoft Maia 2 ramping in H2 2026. ALAB is shorted ahead of GTC, where the author expects new Nvidia NICs with integrated 48-lane PCIe switches to design out Astera's Scorpio PCIe switch narrative; valuation viewed as insane.

**Intel.** The author is long INTC. LBT rejoining as CEO after resigning from the board is seen as the right move. Key prescriptions: fire all Director-and-above management in Advanced Packaging, cut the entire dGPU division (Falcon Shores, gaming, datacenter AI GPU), slash CapEx, and put 18A on a 12-18 month remediation clock. The author is blunt that 18A PDK has real problems today but believes 18A-P (fixed version) can ultimately beat TSMC N3P on backside power delivery and potentially undercut TSMC N2 on wafer price. Honest public communication on 18A status is framed as essential for rebuilding IFS ecosystem trust.
