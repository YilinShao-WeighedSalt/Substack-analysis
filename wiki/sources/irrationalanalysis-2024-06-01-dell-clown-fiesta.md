[[irrationalanalysis]] dissects the buyside-driven mania that pumped DELL into earnings, the resulting collapse when results disappointed, and shares updated views on Marvell and Micron/HBM.

**Dell — The Clown Fiesta**

In mid-May, rumors circulated that Dell was receiving favorable Nvidia GPU allocations and that enterprise customers were migrating away from Supermicro. This sparked a groupthink frenzy across hedge funds, pods, and retail investors, sending DELL to elevated levels before Q1 FY25 earnings. The author initially identified a credible thesis: Dell could upsell AI server customers on high-margin NVMe storage arrays (estimated 70–75% incremental gross margins vs. street at ~60%), given enterprise reliability preferences. However, when Dell actually reported, AI-optimized servers came in with effectively zero operating margin — confirmed in the earnings call by Bernstein analyst Toni Sacconaghi, who noted that ISG operating income was flat despite $1.7B in new AI server revenue. The CFO's response was described as "word salad." The entire setup was an exercise in groupthink and FOMO rather than fundamentals; Dell lacks the engineering moat and gross margin profile to be a quality long.

**Marvell**

Datacenter is progressing but all other segments have imploded. The author sees Marvell's only viable product lines as Amazon's custom AI ASICs (Trainium/Inferentia) and DSP/optics re-timers. Broadcom is encroaching on re-timers. On ASICs, while Amazon's internal culture will drive irrational capex spending on Trainium/Inferentia, the author believes these chips will ultimately be crushed by Nvidia Blackwell and Rubin. Management's generous stock grants incentivize sandbagging Amazon's "capital bonfire" opportunity. Author holds a small call option position purely as speculation and views MRVL as a terrible long-term investment.

**Micron / HBM**

Samsung has failed Nvidia's HBM3E qualification testing due to heat and power consumption issues, and Samsung reassigned the head of its DS division. With the largest triopoly member effectively sidelined for 12–18 months, SK Hynix and Micron are rapidly raising prices. HBM's area intensity is cannibalizing non-HBM capacity across all three players — equivalent to simultaneous destruction of ~30% of total DRAM capacity. This creates an exceptionally violent upcycle. Author normally avoids memory due to chaebol dynamics, poor product visibility, and brutal downturns, but views this cycle as categorically different. Holds a modest Micron call option position and is opportunistically adding.
