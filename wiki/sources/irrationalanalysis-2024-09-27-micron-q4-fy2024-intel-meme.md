[[irrationalanalysis]] covers Micron's Q4 FY2024 earnings beat and a second meme buyout offer for Intel, this time from ARM.

## Micron Q4 FY2024 Earnings

Micron smashed sell-side expectations. The author attributes the beat to historically incorrect framing — Samsung has long dominated DRAM revenue, but this cycle is different because of HBM (High Bandwidth Memory). HBM consumes 3x the wafer intensity of regular DRAM, commands far higher gross margins, and has insatiable demand from AI accelerators.

Samsung has repeatedly failed to produce HBM3E at required quality metrics, ceding share to SK Hynix (the HBM leader with over 80% share) and Micron. Micron's HBM3E is claimed to have 20% lower power consumption than Samsung's equivalent — a hard competitive advantage since thermal limits are fixed and lower memory power frees budget for logic dies to clock higher. HBM yields are good, and all supply is pre-contracted at agreed prices. HBM TAM for 2025 was revised above $25B (from $20B), driven by both yield improvements and AI accelerator TAM expansion.

Additional highlights: traditional server (CPU) revenue is growing as socket consolidation begins; datacenter SSD is strong and Micron may be gaining share from Samsung despite using third-party controllers from Phison and Silicon Motion; smartphone and PC/laptop end markets are stable; auto declined QoQ.

The author liquidated a non-trivial MU options position at a profit, rotating proceeds into KEYS and FN. Despite remaining broadly bullish on MU and SK Hynix, the author finds memory investing too stressful to hold. He frames the cycle: Micron and SK Hynix are "partying at Samsung's expense" and this should continue for at least another year. Equipment plays CAMT and ONTO are mentioned as beneficiaries of the special processing required for HBM production.

## Intel Meme Buyout Offers

Following the author's prior post arguing Qualcomm's buyout offer for Intel was a joke, ARM management has now also leaked acquisition interest to the press. The author characterizes both offers as public trolling rather than genuine bids. At the time of writing, roughly 20% of Intel's market cap derives from these meme buyout offers — public trolling literally accounts for one-fifth of INTC's valuation.
