Written in response to a Fabricated Knowledge post by Doug O'Laughlin and Zero Gravitas on CEVA's design-win opportunity from Apple's internal modem program, this note adds engineering context to the financial thesis. [[irrationalanalysis]] holds CEVA shares and discloses plans to buy more.

**Apple C1 Modem Background.** Apple's internal cellular modem has been in development since 2019 — a six-year, multi-billion-dollar program staffed by over 6,000 engineers drawing from Apple, ex-Intel, ex-Qualcomm, and ex-Broadcom talent. The program was described as the worst engineering failure in semiconductors in 20 years, worse than AMD Bulldozer. Apple originally settled with Qualcomm in 2019 (modem supply through 2025, optional extension to March 2027) after Intel's 5G modem was too poor for carrier approval. The C1 modem is now shipping in the iPhone 16e — globally, but without mmWave support.

**5G Complexity and Carrier Gatekeeping.** A global modem must support every carrier's frequency band combinations and carrier-aggregation (CC) configurations across every regional network vendor (Ericsson, Nokia, Huawei, ZTE, Samsung Infra). This is why MediaTek cannot supply Apple for iPhone despite having a capable modem. Carriers actively gatekeep device access to networks and can restrict band combinations and grant priority. The author believes carriers have limited the CC configs allowed for the iPhone 16e. mmWave is required for ~20-30% of the market (primarily USA), meaning full modem self-sufficiency requires mmWave support by 2027.

**Implications for CEVA.** CEVA earns a royalty estimated at 0.5%–1% per chip on an assumed ASP of ~$20 for 5G modems and ~$4 for Wi-Fi+Bluetooth modems. With 100% of 2025 iPhones already using Apple's internal Wi-Fi+Bluetooth modem, and cellular ramp scenarios of 25%/70%/100% (base case) for 2025/2026/2027 on ~220–250M annual iPhone units, the royalty stream could grow significantly. The street is seen as deeply underestimating the ramp pace.

**Implications for Qualcomm.** Apple will not pay Qualcomm modem supply or QTL royalties after March 2027 — or potentially earlier once modem self-sufficiency is achieved. Apple's strategy mirrors 2017–2019 (force renegotiation via litigation) but this time Apple will have complete modem independence and Qualcomm will have no leverage.

**Implications for Broadcom.** AVGO loses low-margin Wi-Fi baseband content but gains RFFE content. The author views this as a long-term win (H2 2026+) but a short-term headwind in H2 2025.
