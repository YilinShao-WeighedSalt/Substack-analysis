[[irrationalanalysis]] builds a comprehensive bear case on Qualcomm ([[QCOM]]), arguing that eight years of failed diversification have left the company with no credible alternative revenue base before a catastrophic EPS cliff arrives in April 2027, when Apple's modem licensing contract expires.

**The Apple licensing timeline.** The Apple-Qualcomm legal war (2017–2022) ended in a settlement giving Apple a licensing deal through April 2025, extended to April 2027. Apple exercised the extension because its C1 internal modem — launched in the iPhone 16e and iPhone Air — lacks mmWave support, a feature critical to Verizon's network stability. The author argues Verizon's ongoing C-band rollout (targeting 80–90% completion in 2025) is the primary gating factor: once complete, Apple can push 100% internal modem volume in the iPhone 18 (September 2026), eliminating Qualcomm chipset revenue from Apple entirely — well before the April 2027 licensing expiry. Three scenarios for licensing renewal are outlined, with a full non-renewal being the author's modal case.

**Seven diversification failures.** Cloud AI 100/Ultra: a 7nm LPDDR4x chip from 2019 relabeled over years with negligible verifiable revenue. Snapdragon X Elite: Qualcomm promised $1B in compute revenue for FY2019 in a 2018 SEC filing; the target has still not been achieved in FY2025. RFFE: over 88% of segment revenue was handset-exposed, making it leverage not diversification. 5G O-RAN: entering a saturated market with deteriorating economics (Marvell exited, Ericsson/Nokia struggling). XR/AR/VR: Meta moved premium sockets to MediaTek (2454.TW). Datacenter CPU: Centriq killed in 2018, Nuvia datacenter CPU killed in 2022, third attempt not expected to generate revenue until FY2028. The author is baffled by the slow timeline given Qualcomm's CPU architecture team and physical design capability. Automotive is acknowledged as the one genuine success — Qualcomm surpassed Nvidia in quarterly auto revenue — but carries structurally lower margins and high China concentration.

**The April 2027 cliff.** With Apple chipset revenue falling to zero in 2026 and licensing renewal uncertain, the author sees a severe EPS contraction coming in 17 months. Qualcomm's diversification revenue ratio has been flat at approximately 25% despite years of investment. Xiaomi's $6.9B chip investment plan and volume-based rebate deals suggest further handset revenue pressure even before Apple. The "Connected Intelligent Edge" strategy is characterized as a persistent attempt to force QTL patent monetization into markets that neither need nor want 5G modems.

**Positive read-throughs.** SiTime ([[SITM]]) and Ceva are flagged as "very positive" from full Apple modem transition. Qorvo ([[QRVO]]) is "moderately positive." The author is explicitly long SITM, long Tower Semi ([[TSEM]]), and long Broadcom ([[AVGO]]) — the latter two for unrelated reasons. A long position in Intel ([[INTC]]) is also disclosed. Verizon ([[VZ]]) is noted as a potential buy for idiosyncratic reasons unrelated to the QCOM thesis.
