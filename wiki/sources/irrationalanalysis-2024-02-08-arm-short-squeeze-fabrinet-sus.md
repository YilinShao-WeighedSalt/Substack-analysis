A Q1 CY24 earnings roundup from [[irrationalanalysis]] covering ARM's short squeeze, Fabrinet's suspicious guidance, Qualcomm's mixed results, and a bonus note on NYCB.

**ARM**: ARM reported earnings that triggered a massive after-hours short squeeze — up 40% at one point on outsized volume. The author had shorted ARM twice previously (first at $63.69 covered at $57.85; second at $71.25 covered at $68.98, 5% NAV each time) and counts himself lucky to have avoided the squeeze. Key earnings highlights: Armv9 royalty rates are "at least double" equivalent Armv8 rates, and v9 now contributes ~15% of royalty revenue (up from 10% the prior quarter), driven mainly by smartphone ramp. Five new Total Access (ATA) licenses were signed, including three upgrades from Flex Access — a positive surprise. The licensing beat was driven by AI demand pulling customers toward more powerful cores and NPU IP access. The author notes ARM is "obviously overvalued" at $92.30 but concedes management is candid, capable, and that Nvidia GH200 (Neoverse CSS) and Microsoft Cobalt ramps represent major royalty upside the market may be pricing in.

**Fabrinet**: Guidance was unexpectedly weak. Management repeatedly blamed the weakness on a 100G Intel transceiver business transfer and moderating AI program ramp — an excuse the author calls "bovine excrement" for being invoked three times without specifics. The evasiveness prompted him to liquidate his entire FN position the next day to take profit, and he is now evaluating COHR and MRVL as replacement optical exposure.

**Qualcomm**: CEO declined to detail the Samsung partnership extension scope. The author notes QCOM lost significant Galaxy S24 share to Exynos — retaining only North America for the base/plus models and global Ultra (lower volume). Automotive remains a bright spot (record revenues, 75 model launches in 2023) despite a sector-wide down-cycle. However, CFO commentary confirmed Q3 CY24 will be the seasonal trough and that smartphone market recovery has normalized, capping near-term upside.

**NYCB**: Brief mention — flagged solely for entertainment value; the earnings call featured pointed sell-side questioning of management. No investment stance expressed.
