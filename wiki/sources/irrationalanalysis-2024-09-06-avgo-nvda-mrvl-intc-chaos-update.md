Post-earnings reaction piece from [[irrationalanalysis]] covering 2Q2024 results and a special update on Intel's manufacturing meltdown. The author holds a large, leveraged long in AVGO, which sold off hard on the print.

**Broadcom (AVGO):** Results were disappointing — Google TPU orders came in below buy-side expectations. However, enterprise business has decisively bottomed, and the author remains conviction long on the super-chiplet platform, acknowledging being 2–3 quarters early. Broadband/DOCSIS collapse was already written off in the thesis. Near-term headwind attributed to datacenter infrastructure (cooling, power) causing a holding pattern, not fundamental deterioration.

**Nvidia (NVDA):** Blackwell delay fears proved overblown. The fix required changing upper metal layers to spread bump-out and improve CoWoS-L packaging yields — an issue already anticipated. Gross-margin dilution concerns also flagged as overblown; CFO clarified in buy-side callbacks that Q4 is the gross-margin trough. Author's price target: at least $150/share sometime in H2 2025, the level at which they plan to begin selling. NVDA remains the author's largest position; covered calls were sold pre-earnings as a hedge (they expired worthless).

**Marvell (MRVL):** Custom silicon ramp is underway but not fully materialized. oDSP and electrical DSP revenue described as going "parabolic." Ethernet switches dismissed as uncompetitive (only Amazon uses them); CXL viewed as irrelevant to the thesis. Telco DPU margins on 5nm seen as much improved. Author holds March 2025 $90 calls and views the Amazon ramp as the next catalyst — management deliberately sandbagged timing in the earnings call.

**Intel (INTC):** Explosive special section. Lip-Bu Tan's resignation characterized as a protest resignation — "really bad." Separate Reuters report revealed Broadcom evaluated Intel 18A test wafers and found unacceptable yields. The author explains that typical D0 targets are ~0.1 for HVM, with 0.35–0.5 acceptable 3–4 quarters before ramp; Intel's claim of <0.4 D0 on 18A almost certainly applies only to internal products, not third-party customers. Intel's concurrent cancellation of 20A (moving chiplets to TSMC N3) is read as a negative signal, not a positive one — 18A is a derivative of 20A, so canceling 20A while claiming 18A is "so good" is logically inconsistent. Author's verdict: 18A is not ready for external customers, engineers are being surged to fix it, and INTC equity is a "do-not-touch" — "radioactive garbage," neither a long nor a short.

**Portfolio actions:** Actively buying FN, KEYS, ARM, TSM. Running a leveraged spread: long AVGO/TSM/ARM, short QCOM. AMD flagged as a potential short in the near future.
