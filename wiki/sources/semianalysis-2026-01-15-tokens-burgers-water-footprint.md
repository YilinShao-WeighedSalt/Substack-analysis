[[semianalysis]] (Nicolas Bontigui and Dylan Patel) argues that concern over AI datacenter water consumption is dramatically overstated when placed in proper economic and comparative context — using xAI's Colossus 2 facility in Memphis versus a single In-N-Out burger restaurant as the central case study.

## The Comparison

Colossus 2 consumes approximately 346 million gallons of water annually (roughly 0.9 million gallons per day), operating at a Water Use Efficiency (WUE) of 0.51 L/kWh across its 400 MW critical IT load (expandable to 1 GW+). The facility uses a hybrid cooling architecture combining ~130 dry coolers with ~135 adiabatic units, and its aeroderivative turbines consume zero water in power generation (no steam cycle). A breakdown of consumption: ~267 million gallons evaporated in cooling, ~66 million gallons in tower blowdown/flush cycles, and ~64 million gallons amortized for chip manufacturing over five years.

A single In-N-Out store, by contrast, consumes approximately 147 million gallons per year — driven almost entirely (~95%) by beef patties, which embed roughly 245 gallons (927 liters) per Double-Double equivalent. At ~600,000 burgers sold annually per location, the authors calculate lower figures than public estimates, attributing the discrepancy to methodology: notably the exclusion of "green water" (rainfall absorbed by soil) in favor of "blue water" (surface/groundwater) metrics.

Colossus 2 consumes roughly 2.5× the water of one In-N-Out location. The authors conclude: "people clamoring for slowing down datacenters because of water consumption are looking at the wrong problem."

## Token-to-Water Economics

Colossus 2's projected annual output of 3.9 quadrillion tokens translates to 8.9 million tokens per gallon, or 2.7 billion output tokens per burger. One burger's water footprint is equivalent to running Grok ~30 times per day for 668 years.

## Methodological Critique

The authors highlight several factors that undermine water consumption comparisons across datacenters and studies: cooling architecture selection, power source type, geographic climate, local water scarcity conditions, and the lack of standardized accounting methodology. They advocate for embedding water consumption within a broader economic value framework rather than treating it as an isolated metric.

## Net-Zero Water Potential

xAI is constructing a water recycling facility at Colossus 2 that draws reclaimed municipal wastewater from the T.E. Maxson treatment plant. If fully operational, this infrastructure could make Colossus 2 a net-zero water datacenter — treating and returning more water than it withdraws from freshwater sources.
