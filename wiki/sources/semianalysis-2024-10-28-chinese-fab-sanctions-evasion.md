[[semianalysis]] documents how Chinese semiconductor firms are systematically exploiting loopholes in U.S. export controls, and argues the controls have not materially harmed Western wafer fabrication equipment (WFE) suppliers.

**Evasion mechanisms.** The article identifies four distinct schemes. First, SMIC connects an entity-listed advanced-logic fab to a nominally separate "legacy" fab via automated overhead track (a "wafer bridge"), allowing restricted equipment imported for mature processes to physically reach restricted adjacent operations. Second, Huawei has built a web of subsidiaries — including Pengjin High-Tech and the PXW advanced logic fab — that import equipment legally while maintaining implausible claims of non-sharing. Third, Western tool makers (the article specifically cites Lam Research's Malaysia facility) manufacture outside the U.S., enabling sales that avoid the "U.S.-persons" trigger. Fourth, CXMT reclassified its 18nm DRAM as "19nm" by changing half-pitch measurement methodology alone, pushing it above control thresholds without any actual process change.

**Scale of China's fab buildout.** Huawei's state-backed fab network received roughly $30 billion in government funding in the prior year. China's 2024 wafer fab equipment spend reached $7.3 billion — fourth largest globally and up 27% year-over-year. Combined with SMIC and CXMT, China would rank as the world's second largest WFE customer. The network spans materials, equipment, optics, specialty and advanced logic fabs, and memory.

**WFE supplier performance.** Despite equipment-industry lobbying claims of an impending "death spiral," WFE suppliers outperformed the SOX index during the control period, expanded gross margins, and increased R&D. Applied Materials' CEO acknowledged that restrictions blocked "more than 10% of the China market," yet the firm still grew faster than the overall WFE market for a fifth consecutive year. The authors view this as evidence that controls can be tightened further without causing undue harm to U.S. suppliers.

**Technology gap.** China's advanced logic capability (SMIC N+2) lags TSMC N5/N7 by roughly five years. Huawei affiliate Swaysure is developing capacitorless 3D DRAM aimed at the memory-wall bottleneck in AI hardware.

**Policy recommendations.** The authors call for a 0% U.S. content threshold to force allied compliance, monthly entity-list updates with geographic verification, upstream component restrictions on EUV optics and plasma generators, and a presumption that all Chinese fab equipment imports serve restricted purposes until proven otherwise.
