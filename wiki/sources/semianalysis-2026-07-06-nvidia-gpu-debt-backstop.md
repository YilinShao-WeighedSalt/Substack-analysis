# Nvidia GPU Debt Backstop Unleashes the AI Project Trinity

SemiAnalysis (Daniel Nishball, Cheang Kang Wen, Zane Fong + 5 others), paid, ~4,600 words.

The financing of the AI buildout is now the binding constraint (after datacenter space in 2025 and chip supply in early 2026), and [[NVDA]] has stepped in to guarantee ("backstop") its customers' GPU-rental revenue so lenders will fund them. SemiAnalysis frames Nvidia as the **"Central Bank of AI"** (a term they introduced in Jan 2026) — supplying liquidity when normal lenders won't.

## Key facts & numbers
- **AI debt** projected to reach **~$7.1T outstanding by 2029** (from hundreds of billions in 2024–25) — 2nd-largest US asset-backed market after mortgages (~$13T). Cumulative AI capex 2024–2029 ≈ **$11.1T**; annual capex north of **$2T by 2028**.
- Funding shift: buildout was hyperscaler cashflow-funded; now Oracle → Meta → Google are turning to debt.
- **The "AI Project Trinity"** (all three needed, each requires the others): **Capital** (lenders want an IG offtake/backstop first) · **Offtake** (need equity for deposits to win a customer) · **Datacenter** (landlords want offtake+lending lined up).
- Old bankable template = only a **5-year take-or-pay** backed by an investment-grade hyperscaler. Problem: hyperscaler balance sheets are finite, and it excludes short-tenor renters (AI startups, inference providers who won't sign >1y).
- Seller's market: some neoclouds demand up to **100% prepay** on 1y rentals → theoretically infinite IRR (no cash out of pocket).

## The Nvidia backstop mechanics
- Nvidia gives a **take-or-pay minimum-revenue guarantee**; in return takes a **revenue share above the floor**. Neither side expects it triggered. Nvidia's **AA/Aa2 rating** is what makes it bankable.
- Typical term **6 years**; illustrative floor avg **~$2.36/hr/GPU** (year-1 $3.68). Worked example: GB300 rented at $6.75/hr yr1; on the $3.07 excess over floor, Nvidia takes $1.23 / neocloud keeps $1.84 → neocloud realizes $5.52; ~18% avg Nvidia take rate over 6y.
- Return profiles: 1y-rental book **~25.4% IRR with backstop** vs **~40.7% without**; if backstop is *triggered* (renting to Nvidia at floor) IRR ≈ 0 or slightly negative — but still covers debt, which is why it's financeable.
- **DSCR** ≥ **1.3x** (evaluated in the backstop-triggered scenario) → **70–80% LTV**.
- Pricing anchor: CoreWeave 5y **unsecured bonds ~10%** vs its Meta-backstopped **DDTL 4.0 ($8.5B) fixed tranche at 5.9%** (~90bps over Meta's ~5.0%). Nvidia-backstopped deals expected between SOFR+225bps (~5.9%) and ~10%.

## Announced deals (all APAC so far)
- **SharonAI** — 72MW AU AI factory, up to 40,000 GB300 under 6y backstop; disclosed backstop value **$4.88B** (~$2.33/hr/GPU floor). Growing to 132MW / 55,000+ GPUs by mid-2027.
- **Firmus** — 360MW Batam, Indonesia (DayOne/KITP); projects **$25–30B customer revenue over 6y**; prior 18k-GB300 Melbourne cluster financed via $10B Blackstone-led facility + Coatue; 600MW Gunvor energy deal.
- **[[AMD]]** has done backstops since 2025 (rent-back to AWS, OCI, DigitalOcean, Vultr, Tensorwave, Crusoe).
