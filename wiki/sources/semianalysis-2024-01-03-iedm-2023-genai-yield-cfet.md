[[semianalysis]] covers the major semiconductor process and memory technology announcements from IEDM 2023, with implications for equipment suppliers, memory makers, and logic foundries competing at advanced nodes.

**GenAI for Yield (Intel).** Intel demonstrated diffusion models outperforming GANs for predicting device variation and electrical characteristics from SPICE parameters. By better replicating distribution tails, the approach promises reduced mask respins and shorter development cycles — a potential lever on wafer yield economics.

**2D Materials & CFET.** TSMC and Intel both showed working nanosheet FETs using transition metal dichalcogenides (MoS₂ for NMOS, WSe₂ for PMOS), though still at lab-scale pitch metrics. Complementary FET (CFET) architectures — stacking one NMOS and one PMOS layer — are expected from all major logic players around 2030, bringing power delivery and thermal integration challenges.

**Thermal and Backside Power Delivery.** AMD quantified up to 5% performance degradation from wafer thinning required by backside power delivery. TSMC proposed thermally conductive interlayer materials (AlN and diamond) as mitigations.

**Applied Materials — Interconnect Share Gains.** Applied Materials presented all-tungsten interconnect schemes now in high-volume manufacturing. In-situ vacuum processing cuts resistance >20% versus ex-situ methods. The platform's multi-tool bundling positions AMAT for share gains at 2nm and beyond.

**SK Hynix HBM4 — MR-MUF Advantage.** SK Hynix detailed Mass Reflow-Molded Underfill (MR-MUF) versus TC-NCF (Thermal Compression Non-Conductive Film): MR-MUF offers higher throughput as a batch process and superior thermal performance via epoxy molding compounds with better heat conductivity, supporting HBM4 roadmap execution.

**Micron 3D DRAM & FeRAM.** Micron indicated 3D DRAM adoption likely within 10 years (2030–2035). The transition implies ~50% less lithography demand but increased etch and deposition tool requirements — a headwind for ASML, tailwind for etch/dep vendors. Micron also unveiled competitive ferroelectric RAM (FeRAM) approaching DRAM speed and density, an alternative scaling pathway.

**CXMT Export Control Violation.** Chinese DRAM maker CXMT presented Gate-All-Around vertical transistors at 18nm half-pitch at IEDM — simultaneously violating two U.S. export restrictions (18nm DRAM limits and GAA transistor prohibitions). This triggered potential restrictions on American tool shipments from Applied Materials, Lam Research, KLA, and Onto Innovation to CXMT, which had estimated $7B in 2024 equipment spending planned.
