[[irrationalanalysis]] writes a rapid-fire post-earnings reaction to Intel's Q2 2025 results, framing the company's own 10-Q (page 28) as a "suicide note" — an extraordinary SEC disclosure that officially acknowledges Intel may not be able to survive on products alone given accelerating share losses to AMD and ARM. INTC dropped 9% on the day while ARM and AMD rose ~2.5%.

The author argues Intel's book value is overstated because fab equipment is already being written down on an accelerated basis due to demand shortfalls, and further write-offs are likely — suggesting the stock should trade below 1x book. On the IFS (Intel Foundry Services) timeline, he states that even if the 14A 1.0 PDK ships by year-end, external foundry customers need 12–18 months of engineering work before they reach high-volume production readiness. Publicly threatening to cancel 14A is a "queen sacrifice" gambit by Lip-Bu Tan that risks ecosystem paralysis: nobody will finish porting IP from TSMC N3 to Intel 18A under this continuity uncertainty.

Additional Intel failures cited: chiplets were implemented in a margin-destructive way that boosted demand for older products; the IFS advanced packaging group botched engagements with two hyperscalers through self-inflicted design-rule deficiencies; and Intel's removal of Hyperthreading (its hacky SMT implementation) is framed as an inevitable consequence of Spectre-class vulnerabilities — but the author thinks Lip-Bu Tan's push to re-add SMT will backfire.

On AMD, the author is holding AMD call options for a "GPU meme trade" but now sees CPU revenue and gross margin expansion as a meaningful additional tailwind, potentially offsetting margin dilution from MI350 fire-sale pricing. Plans to hold through AMD's earnings in early August.

On Aehr Test Systems (AEHR), the author notes the Sonoma order was for packaged AI ASIC burn-in, not the wafer-level burn-in of AI logic designs that anchored his original thesis. The core thesis has not yet played out but he is still holding all shares.
