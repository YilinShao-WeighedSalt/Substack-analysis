[[irrationalanalysis]] publishes a year-end review of portfolio performance across three self-managed accounts, cataloguing every major mistake made in 2024 and articulating lessons for 2025.

The author operates three accounts: a Webull leveraged-trading account (largest, benchmark SMH), an E*TRADE long-only account holding most NVDA shares, and a Schwab long-only diversified account. The Webull account used no leverage in H1 2024, then averaged 2.2x (peak 2.7x) in H2, sustaining multiple margin calls primarily from the failed ALAB short. Despite this, the Schwab account delivered a surprise standout result, partly driven by a GEV tip from a friend.

The author discloses all active positions with average prices for holdings above 1% of NAV, and has already withdrawn funds to pre-pay capital gains taxes.

The bulk of the post is a candid forensic accounting of eight distinct mistakes: (1) selling AVGO call options (180c 12/20/24) too early after being up ~150%, missing $50–80K of additional profit; (2) stubbornly holding a short in low-float ALAB past rational risk management, vaporizing buying power across the account; (3) writing covered calls on NVDA and AVGO repeatedly at a net realized loss of $15–20K; (4) failing to sell CAMT during its bubble run despite recognizing it was overbought; (5) not covering a QCOM short leg at profit after using it to hedge a leveraged AVGO long; (6) a buy-write failure on INTC; (7) an epic buy-write failure on TSLA executed just before a vacation; and (8) buying high and selling low on VRT multiple times. A ninth error involved purchasing ARM call options ahead of a trial expecting ARM to win royalty disputes — ARM lost, and even a hedge via QCOM calls produced no gain because QCOM stock failed to move on its total legal victory.

Consolidated lessons: avoid shorting equities with less than 2x margin availability, cap such bets at $20–50K, never write covered calls, be less stubborn about trimming overbought longs (CAMT, COHR cited), and avoid selling an entire profitable options position in case of tail events.

The 2025 goal is to beat SMH by at least 30% in the leveraged account, with a stretch goal of 200% outperformance.
