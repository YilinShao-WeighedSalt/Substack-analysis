A quick reaction note from [[irrationalanalysis]] covering AMD's "Advancing AI 2024" event and the Astera Labs PCIe Gen6 switch announcement on the same day.

**AMD — Turin and MI325X**

Turin's server consolidation ratio of 7:1 is characterized as optimistic; a 5:1 ratio is considered more realistic. The MI325X is framed as a minor refresh of the MI300X: the logic dies move to a derivative TSMC node for sub-7% PPA gains, HBM3E replaces HBM3, but only 256 GB ships (versus the anticipated 288 GB) because of poor Samsung yield. The author pegs MI325X availability as Q4 2025 rather than AMD's stated H2 2025, placing AMD roughly 0.5–1 generation behind Nvidia. AMD's competitive comparisons against the H100 are criticized as misleading given that H200 is the current market standard and GB200 arrives within six months. On the CPU side, non-uniform latency and driver problems leave AMD in last place for AI head-node CPU sockets; Intel holds a short-term edge but ARM/Nvidia/Grace is expected to take substantial share from both. Pensando, acquired for $1.9B in 2022, is called out as having gone nowhere. The overall verdict is that buy-side was expecting more; AMD is "decisively #3" in AI compute. Sell-side is modeling $7–8B in AMD DC GPU revenue for 2025; the author believes AMD will be lucky to reach $4–5B in H1 2025 before Blackwell supply crushes demand. AMD is not being shorted yet but the author is "getting close."

**Broadcom competitive ranking**

Broadcom is ranked #2 in AI compute and may outgrow Nvidia in 2026, widening the gap between itself and AMD.

**Astera Labs — PCIe Gen6 Switch**

ALAB gapped up sharply on the PCIe Gen6 switch launch announcement, which the author argues was universally anticipated — the only logical response to re-timer content shrink on GB200. Broadcom has dominated the PCIe switch market since acquiring PLX, with far superior SerDes IP, package design, firmware, and interoperability testing. The author doubts ALAB will secure any customer beyond Amazon, whose warrants/options vest off purchase volume, creating a structurally distorted incentive. PCIe for GPU-to-GPU scale-up is dismissed as "infuriatingly stupid." ALAB is compared to RIVN as a finance-driven narrative. A small short position is opened at $65/share with intent to add on further rallies.
