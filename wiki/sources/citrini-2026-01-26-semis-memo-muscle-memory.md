[[citrini]] launches its inaugural "Semis Memo" series, arguing that the AI trade has matured past the point where GPU-level understanding is sufficient to capture the most asymmetric opportunities. With expected returns moderating on "mainstream layups" like NVDA, MU, SK Hynix, and AVGO, the firm introduces two new semiconductor analysts — Zephyr and Jukan — to provide supply-chain depth that translates technical nuance into actionable thematic upside.

The central thesis is that the biggest asymmetric winners of the AI infrastructure buildout now lie in second- and third-order supply chain names, not in the primary GPU/memory players. The post is framed as a roadmap for generalist investors to keep pace with an increasingly complex semiconductor landscape.

**Memory Supercycle.** Citrini points to HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) supply constraints as the structural driver behind the memory trade, noting that their "Post-Traumatic Supply Disorder" idea from the 26 Trades for 2026 watchlist had already flagged memory, storage, semicap, and advanced packaging as performing well over the prior month. The argument is that understanding HBM constraints — and the knock-on effects on DRAM supply — was necessary to underwrite the Q1 2024 outperformance call on SK Hynix and Micron when they were depressed by the post-COVID RAM glut. Second-order memory winners (e.g., memory testing via Teradyne) are flagged as the next layer to explore.

**Semicap Subsystems.** Equipment suppliers and their subsystem vendors are identified as an underappreciated layer of the AI capex buildout. The memo promises a deeper look at which semicap subsystem names benefit most from the expanding wafer-start and advanced packaging demand driven by AI.

**Silicon Photonics.** Optical interconnects are presented as a critical and still-emerging bottleneck in AI data center scaling. The post references prior coverage of optical interconnect names and signals continued focus on co-packaged optics and silicon photonics as a thematic area.

**Watchlist additions.** The memo also flags SpaceX supply chain beneficiaries and names arising from the AI-driven SaaS selloff as early-stage ideas worth monitoring, though specific tickers in these categories sit behind the paywall.

Prior validated calls cited to establish track record: TER (robotics/memory testing), AMKR (advanced packaging/OSAT/chiplets), MU and SK Hynix (memory supercycle from early 2024).
