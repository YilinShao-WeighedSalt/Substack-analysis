A Halloween 2024 earnings roundup covering four semiconductors: Qorvo, Onto Innovation, AMD, and Intel. Published by [[irrationalanalysis]].

**Qorvo (QRVO):** Qorvo's guidance is described as disastrous, driven by a structural pincer squeeze. QCOM has 100% share in the Samsung Galaxy S25 RFFE, while Apple's internal 5G modem ramp will deliver RFFE content gains to AVGO — leaving Qorvo losing share on both Android and iOS simultaneously. The smartphone market is going bi-modal (flagship vs. cheap), permanently eroding the midrange where QRVO has its footing. The author explicitly says "do not buy the dip" and suggests QCOM call options as the better smartphone play.

**Onto Innovation (ONTO):** The author is long ONTO and wants to add opportunistically. The key bear case flagged: SK Hynix and Micron are both holding back HBM-related WFE orders because they fear Samsung may have finally fixed its HBM3E yield issues. Both memory players are playing a game of chicken with Samsung rather than investing ahead. This creates near-term headwinds for ONTO despite strong demand in logic advanced packaging (Nvidia Blackwell/2.5D). The author remains bullish on the stock but acknowledges MU and SK bulls need to re-underwrite their theses. Separately, the author is broadly bearish on semicap majors with heavy China revenue exposure (40%+), citing SemiAnalysis reporting on Chinese companies evading U.S. export controls.

**AMD:** The author is firmly bearish. AMD's stock is entirely driven by datacenter GPU (MI300X/325X) revenue and gross margin, and those numbers came in light relative to buyside expectations — consensus was expecting $8–9B for 2024 DC GPU; actual guidance is $5B. Gross margin disappointed by 200+ bps. AMD's competitive positioning is deteriorating as Nvidia Blackwell ramps; the MI300/325X is limited to single-node inference for a narrow subset of models. The author bought puts, doubled profits, and plans to re-enter AMD shorts in size. Sees a larger disaster coming next quarter as Blackwell ramp pressures AMD ASP and GM further.

**Intel (INTC):** The author had put positions expecting gross margins of 33–37% due to Lunar Lake ramp, but Intel surprised positively. Backing out the Intel 7 process node write-down, adjusted GM was ~41%, well above expectations. More significantly, Intel disclosed two new secret 18A foundry customers. The author is 90% confident one is IBM (currently on Samsung SF5, needs an exit ahead of SF2 failure) and assigns 30% confidence to Nvidia as the other, given 18A's backside power delivery advantages for HPC. The author is genuinely positive on Intel's 18A progress but notes Intel has poor advanced packaging traction due to an extremely non-standard packaging flow.
