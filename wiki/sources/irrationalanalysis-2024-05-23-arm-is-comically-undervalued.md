[[irrationalanalysis]] argues that ARM (NASDAQ: ARM) is the highest-conviction long-term investment idea following Nvidia's earnings report and after-hours spike. The post is an extension of a prior thesis on the GB200 NVL72 "Mainframe of Doom" — that the NVL36/72 integrated rack will dominate Blackwell-generation shipments.

The central catalyst is a credible rumor that Nvidia is re-doing the packaging for GB200 mid-generation, switching from CoWoS to a fan-out (InFo) derivative. The author argues this is an enormous engineering undertaking — requiring full redo of power integrity (PI) and signal integrity (SI) across PCB, substrate, RDL, and silicon interposer, plus three separate high-speed I/O buses (NVLink C2C, D2D, and 200G SerDes). Such effort would only make sense if Nvidia anticipates demand that CoWoS capacity cannot satisfy, which gives enormous credibility to leaked Grace CPU shipment numbers.

The Grace CPU is the key to the ARM thesis. The author builds a COGS model for Grace and highlights a quirky but important dynamic: ARM (LTD) actually benefits from bad Grace yields, because lower yields raise COGS, which forces higher ASPs at Nvidia's target gross margins — and ARM's royalty is a percentage of ASP. The model projects infrastructure royalty revenue for ARM "just from Grace," with base/bear/bull scenarios, noting that Grace is only one of many ARM-based CPU and DPU product lines ramping in datacenter. The conclusion is that Grace alone could "shatter sell-side expectations" for ARM.

The author states they plan to aggressively buy more ARM and may use margin to purchase LEAP calls, making this their highest conviction position. Nvidia is mentioned as the direct catalyst — its packaging speedrun is described as a "gigantic blaring signal" — though no explicit bullish or bearish stance is taken on NVDA itself beyond the context it provides for ARM.
