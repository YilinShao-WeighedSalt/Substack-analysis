[[irrationalanalysis]] opens the post by disclosing a fresh long position in KEYS purchased after-hours following the company's Q3 FY2024 earnings report, with intent to add more shares and possibly explore LEAP options.

**Business overview and moat.** Keysight's core business is electrical test equipment, divided into wireless and wireline within its "commercial communications" segment. On the wireless side, the Keysight UXM is described as the industry-standard call-box / fake basestation for cellular testing; competition from Anritsu and R&S is dismissed as inferior. Keysight is also acquiring Spirent to fill its channel-emulator gap, a move the author views as smart M&A. On the wireline side, Keysight oscilloscopes and BERTs (bit-error rate testers) are characterized as "industry leading by a massive margin" for 224G SerDes R&D, with competing products described as "outright unusable" — the key differentiator being best-in-class jitter (time-domain noise).

**Software and services flywheel.** The author highlights Keysight as a "sleeper SaaS business": add-on software licenses often exceed $100K/year per box and cannot be moved to third-party providers. On top of that, annual calibration is mandatory regardless of software purchases, creating guaranteed high-margin recurring revenue that scales with the installed base.

**Earnings read-through and inflection thesis.** The wireless segment has been the source of Keysight's multi-quarter pain; management signaled stabilization. Critically, wireline revenue has nearly surpassed wireless — a milestone the author had been waiting for. Cloud hyperscalers are meaningfully ramping wireline test spend, and the author expects a higher software attach-rate from hyperscaler customers than from traditional telecom/handset customers, due to large budgets and less experienced procurement teams. QCOM and others "geoshifting" volumes to Asia-Pacific is noted as a gross-margin headwind for the wireless segment going forward, reinforcing the view that wireless will flatten while wireline drives growth.

**Short thesis on ALAB.** The author recommends ALAB as the short leg of a pair trade. Astera Labs is characterized as "comically overvalued," with the entire valuation predicated on winning PCIe switch content in Nvidia GB200/GB200A platforms — a qualification the author views as unlikely. Re-timer revenue is already imploding with B100/B200 effectively canceled. AVGO's SerDes is noted as technically superior to Astera's licensed IP. The author cannot personally participate for ethical reasons but flags the pair as high-alpha.
