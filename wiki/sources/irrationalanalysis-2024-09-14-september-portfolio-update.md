[[irrationalanalysis]] publishes a full portfolio snapshot as of September 2024, disclosing every holding and providing brief investment rationale for each position. The post was prompted by an influx of new subscribers following a recommendation from Asianometry's Jon.

**Optics theme (FN, CIEN):** The author re-entered Fabrinet (FN) after previously selling on management concerns, framing it as a low-risk/medium-reward optics play driven by the SemiAnalysis multi-datacenter AI training thesis — massive GPU cluster buildouts require enormous optical interconnect capacity. Ciena (CIEN) is a higher-risk/higher-reward expression of the same thesis. Both positions are described as precursors to a forthcoming deep-dive on communication systems.

**Keysight (KEYS):** Rated a "screaming buy" with a cross-reference to a prior detailed write-up; no additional thesis added here.

**Long AVGO/TSM/ARM, Short QCOM spread:** The author's largest and most detailed trade. Broadcom is expected to gain incremental RFFE content with Apple's 2025 modem rollout, has significant upside from semi-custom AI ASICs, and a credible co-packaged optics ramp (Q4 2025 possible, H2 2026 high-confidence). TSMC benefits from Samsung foundry's catastrophic 3nm yield failures (sub-20%) and Intel Foundry's ongoing struggles. ARM is expected to win the Nuvia lawsuit and extract higher royalty rates from Qualcomm and others, while datacenter exposure is under-appreciated by sell-side. Qualcomm is the short leg: Apple modem share projected to fall 100%→60%→20%→0% in 2025–2027, AI PC guidance expected to disappoint at the Nov 19 Investor Day, and margin pressure from TSMC price hikes.

**AbbVie (ABBV):** Long based on durable pricing power from chronic-disease products, solid R&D pipeline, and attractive dividend/P/E.

**GE Vernova (GEV):** Long as a natural gas turbine and grid transformer play benefiting from AI-driven US energy grid buildout — not primarily a wind/clean-energy story.

**First Solar (FSLR):** Long on superior panel degradation economics for utility-scale deployments; acknowledged dependency on government policy as a key risk.

**Ideas not held — Intel (INTC):** Author assigns 30% probability of bankruptcy within 18–24 months but declines to short due to event-risk (e.g., Altera spinout could spike the stock 20%). Verdict: avoid, not short.

**AMD:** Genuinely uncertain long or short. Expects MI300/325X GPU sales to collapse once Blackwell ramps, but sees a plausible semi-custom win (possibly Microsoft Maia) given AMD's packaging expertise (Xilinx chiplets, 3D-IC) and existing MSFT relationship via Xbox SoCs. No position taken.

**Astera Labs (ALAB):** Short thesis maintained — PCIe retimers excluded from GB200 will cause a revenue implosion; current sell-side estimates described as hilariously uninformed. Author does not hold due to risk/ethical constraints but views it as comically overvalued.

**Verizon (VZ):** Speculative theory that the Frontier fiber acquisition was motivated by leasing fiber to hyperscalers for multi-datacenter AI training, given geographic overlap with AI data center clusters in Texas and the Midwest. Not a current position.
