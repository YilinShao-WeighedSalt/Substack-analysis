# Micron's Record Profits, Apple's CXMT Plea (podcast)

Austin Lyons + Vik Sekar on the memory supercycle hitting consumers. Best plain-language explainer of the crunch this cycle.

- **Micron**: ~$41.5B revenue in a single quarter (> any full year in its 50-yr history), **85% gross margin** (> Nvidia's); briefly worth more than Meta. GM path: 45→56→75→~85%, guiding ~85–86% next Q. Booked/prepaid through 2027 via take-or-pay LTAs.
- **Consumer pain**: Apple first-ever *mid-cycle* price hikes; synths, routers up; GoPro near penny-stock; low-end phones "de-specced" (shrinkflation). Hosts: "AI is causing inflation."
- **Apple → CXMT**: Apple asked USG whether it can buy DRAM from CXMT (China's biggest DRAM maker, on the entity list — supplies Chinese military). Unprecedented for a US co to request buying from a blacklisted firm.
- **Geopolitics**: Korean govt committing **>$500B** to expand SK Hynix + Samsung (Korea ~2/3 of world DRAM). **SK Hynix passed Samsung** as Korea's most valuable company (first in 25 yrs). **Kioxia passed Toyota** as Japan's most valuable.
- **Technical why**: HBM stacking needs TSVs → can't place cells near drilled holes → **1GB HBM burns ~3× wafers vs 1GB DRAM**. Plus agentic AI needs DRAM for CPUs + KV cache (working memory). Data-center demand inelastic; consumer demand elastic and getting crushed. CXL-pooled DRAM/NAND for KV cache.
- **Cycle debate**: hosts concede the memory "merry-go-round" (boom-bust) still exists, but many new demand drivers keep it in the favorable quadrant far longer; supply relief not until 2027–2029. Michael Burry quoted calling govt-level memory investment "the beginning of the end."
- **AI demand macro**: frontier training facing political/competitive headwinds (US withholding leading models — "Fable"/"Mythos"/"Soul" cut off), but inference/agentic demand enormous; cost-optimization via cheaper models (GLM 5.2, DeepSeek V4) + model routing becoming standard.
