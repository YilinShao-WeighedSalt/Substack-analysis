[[semianalysis]] argues that Meta has undergone a fundamental strategic transformation from cautious AI incrementalism to an all-out superintelligence push, driven by Mark Zuckerberg assuming personal control of the AI program after losing open-source model leadership to DeepSeek in the wake of Llama 3's success.

**Leadership and talent.** Zuckerberg's decisive response was to recruit at unprecedented compensation levels — typical offers of $200–300M over four years, roughly 100x peer compensation — pulling in Nat Friedman (former GitHub CEO), Daniel Gross (SSI co-founder), and Alex Wang (Scale AI CEO). Meta acquired a 49% stake in Scale AI at approximately a $30B valuation, bringing with it Scale's SEAL reasoning-evaluation lab. Attempts to acquire Thinking Machines and SSI were turned down. Meta's 2 billion daily active users are positioned as a structural recruiting and data-flywheel advantage.

**Compute infrastructure.** Two flagship clusters anchor Meta's ambitions. The Prometheus cluster in Ohio targets ~1GW of AI training capacity using a "tent" datacenter design optimized for construction speed over traditional architecture; on-site power comes from two 200MW natural gas plants (built by Williams) using Solar Turbines Titan 250s, Siemens Energy SGT400s, and Caterpillar 3520 reciprocating engines. Backend networking runs on Arista 7808 switches equipped with Broadcom Jericho and Ramon ASICs. The Hyperion cluster in Louisiana, which broke ground at end of 2024, is planned to reach 1.5GW+ by end of 2027 — positioning it as the world's largest single campus. Multi-datacenter training across owned and leased sites is stitched together over ultra-high-bandwidth backend networks. Meta's $100B annual advertising cashflow enables this capex without external financing constraints.

**Llama 4 Behemoth failure.** The post provides a detailed post-mortem on why Llama 4 underperformed: (1) chunked attention created reasoning blind spots across context boundaries; (2) a mid-training switch from expert-choice to token-choice routing in the MoE architecture degraded performance; (3) the internal web crawler lacked proper deduplication and YouTube data was absent; (4) competing research directions without decisive leadership coordination stalled progress. The pivot to centralized leadership under Zuckerberg directly addresses this last failure mode.

**Competitive framing.** Despite Behemoth's setbacks, Meta projects its training FLOPs will approach OpenAI scale. The company is described as transitioning from "GPU-poor to GPU-filthy-rich on a per-researcher basis." Federal tax incentives under recent U.S. legislation accelerate the infrastructure spending timeline.
