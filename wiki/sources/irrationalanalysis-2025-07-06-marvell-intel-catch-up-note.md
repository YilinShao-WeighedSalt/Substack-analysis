A rapid catch-up note from [[irrationalanalysis]] covering two troubled semiconductor names: Marvell (MRVL) and Intel (INTC). The post is framed as a quick "shitpost" written on a one-hour timer, but delivers dense, technically grounded bearish arguments for both.

**Marvell — SerDes failures undermining the custom XPU franchise**

The author's prior bearish thesis on Marvell's optical 200G SerDes is now corroborated by third-party research notes: Edgewater Securities (June 18, 2025) reports that AWS's Trainium 2 Max program is on hold due to Marvell SerDes performance issues, and that AWS's Trainium 3 (with original designer Alchip) also hit SerDes failures requiring a re-spin, prompting AWS to engage Marvell — which then encountered its own SerDes problems. A second tape-out implies Trn3 mass production slipping to H2 2026. Separately, Semianalysis (June 24, 2025) notes that Microsoft is evaluating other silicon partners after being "disappointed" with Marvell's networking performance on the Maia program.

At Marvell's Custom AI Investor Event 2025, the author dismisses the "XPU attach" narrative as distraction from the Amazon and Microsoft program disasters. Google's next-gen ARM CPU project is reportedly moving to GUC. Amazon Trainium 4 is likely going to Alchip + Astera Labs. Microsoft Maia 200's failure is attributed ~90% to Microsoft's own digital logic timing closure errors and ~10% to Marvell. The author flatly denies that XAI is a Marvell XPU customer, pushing back on Citigroup speculation. On a technical level, Marvell's 3nm 224G SerDes demo at OFC is described as mediocre (4e-8 pre-FEC BER, errors at FEC symbol 9, under aggressive cooling). The post provides a practical guide for spotting cheesed SerDes demos, and notes Marvell's 0.3 pJ/bit efficiency is essentially tied with commodity off-the-shelf Synopsys/Cadence IP, while Broadcom MAX is better. The overall conclusion: Marvell's "full-service" margin profile has no justification if Alchip + Astera Labs can deliver comparable results at lower cost, and the real beneficiary is Broadcom.

**Intel — 18A/18AP effectively dead**

The author cites a Reuters report (July 2, 2025) that Intel's new CEO is exploring a major shift in the chip manufacturing business. Both 18A and 18AP are described as "toast": the process competes with TSMC N3P rather than N2 as bulls claimed, the time-to-market window versus TSMC N2 has closed, parametric yield on 18A is privately described by Lip-Bu Tan to buy-side analysts as "terrible," and the backside power delivery advantage is rendered moot by poor area efficiency from process variation margins.
