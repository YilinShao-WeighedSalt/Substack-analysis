[[citrini]] publishes a conversation with Dan O'Neil, VP of Investor Relations at Credo Semiconductor (CRDO), covering the state and trajectory of the silicon interconnect industry. The discussion spans the industry's move to 200G/lane technology, client sensitivity around power and cost, and Credo's competitive positioning at the time the stock was trading at all-time highs. CRDO had nearly tripled since Citrini first featured it in its inaugural client article on May 30, 2024, making it one of the top performers in the CitriniResearch Global AI Beneficiaries selections.

## Key Competitive Advantages

The central takeaway from the O'Neil conversation is Credo's ability to deliver power-efficient SerDes (Serializer/Deserializer) solutions that either undercut competitors on price or offer materially lower power consumption at equivalent performance. This is positioned as a critical differentiator as the AI infrastructure buildout moves into more power-constrained environments — specifically inference workloads and edge AI applications where thermal and energy budgets are tight.

## Industry Direction: 200G/Lane and Edge AI

Citrini highlights the industry's migration toward higher lane rates (200G/lane) as an ongoing structural tailwind. As hyperscalers and enterprises push for more throughput per port, Credo's focus on winning design sockets that will ship into production beyond the next 12 months is characterized as a sign of durable revenue visibility rather than near-term opportunism.

## Apple Intelligence as a Demand Signal

The piece draws a connection between Apple's "Apple Intelligence" edge AI initiative and the broader explosion in demand for faster, more efficient on-device interconnects. The argument is that as AI capabilities migrate from cloud to consumer devices, the need for high-speed, low-power interconnects — precisely Credo's specialty — will broaden well beyond hyperscaler data centers into a much larger total addressable market.

## Broader Context

The post references Citrini's earlier primer "Interconnects 101" (July 3, 2024) as background reading and links to highlights from the June 2024 anniversary post where CRDO was upgraded. The overall stance remains bullish on CRDO given its design-win pipeline, power efficiency leadership, and positioning across both cloud and edge AI deployment scenarios.
