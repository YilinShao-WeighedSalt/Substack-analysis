A 300+ slide Dell internal deck leaked publicly, prompting [[irrationalanalysis]] to revise a prior Qualcomm laptop-chip analysis. The author raises his base-case gross-margin estimate for QCOM's laptop SoC business to 52% and turns "incredibly bullish."

**PMIC Fiasco — Real but Not Fatal for QCOM**
Dell's internal BOM data confirms reporting by SemiAccurate: Qualcomm's proprietary PMIC requires a high-pitch HDI PCB (four extra board layers), adding ~$18 in motherboard-material cost and lifting the total "carcass" (motherboard + chassis) cost ~$100 above the prior Intel version. Qualcomm is paying a $60/unit subsidy to Dell to compensate OEMs — a subsidy larger than the PMIC cost itself, which will pressure QCT operating margins in the near term. Critically, Qualcomm blocked OEMs from substituting cheaper off-the-shelf PMICs, compounding the self-inflicted damage.

**Still Crushing Intel on BOM**
Despite the PMIC mess, Qualcomm's SoC is less than half the price of Intel's competing CPU. Dell saves money on BOM *before* the $60 subsidy is counted. Intel (Meteor Lake) shows battery life essentially flat with Alder Lake; Lunar Lake is projected at only ~20% improvement. The Dell leak shows Qualcomm Oryon delivering roughly double real-world battery life — an outcome the author views as the single most impactful consumer metric, portending significant PC market-share gains.

**Volume and Roadmap**
Dell modeled the QC-powered device with healthy unit volumes over an 18-month lifecycle (vs. 24 months for Intel predecessor), implying confidence in a V2 Oryon that fixes the PMIC issue. A V2 would eliminate the subsidy, raise ASPs above $180, and expand gross margins toward 60%. 5G was evaluated and dropped from the final product.

**Bull Case**
The author projects QCOM exceeding 10% laptop/PC market share by H2 2025. Operating profit is near-term impaired by the subsidy but the V2 product normalizes economics. The diversification-away-from-handsets narrative strengthens, supporting multiple expansion. Intel faces existential BOM and performance disadvantages.
