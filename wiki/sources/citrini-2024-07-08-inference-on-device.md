[[citrini]] argues that the next major phase of AI — "Phase 2" — will be defined by inference running directly on consumer devices, particularly the iPhone, with Apple uniquely positioned to lead due to its privacy-first architecture, vertical silicon integration, and walled-garden ecosystem. The post is structured as a "picks and shovels" basket build for the edge AI and on-device inference theme.

## Core Thesis: Apple and the Edge

The central argument is that on-device inference is not optional — it is the only architecturally coherent way to deliver deeply personal AI assistance without routing sensitive user data to a remote server. Citrini illustrates this with a real-world anecdote: an iPhone anticipating a destination from a text message, which captures exactly the kind of contextual intelligence that requires local inference. Apple's WWDC 2024 "Apple Intelligence" announcement — including Private Cloud Compute, on-device generative models, and a ChatGPT integration — is cited as confirmation the thesis is playing out. Citrini had recommended going long AAPL and short GOOGL in early June 2024; by the time of this post AAPL was up ~19% and GOOG up ~7.5% over five weeks, with the AI basket position up ~30%.

## Replacement Cycle Catalyst

The author contends that the iPhone 16 or 17 will drive the first truly massive replacement cycle in roughly a decade, because edge AI inference will render prior models materially less capable — not just incrementally slower. AI smartphones are forecast by IDC to surge over 200% YoY to 170 million units in 2024. The hardware requirement alone (DRAM scaling from 8–16 GB to 16 GB minimum, with higher estimates) forces upgrades, while on-device "model of models" architectures — cascading specialized sub-models trained on-the-fly — are expected to widen the capability gap with older hardware.

## Supply Chain Basket

Citrini builds a global basket of beneficiaries across Taiwan, China, Korea, Japan, and the US:

**Owned positions:** Foxconn/Hon Hai (2317.TW), Quanta Computer (2382.TW), Wistron (3231.TW), TSMC (TSM), MediaTek (2454.TW), SK Hynix (000660.KS), Renesas (6723.JP), Broadcom (AVGO), Micron (MU), Western Digital (WDC), Qualcomm (QCOM), Intel (INTC), Teradyne (TER), Amkor (AMKR), LuxShare.

**New or prospective longs:** ASE Technology (ASX) for advanced packaging; Catcher (2474.TW) as a casing/replacement-cycle play; WPG Holdings (3702.TW) and Simplo Technology (6121.TW) for distribution and batteries; Corning (GLW) as a replacement cycle beneficiary; Analog Devices (ADI) for power management (Maxim Integrated heritage); QRVO for RF; Samsung (005930.KS) for memory until the AI phone lands; Murata (6981.JP), TDK (6762.JP), and Taiyo Yuden (6976.JP) for RF components; Ambarella (AMBA) for edge inference silicon.

**Telcos** (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) get a marginal mention — Citrini would not exit them but does not expect outsized direct benefit.

## Risks

Key risks include iPhone 16 launch shipments disappointing relative to elevated expectations; the exclusion of Apple Intelligence from Chinese-market phones (~20% of AAPL revenue); and the possibility that rival platforms close the privacy/ecosystem gap. The basket diversification is explicitly framed as a hedge against AAPL-centric risk.
