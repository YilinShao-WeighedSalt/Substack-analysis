[[irrationalanalysis]] opens with a eulogy: Pat Gelsinger was fired on December 1, 2024, five days after what the author calls his "last supper" — a November 26 celebration of funding secured for his foundry turnaround plan. The author is unambiguously bearish on Intel's future without Gelsinger, calling the board's interim co-CEO structure proof it had no successor plan and no coherent strategy.

The core thesis is that Gelsinger was uniquely qualified — joining Intel at 18, trained on company benefits, already wealthy when he returned in 2021, and motivated by mission rather than money. His acknowledged flaws (too optimistic, slow to cut underperformers) are presented as minor relative to the board's recklessness in removing him. The author says Intel is now on a path toward bankruptcy, with a "chaotic consultant-driven bloodbath" of layoffs coming that will spare no one regardless of team or tenure.

Five possible outcomes are laid out, all grim:

1. **GloFo but more painful** — A fab spin-out echoing the AMD/GlobalFoundries split, which the author calls a decade-long near-death experience for both parties. Intel's position in 2025 is described as materially worse than AMD's was in 2009, making a repeat far more destructive.

2. **Seppuku for Shareholderu** — A fire sale of assets; the "patient" commits suicide and donates organs. The author cites WSJ reporting on Gelsinger's departure framing.

3. **Lip-Bu Tan Cleanse by Fire** — The author's preferred upside scenario. Lip-Bu Tan (former Cadence CEO, resigned from Intel's board in August 2024 over disagreements with Gelsinger) understands the foundry ecosystem and would do the slash-and-burn restructuring Gelsinger was too kind to execute.

4. **Hock Tan and Trump Bailout** — Broadcom's Hock Tan takes Intel Products, cuts headcount ~80%, milks it; Intel Foundry is spun out with government-backed debt. Author notes Tan is wrong for foundry but right for the product business.

5. **Samsung Foundry Shotgun Marriage** — Two failing foundries merge into what the author calls a "stupidity singularity." Presented as the darkest comedic scenario.

The post closes with a chart of INTC and urges Intel employees to leave immediately.
