[[irrationalanalysis]] publishes a sprawling educational-plus-investment guide explaining how all communication systems — electrical Ethernet, 5G, Wi-Fi, and long-haul optics — share the same underlying physics and DSP. The first two-thirds are a first-principles tutorial covering ADC/DAC non-linearity, Fourier transforms, the Nyquist-Shannon sampling theorem, the Shannon-Hartley capacity theorem, impulse/frequency response, noise sources, propagation media, forward error correction (FEC), modulation (NRZ, PAM4, QAM), phase noise, non-linearity, equalization (FFE/DFE/CTLE), statistical estimation (LMS, MLSD), PLLs/CDRs, MIMO, and beamforming. Three full walkthroughs tie the theory to real standards: 800 Gbps electrical Ethernet (IEEE 802.3ck), 5G NR (3GPP Release 15), and long-haul Open ZR+.

The investment section ranks communication-silicon and photonics companies across three tiers. **Core longs**: AVGO is the primary winner — author believes CPO switches ramp H2 2025/Q1 2026 at the latest, already enabling CPO TPUs for Google. MRVL is a near-term swing trade on the Amazon Trainium 2 ramp but a poor long-term hold as LPO/CPO erode its oDSP business. FN (Fabrinet) is liked long-term as a beneficiary of CPO volume and has Thailand manufacturing as a tariff hedge. CIEN is a pure-play on dark-fiber monetization driven by multi-datacenter AI training. KEYS is called a "screaming buy" — asymmetric risk/reward tied to AI ASIC R&D intensity, optical proliferation, and high-margin SaaS. SITM is a small position opportunistically added; key insight is Nvidia using SiTime on GB200 to enable programmable PPM cancellation, giving SiTime a datacentre opportunity beyond Apple. LUMN is described as debt-laden garbage but a degen trade on hyperscaler dark-fiber leases. TSEM and GFS are preferred photonics foundry plays over TSMC for specialty PIC process nodes.

**Secondary names**: COHR (Coherent) is flagged as potentially differentiated with phase-modulated laser specialisation and optical circuit switching, with the author noting intent to buy. MXL has inverted risk/reward. AAOI is "too hard" due to PRC exposure. MediaTek won a mini-TPU program at Google as a 224G SerDes player, possible trade. Niche mentions: CSCO is avoided (failed SaaS pivot, losing relevance in AI switching); ANET is avoided (no proprietary silicon, CPO is a content risk, white-box threat from CLS); ALAB remains a structural short thesis in the author's view despite prior losses on the short. CRDO is a speculative trade idea on LRO DSP with no customers yet.
