[[irrationalanalysis]] revisits two previously wrong calls — a short on Sandisk (then WDC) and a bearish stance on Marvell's 1.6T DSP — and updates the thesis on several sub-topics within each.

**Sandisk:** The author admits the early-2025 short call on Sandisk was badly wrong. The original bearishness stemmed from hatred of QLC NAND and skepticism toward Sandisk's "QLC scaling first" strategy. The key admission is that Sandisk's wafer-bonding architecture substantially mitigates the traditional weaknesses of QLC (poor random IOPS, ECC overhead, charge-level logic complexity), while AI video generation ("AI slop") is emerging as an enormous, underestimated NAND demand driver. A 256 TB QLC-only SSD with no SLC cache — maximizing capacity density at acceptable write endurance for AI video workloads — is now viewed as a genuine product winner. The author plans to buy Sandisk call options. High-Bandwidth Flash (HBF) remains dismissed as irrelevant.

**Marvell — 1.6T DSP:** A Barclays ECOC note raised concerns about Marvell's 1.6T oDSP underperforming Broadcom's by ~1W and limited OFC booth presence. The author pushes back: Marvell appears to have re-spun the chip (not merely a firmware fix) to resolve earlier BER/TDECQ regressions first noted at OFC, effectively closing the performance gap with AVGO at operational temperatures. Critically, Nvidia is using Marvell's 1.6T DSP for its large transceiver ramp — the author's prior assumption that Nvidia's ring architecture required an internal DSP was incorrect. A 1–2W power penalty at the DSP level (~7% of module power) is dismissed as non-decisive. oDSP is Marvell's highest-margin product line and the author remains bullish even if share shifts from 80/20 to 50/50 vs. Broadcom.

**Marvell — Alaska AEC:** Marvell's Alaska AEC re-timer (800G) has reportedly won a major Amazon socket, displacing Credo which had been charging premium prices. Broadcom's competing AEC chip is too power-hungry for this generation. The author frames "long MRVL, short CRDO" as an interesting pair trade but declines to execute given Marvell's near-term volatility from Trainium 3 rumor noise.

**Marvell — Meta mini-ASIC and D2D PHY:** Meta is reportedly evaluating Marvell and MediaTek for a small AI ASIC. The author assigns ~90% probability to Marvell winning, citing their 64G D2D PHY IP — demonstrated at Hot Chips 2025 with strong BER data even at high temperature and under heavy crosstalk — as a decisive differentiator.

**Marvell — Innovium switching:** The Innovium Ethernet switching business is characterized as struggling due to software ecosystem gaps and a late 51.2T product. Startup Upscale AI is identified as a potential software/ODM partner that could help Marvell become a credible third player in high-end Ethernet switching behind Broadcom and Nvidia.
