A post-earnings roundup covering Broadcom (AVGO), Marvell (MRVL), and Micron (MU) following their 4Q CY24 prints. The author describes making money on all three trades and frames the piece as a brief preview ahead of a more detailed January 1st portfolio and research update. Published amid Fed-driven market turbulence forcing deleveraging and capital gains tax pre-payment.

**Micron (MU):** The author characterizes Micron's quarter as a "faceplant," with Q2 revenue guidance of $7.7–8.1B missing the $8.99B consensus estimate and gross margin guidance of 37.5–39.5% missing the 41.3% estimate. The author dismisses the AI PC refresh narrative as "delusional hopium," argues that YMTC is destroying the NAND market and that Micron is actively losing NAND share to both YMTC and CXMT. CXMT is flagged as a present-day threat — not an 18–24 month risk — already ramping DDR5 and LPDDR5 at 80% yield, which undercuts any DRAM supply tightening that HBM wafer intensity might otherwise cause. The HBM3e ramp is noted as later than expected. Despite the bearish framing, the author hints at a "non-consensus" bull thesis to be published January 1st and is "browsing the options chain" — holding no position but signaling potential interest.

**Broadcom (AVGO):** Hock Tan delivered hyper-bullish commentary that caused the stock to spike. The author confirms the three existing hyperscale AI ASIC customers as Google, Meta, and ByteDance, and reveals the two new hyperscale customers as OpenAI and Apple. The author holds a large long position in AVGO (second only to Nvidia) via shares and has no intention of selling, calling it an "excellent long-term investment."

**Marvell (MRVL):** The author is actively bearish and holds no position. Rumors have circulated that Trainium 3 has been moved — partially or completely — to Alchip rather than Marvell. Separately, rumors suggest the Microsoft Maia 2 ramp will be larger than Amazon Trainium and Google Axion combined, though Marvell's role in that program is left unclear. The author says Marvell is "toxic" right now and wants "nothing to do with this stock."

[[irrationalanalysis]] frames the Micron miss as a structural story about Chinese memory competition, not a one-quarter blip, while Broadcom's new hyperscale customer disclosures (OpenAI + Apple) are the key surprise for the AI ASIC buildout thesis.
