[[irrationalanalysis]] recaps Broadcom's 2024 AI Infrastructure Day, offering color on Broadcom's custom ASIC customer list, networking strategy, co-packaged optics reliability, and silicon design speed.

**Customer #3 mystery.** Broadcom disclosed three hyperscaler custom-chip customers: Google (TPU), Meta (MITA recommendation-network accelerator), and an unnamed third. The author dismisses sell-side guesses pointing to U.S. hyperscalers — Amazon is locked into Marvell, Microsoft is too cost-sensitive for Broadcom margins, Oracle is all-in on Nvidia — and concludes with 99.9% confidence that customer #3 is a Chinese hyperscaler, most likely ByteDance. The rationale: ByteDance runs latency-sensitive, massively parallel, network-heavy workloads, and designing a chip that is PCIe/Ethernet-heavy rather than compute-dense can comply with U.S. export-control rules on memory bandwidth and compute density thresholds.

**Ethernet vs. Infiniband.** Broadcom used the event to aggressively promote open Ethernet over Infiniband, including benchmarks the author characterizes as deliberately misleading because they strip out Infiniband's in-network compute advantages (e.g., Nvidia SHARP for all-reduce). The author — who works on Ethernet in his day job but holds ~50% of his portfolio in Nvidia shares — argues both interconnects have merits and will coexist; Broadcom's one-sided framing amounts to "bovine excrement."

**Thor2 NIC.** Broadcom's Thor2 is described as a high-efficiency, traditional NIC — the opposite of the DPU direction the industry is moving toward, where the network card takes on ARM cores, fixed-function logic, and accelerators to free up GPU resources. The author views Broadcom's commentary here as misleading.

**Co-packaged optics (CPO).** Broadcom addressed CPO reliability but glossed over serviceability. Pluggable transceivers can be swapped; CPO cannot, demanding orders-of-magnitude higher reliability for everything except the laser. The author flags this as an unresolved economic viability question.

**Custom silicon speed and 3D wafer roadmap.** A presenter demonstrated that Broadcom completes all pre-silicon activities in 7–9 months — highlighted as impressive. Commentary on a 3D-wafer design targeted for 2026 and beyond was called "incredible."

**Conclusion.** The author is an incremental buyer of AVGO, calling it one of his favorite stocks given diversified revenue, competent management, strong existing technology, and a compelling roadmap. Cash deployment is limited by personal financial constraints (401K, ESPP, rent).
