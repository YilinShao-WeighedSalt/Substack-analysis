[[irrationalanalysis]] wraps up a volatile January 2026 with a rapid-fire update across semiconductors. The author survived a brutal month on Nvidia options — down $62K at the trough on Jan 20, recovering somewhat to -$36K by month end — but remains stubbornly bullish, holding call options at ~5.2x intrinsic leverage. The thesis is that buyside anti-Nvidia sentiment (driven by TPU narrative, China uncertainty, and rotation into memory/optics) is misguided and a vibe shift is expected within 2–4 months.

On Broadcom, the author is equally convicted. Taiwan supply-chain rumors about Mediatek challenging Broadcom's SerDes position are dismissed as "cheesed bullshit." At OCP, Mediatek's 224G SerDes demo had an unlabeled y-axis and zero BER numbers — a stark contrast to credible demos from Credo, Broadcom/TE, Astera Labs, Marvell, and Ciena. The author's read is that Google's TPU ambitions are being overstated and that CoWoS allocation leak rumors from Taiwan are unreliable. A detailed takedown post is forthcoming.

Intel is held for the long term, specifically for the fabs. Foundry Day may move the stock short-term; otherwise INTC is expected to grind sideways through H1. ASML backlog is cited as evidence that Intel 14A has real traction, though products group missteps keep buyside cautious.

Semicap remains a high-conviction long. The recent KLAC selloff is attributed to buyside misreading LRCX commentary as WFE growth guidance when it was actually share gains — a spreadsheet-driven overreaction. The author holds LRCX, CAMT, TER, NVMI, and ONTO and would add KLAC but is already at maximum leverage (margin at 4.20%).

In memory, exposure to NAND via Sandisk (buy-write) is described as terrifying but unavoidable; the covered calls served as a hedge on a violently cyclical position. For February earnings, LITE is flagged as a short-squeeze candidate. COHR is interesting as collateral damage if LITE squeezes and factor-neutral funds are forced to sell. SITM is held with a plan to buy more on any earnings puke; the iPhone Air miss may create a one-off headwind. GFS puts are being considered given Qualcomm RFFE exposure. QCOM and QRVO are both bearish: QRVO already reported bad earnings and Qualcomm's chart implies downside is priced in, though the author holds a small put position.
