[[irrationalanalysis]] presents the second annual "Irrational Ideas" — a curated list of obscure and high-conviction semiconductor investment ideas deliberately excluding the obvious names (Nvidia, Broadcom, TSMC, Lam Research). The post is organized into nine sections covering optics/connectivity, semicap, energy, timing, logic fabs, miscellaneous longs, funding/tactical shorts, neoclouds (to avoid), and Marvell.

**Optics/Connectivity** is the largest and most bullish section. Lumentum (LITE) is the top pick, held as the largest trading account position, due to its monopoly on ultra-high-power narrow-linewidth lasers and highest-quality EMLs for CPO. Tower Semi (TSEM) is the second top pick as an effective monopoly in silicon photonics fab, with better margins than its RF business. Ciena (CIEN) earns praise for in-house 400G SerDes and superior engineering ahead of a WDM/DCI upgrade wave. Semtech (SMTC) is highlighted for allegedly best-in-class 200G/lane PAM4 linear equalizer (analog CTLE) giving it ACC/LPO/MediaTek TPU torque; Macom (MTSI) favored for higher-quality photodiode revenue mix — author is buying both. Fabrinet (FN) is called the only credible western optical manufacturer matching Chinese quality. Coherent (COHR) is viewed as an inferior Lumentum but acknowledged for strong InP yields. Applied Optoelectronics (AAOI) seen as high-risk/high-reward on 800G Amazon qualification and CPO laser capacity. Furukawa Electric (5801.T) is owned in the long-only account for high-quality fiber assemblies and tunable lasers.

**Semicap**: Camtek (CAMT) preferred over Onto (ONTO) for geopolitical reasons (Israeli firm sells freely into China). Besi (BESI.AS) is king of hybrid bonding tools for CPO and HBM4. Aehr (AEHR) thesis unchanged — hyperscaler burn-in logic wafers, possibly AMD. Aixtron (AIXA.DE) attractive entry after five years of SiC-driven underperformance; positioned for InP/GaN epitaxy demand surge. Advantest (6857.T) dominates HPC testing. Nova (NVMI) initiated on subscriber request and bought for engineering transparency.

**Energy**: On Semi (ON) flagged as asymmetric on new vertical GaN power semis despite SiC/Tesla headwinds. EQT highlighted as lowest-cost US natural gas producer. Solaris Energy (SEI) and Bloom Energy (BE) both cited for "time to power" datacenter solutions — Bloom's 800V DC direct-to-datacenter seen as potentially superior solution on merit. Vistra (VST) and Talen (TLN) excluded on coal exposure.

**Timing**: Frequency Electronics (FEIM) as rubidium clock pure-play military name; SiTime (SITM) improving jitter to near-quartz levels at 80 fs.

**Logic Fabs**: Skywater (SKYT) as only viable US military old-node fab. Intel (INTC) is the #3-4 overall holding (~$315K exposure); thesis is pure IFS — 18AP fixing 18A's problems, advanced packaging opportunity, trading at same P/B as inferior GloFo (GFS).

**Miscellaneous Longs**: TTMI for high-end PCBs under shortage. Keysight (KEYS) as the safest AI datacenter play with an unassailable T&M moat. Sandisk (SNDK) traded profitably but no current position. Musashi Seimitsu (7220.T) for leading supercapacitors critical in high-power-density AI racks.

**Shorts**: QCOM (out of time thesis unchanged), ANET (NextHop AI threatening gross margins), CRDO (Marvell Alaska AEC delays inflated valuation), ALAB (meme SerDes repackager, Amazon risk from NVLink Fusion). GFS as natural short paired vs. TSEM.

**Neoclouds**: Broadly bearish; expects most to be wiped out by generation N+1 hardware supply shock within 1.5–3 years. Oracle (ORCL) flagged as too-big-to-fail exception.

**Marvell** (MRVL): Described as the most controversial semiconductor stock. Amazon (Trainium 3/4) content is largely gone, Microsoft ASIC a disaster, Innovium switching team departed. Celestial AI acquisition ($3.8B) is a high-variance binary bet on re-acquiring Amazon for Trainium 5/6/7. 800G optical DSP at 80%+ share but losing 1.6T share. Author remains long, views outcome as binary.
