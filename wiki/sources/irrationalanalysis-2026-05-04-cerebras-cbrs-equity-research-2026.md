[[irrationalanalysis]] published this updated LaTeX-typeset equity research report on Cerebras Systems ($CBRS) in May 2026, ahead of Cerebras's eventual Nasdaq IPO (priced May 13, 2026 at $185/share, first-day close $311.07). The author metaphorically frames the company as a "partially corroded challenge coin" — the original training ambition is dead and tarnished, but the underlying wafer-scale technology is solid and has been repurposed for ultra-high-speed inference. The tagline: "the clown car that stumbled upon a gold mine."

**Thesis pivot — training to inference:** Cerebras spent years positioning the Wafer-Scale Engine (WSE) as an Nvidia competitor for large-model training. That goal is assessed as effectively dead. However, the same architecture — hundreds of thousands of cores, on-chip SRAM in the gigabyte range, and near-zero inter-chip latency — turns out to be structurally advantageous for low-latency inference on large open-source models. This pivot is the core of the updated bull case: a company that failed at its original mission accidentally landed on a defensible inference niche.

**Commercial validation:** By the time of this report, Cerebras had secured a landmark Master Relationship Agreement with OpenAI worth over $20 billion, committing 750 MW of inference capacity through 2028 with an option to expand to 2 GW by 2030. AWS signed a binding term sheet in March 2026 to offer WSE hardware to cloud customers, providing hyperscaler distribution. These two deals represent a step-change in credibility relative to the 2024 G42-concentrated revenue base. Reported 2025 revenue was approximately $510 million (76% YoY growth), with a $24.6 billion remaining performance obligation backlog.

**Persistent bear flags:** The author's tone remains cautious. Customer concentration is still extreme — G42 and MBZUAI together represented 86% of 2025 revenue. GAAP net income of $237.8 million was inflated by a $363.3 million non-cash gain; the actual operating loss was $145.9 million. Two material weaknesses in internal controls were disclosed in the S-1/A. OpenAI exclusivity clauses constrain Cerebras from serving named competitors, limiting diversification. The CUDA ecosystem moat built by Nvidia over 15+ years is cited as the primary structural advantage Cerebras cannot replicate, keeping Nvidia as the dominant AI infrastructure incumbent.

**Competitive context:** The WSE-3 is described as delivering up to 15x faster inference than competing GPU solutions on open-source models and 2,625x more memory bandwidth than the B200 package for certain workloads. Groq, SambaNova, and Tenstorrent are noted as private-market inference competitors. Hyperscaler internal accelerators (Google TPU, AWS Trainium, Microsoft MAIA) represent additional long-term competitive pressure even as AWS simultaneously deploys Cerebras hardware.
