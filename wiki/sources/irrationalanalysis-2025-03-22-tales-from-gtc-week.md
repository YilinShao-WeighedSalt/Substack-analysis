A field dispatch from GTC 2025 week, covering eight noteworthy developments across semiconductors and AI infrastructure. Published by [[irrationalanalysis]].

**SoftBank acquires Ampere Computing.** The author views this as a strategic pump for ARM-LTD's tiny free float rather than a genuine bet on Ampere's custom v9 CPU cores. SoftBank's "Project Izanagi" with Broadcom involves Graphcore AI IP and Ampere cores, but the real motive is forcing ARM-LTD to recognize v9 royalty revenue and supporting the distorted ARM share price. The author notes hedge funds want to short ARM but are deterred by the extremely low free float.

**MediaTek/Nvidia IP agreement.** Details remain murky — even the MediaTek presenter was unclear on what IP would transfer. The author flags this as bad for Broadcom, "very bad" for Marvell, and somewhat negative for Synopsys/Cadence. Magnitude of impact is uncertain; the author is watching closely.

**Nvidia Vera custom ARM-ISA CPU core.** Nvidia has completely designed out ARM-RTL (Neoverse) content in its Vera CPU, following Grace where it already dropped ARM's NoC IP. The author argues ARM-RTL is systematically low-quality — humiliated by Apple since 2013, with only five competent microarchitecture teams in the world (Apple, AMD, Intel, Qualcomm, Tenstorrent). Vera reduces ARM-LTD royalty rates meaningfully.

**Cerebras GTC party / memecoin.** Largely color: the author attended, noted G42 was footing the bill, and observed physical "meme coins" being distributed. Skeptical of Cerebras's batch-1 inference economics given SRAM scaling and waferIO constraints.

**CoreWeave IPO.** The author describes the S-1 as "radioactive garbage." Key concern: Microsoft is both a major customer and a direct competitor via Azure. SoftBank's Satya Nadella is publicly signaling intent to wait for GPU overcapacity rather than sign new leases, creating acute customer concentration risk. The author frames the investment thesis as requiring mental gymnastics to justify.

**AI GPU usable life / TCO.** Disputes SemiAnalysis's 4–6 year usable economic life assumption for AI GPUs. Author's base case is 2.5 years (bear case 2 years), implying far worse TCO economics for Hopper-era neoclouds and ASIC-based clouds (e.g., Trainium 2).

**Google compiler team attrition to Etched AI.** Several senior Google TPU compiler engineers have departed for Etched, prompting the author to take Etched more seriously than before.

**Micron earnings reaction.** Sold most calls at a profit to de-risk. HBM ramp remains slow, margins are compressed by CXMT LPDDR5 ramp and broader pricing pressure. No new information this week; watching closely.

**SiTime in GB300.** SiTime timing chip appears on a mini M.2 daughterboard in Nvidia GB300, likely due to PCB routing constraints for clean clock distribution across the switch's many ports.
