[[globalsemiresearch]] publishes a direct rebuttal to SemiAnalysis's June 9 note "Powered Down, Lights Off," which claimed co-packaged optics (CPO) deployments are materially delayed — scale-out CPO revised down through 2027 and scale-up pushed to 2029. The sell-off in Lumentum, Coherent, Applied Optoelectronics, Corning, and Marvell within hours of that note is the backdrop for this piece. The author's core claim: the delay thesis is not a supply-chain finding; it is a positioning call dressed in supply-chain language, and it collapses when you speak to the people actually building CPO hardware.

**The 19% yield argument deconstructed.** SemiAnalysis's bear case rests on a single compound-yield calculation: 95% optical-engine attach yield raised to the 32nd power (one per COUPE in Spectrum-6) yields ~19% system yield. The author calls this a conjuring trick. It freezes yield at a pessimistic snapshot, ignores yield improvement over time, ignores screening and binning, and ignores redundancy (Spectrum-6 carries spare engines so a single bad coupling does not brick the switch). SA's own report partially refutes the number by noting that Nvidia's Quantum-X — with only 3 COUPEs per module — is in better shape precisely because modules can be screened, which is an implicit admission that the 0.95^32 framing is a function of module granularity, not physics. The author acknowledges real engineering challenges: a soldered switch substrate has no rework path, Spectrum-6 reportedly showed >3.5 dB insertion loss in system-level testing eating the entire optical channel budget, and neither Nvidia nor TSMC had pinned the root cause at time of writing.

**The upstream supply chain falsifies the delay story.** Nvidia's guidance to Coherent and Lumentum for high-power CW (continuous-wave) lasers rose from ~40 million units in January to ~100 million units by April–May 2026 — a jump the author interprets as pulling 2027–2028 demand forward. Back-solving: ~10 million+ ELSFP external-laser units implies CPO switch shipments in the range of 200,000–300,000 units in 2027 scaling toward 600,000–800,000 in 2028, consistent with TSMC's COUPE production plan. Lumentum announced its largest-ever ELSFP purchase order, and both Lumentum and Coherent have stated they expect to ship into scale-up hardware by 2027. The author argues that no rational company books out an entire FA production line for a technology supposedly delivering "meaningfully below prior expectations."

**TSMC ramp check.** Per the author's proprietary channel checks, TSMC's COUPE ramp is running ahead of expectations, not behind.
