[[citrini]] publishes the AI-focused section of the Citrini Research Annual Review, covering the one-year track record of the Global AI Beneficiaries thematic basket and laying out forward-looking views across five distinct sub-themes: AAPL vs. GOOGL in on-device AI, AI datacenter power demand, and individual thoughts on NVDA, CRDO, CGNX, and TSMC.

## AAPL vs. GOOGL: On-Device AI and Consumer Trust

The most decisive rotation in the AI basket is the swap of Alphabet out and Apple in. Citrini argues that the next phase of AI adoption shifts from cloud-hosted foundation models toward agentic, on-device AI assistants that serve as a consumer's "single sign-on for life" — integrating biometric identity, location, payment, and communication data. Apple (AAPL) is uniquely positioned to own this last-mile interface because of its closed hardware-software ecosystem and the deep consumer trust it has cultivated around data privacy. Google's (GOOGL) 2024 AI rollouts — most notably the Gemini Search product plagued by hallucinations — eroded investor confidence and exposed a fragmented strategy across autonomous vehicles, robotics, and language models. Citrini deletes GOOGL from the basket and upgrades AAPL to a core long, anticipating that Apple Intelligence (announced at WWDC 2024) will catalyze a device upgrade supercycle.

## AI Datacenter Power Demand

The annual review dedicates substantial space to the intersection of AI capital expenditure and power infrastructure. As hyperscalers scale GPU clusters to hundreds of thousands of units, electrical power has become the binding constraint on datacenter expansion. Citrini frames this as a convergence of AI capex and fiscal infrastructure spending, arguing that grid upgrades, power management, and cooling solutions represent a durable multi-year opportunity irrespective of near-term AI spending cycles. This theme is flagged as a companion to the separate "Utility of Bubbles" section of the same annual review.

## NVDA, CRDO, CGNX, TSMC Thoughts

**NVDA**: Remains the dominant GPU incumbent, underwritten by CUDA's entrenched developer ecosystem. Competitors cannot simply replicate the hardware; they must recreate the full software stack — a multi-year moat. Post-record Q1 FY2025 results ($26B revenue, $22.6B in data center), Citrini views NVDA as a mature Phase 1 holding with continued upside but notes increasing competition from custom silicon.

**CRDO**: A top-conviction pick. Credo Technology's active electrical cables (AECs) and high-speed SerDes solutions address the power-efficient, high-bandwidth connectivity needs of AI clusters. AI represented nearly 75% of CRDO's Q4 FY2024 revenue. Citrini had held the position since the initial basket formation; CRDO was up roughly 140% from their entry at the time of the review.

**CGNX**: Cognex surfaces as a machine vision beneficiary of AI-driven industrial automation. The company's integration of AI into vision systems positions it to benefit from smart manufacturing and datacenter construction inspection use cases. The Moritex acquisition expands its optical sensing capabilities globally.

**TSMC**: Characterized as the indispensable node in the AI supply chain — foundry to NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Apple, AMD, and most hyperscaler custom ASIC programs. Citrini holds TSMC overweight, citing recovering automotive segment demand, AI tail demand expanding across customers, and diminishing market sensitivity to Taiwan geopolitical risk.
