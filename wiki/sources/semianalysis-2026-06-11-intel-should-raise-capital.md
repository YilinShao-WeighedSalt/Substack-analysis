[[semianalysis]] argues that Intel should issue equity now — roughly 4–5% dilution raising ~$25 billion — to fund its foundry buildout while the stock trades at its richest valuation since the 2000 bubble.

Intel's board has been overhauled: Franky Yeary departed after 17 years and was replaced by industry veterans including the ex-Qualcomm chair, Lip Bu Tan as CEO, Steve Sanghi (Microchip), Stacey Smith, and Eric Meurice (ASML). The new board understands technology rather than financial engineering, giving the authors confidence a large strategic capital raise is viable.

The case for equity rests on three legs. First, Intel has already raised ~$20 billion from the U.S. government (up to 433 million shares at $20.47, a 9.9% stake), SoftBank ($23.00/share), and Nvidia ($23.28/share). Every anchor investor is already in-the-money, so further issuance above those levels is accretive to book value and does not punish early believers. The sovereign anchor also gives Intel the ability to clear a large offering cheaply.

Second, the alternatives are exhausted. Apollo's $11.2B SCIP on Fab 34 was unwound in March/April 2026 for $14.2B (cash plus a $6.5B bridge), proving the SCIP structure was expensive capital. Debt already stands at ~$45B ($51.5B post-bridge). Major asset sales — Altera (51% to Silver Lake at $8.75B EV) and Mobileye share sales — have been largely harvested; Intel retains ~48% of Altera and ~78% of Mobileye.

Third, the demand book is now visible. The Terafab program (initial 100k WSPM targeting 1 million) is anchored by SpaceX and Tesla. Nvidia's DGX Rubin NVL8 ships with dual Intel Xeon 6 CPUs; Google signed a multiyear Xeon/IPU deal; SambaNova came in on inference. The full multi-phase Terafab project is estimated at up to $119 billion. Intel's ~$498B market cap supports a large follow-on, and the authors note that if Cerebras can raise $5.55B, Intel can raise $25B.

The authors' core view: a decade of financial engineering through buybacks should now be reversed — issue equity into strength to fund the Terafab capex cycle and cement silicon sovereignty leadership at exactly the moment that AI-driven agentic CPU demand is accelerating.
