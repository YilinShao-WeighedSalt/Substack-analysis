[[irrationalanalysis]] surveys the optical/photonics semiconductor landscape, identifying high-quality investments amid a field of "shitco" operators. The post is structured around technical primers on Optical Circuit Switching (OCS), Co-Packaged Optics (CPO), and silicon timing (SiTime), followed by investment verdicts across several categories.

On the long-haul side, Ciena is the author's core pure-play holding for multi-datacenter AI training interconnect; recent Fabrinet earnings confirmed the thesis. Cisco is flagged as a cheaper alternative with a "mini-Ciena inside surrounded by garbage," but pure-plays are preferred.

Coherent receives cautious treatment. The author concludes it makes excellent low-phase-noise lasers but is uncomfortable with SEC inquiry letters and management credibility. Coherent's OCS demo at OFC is dismissed as unimpressive — the demo cherry-picked low insertion-loss paths and barely passed specs. If the stock spikes on OCS hype the author would sell. Lumentum is seen as more expensive than Coherent on forward P/E and its claimed differentiation is unverifiable; a small call-option position is held for optionality only.

Fabrinet is described as a long-term optical winner: CPO's complexity leap plays directly to Fabrinet's manufacturing experience and IP-protection capabilities, but the stock is volatile due to leveraged traders.

SiTime receives the most extended analysis. The author argues the market has missed SiTime's core value proposition: programmable 0-PPM frequency synchronization. While SiTime's jitter is inferior to quartz oscillators, its ability to digitally guarantee 0 PPM across every link — confirmed by Nvidia's GB200 design win — is uniquely valuable as data rates rise and CDR circuits become PPM-constrained. The TAM spans switches, NICs, AI accelerators, AOCs, and AECs. Risks include MegaChips' ~15% overhang and Apple modem volume uncertainty.

Tower Semiconductor and GlobalFoundries are reviewed as photonic-fab plays but dismissed as non-pure-plays with heavy RFFE exposure. If forced to choose, the author would pitch Tower Semi.

Applied Optoelectronics (AAOI), Innolight, and Eoptolink are labeled ultra-degen due to China tariff exposure and management quality concerns; AAOI faces a short report characterizing management as incompetent.

## Calls

- [[FN]] — LONG — px@n/a — long-term CPO manufacturing winner with experience and IP moat, short-term volatile
- [[CIEN]] — LONG — px@n/a — pure-play long-haul optics for multi-datacenter AI training; valuation stretched but story intact
- [[CSCO]] — MENTION — px@n/a — cheaper alternative to Ciena with an embedded optics business but surrounded by non-core assets
- [[COHR]] — NEUTRAL — px@n/a — good low-phase-noise lasers but suspicious management and OCS hype is a sell trigger
- [[SITM]] — LONG — px@n/a — 0-PPM sync via programmable MEMS oscillators is an unrecognized alpha opportunity validated by Nvidia GB200 design win
- [[TSEM]] — NEUTRAL — px@n/a — quality photonics fab but RFFE exposure and not a pure-play; preferred over GFS if forced to pick
- [[GFS]] — NEUTRAL — px@n/a — only 8.5% of revenue in photonics; RFFE mix and Mubadala overhang make it unattractive as an optics play
