[[semidoped]] covers Computex 2026 from the show floor, focusing on two dominant themes: optical interconnects (specifically co-packaged optics) and high-voltage power delivery infrastructure for AI data centers.

**Marvell and the Jensen Endorsement.** The most market-moving moment of the show was Jensen Huang appearing at Marvell's networking keynote and declaring Marvell "the next one trillion dollar company," sending MRVL up roughly 70%. The author was present on the floor while filming and missed the immediate trade — calling it "a hundred billion dollar sentence." Marvell unveiled its Teralynx switch at 102.4 Tbps, positioning itself as a serious interconnect competitor to Broadcom's Tomahawk 6 (also 102.4 Tbps with CPO). The author's criticism of Marvell: the company should be doing a better job articulating its cross-selling potential between its ASIC custom silicon and interconnect/optical businesses, as the combination is strategically powerful.

**Co-Packaged Optics (CPO).** CPO dominated show discussions. It is coming, but manufacturing challenges in yield, testing, and scale remain unresolved. Ayar Labs demonstrated a fully-scaled CPO rack implementation using external laser modules. As intermediate alternatives, "XPO" (pluggable optical modules the size of a hand, equivalent to eight OSFP plugs) and near-package optics emerged as pragmatic bridging solutions before full CPO mass production. Broadcom's Tomahawk 6 is a key CPO switch design.

**Power Delivery.** 800-volt power architectures appeared universally across data center rack vendors at the show. Silicon carbide handles grid-to-800V conversion; plain silicon handles 800V step-downs (not GaN). LiteOn was called out specifically in this context. Power cables have grown to forearm-thickness liquid-cooled whips — a visceral signal of the efficiency gains from higher voltages.

**Prefabricated AI Infrastructure.** Vertiv, Delta, Schneider Electric, and Eaton all showed prefabricated AI infrastructure blocks that cut deployment time by roughly 50%. Ketaflow demonstrated a chest-freezer-sized immersion cooling unit with GPU racks submerged in non-conductive liquid for edge AI deployments.

**Intel Foundry Signal.** Austin (co-author) highlights Clearwater Forest (Xeon 6 Plus) as "a really compelling Intel Foundry story." The chip uses 17 chiplets across three process nodes (Intel 7 for I/O tiles, Intel 3 for active base tiles, Intel 18A for compute tiles) with EMIB interconnects, Foveros Direct 3D logic-on-logic stacking, backside power delivery (PowerVia), and RibbonFET transistors. The argument is not that Intel beats TSMC on process, but that Intel Foundry's advanced packaging depth is maturing to the point where it provides a necessary industry alternative to TSMC CoWoS capacity constraints.
