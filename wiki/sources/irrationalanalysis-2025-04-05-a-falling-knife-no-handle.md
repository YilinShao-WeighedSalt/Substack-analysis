Published on April 5, 2025, this post from [[irrationalanalysis]] is a short, visceral macro-sentiment piece written in the immediate aftermath of a sharp multi-day equity selloff triggered by the Trump tariff escalation. The core message is blunt: do not buy the dip.

The author opens by sharing screenshots of their trading account, noting they had been very active over the preceding three days — covering many short positions heading into the weekend to avoid overnight exposure, while keeping "smartphone exposed" shorts on. YTD performance and all-time records are shared transparently, with downloadable trade logs attached, as a way of demonstrating accountability without giving explicit advice.

The intellectual argument rests on a single analogy: the situation in Washington resembles a hospital whose insane asylum wing has staged a revolt, with the "chief lunatic" now in charge. The author argues it is futile to psychoanalyze or negotiate with an actor operating outside rational incentive structures. The rational response is to fortify and wait — stockpile cash, do not deploy capital, and let the chaos exhaust itself.

The author diagnoses the proximate cause of the three-day selloff as the unraveling of elite plutocrat positioning: wealthy insiders who spent months publicly flattering Trump in hopes of influencing policy have been forced to concede they cannot control him. When that illusion broke, risk assets repriced. Buying into the bounce, the author warns, is buying exit liquidity from those same insiders.

The author's own long-only accounts are described as holding significant cash. No price targets, no specific stock calls, and no sector calls are made. The post closes with a tease for a forthcoming CPO (co-packaged optics) writeup delayed by personal circumstances.
