[[irrationalanalysis]] covers two major semiconductor catalysts on July 25, 2024: the collapse of MaxLinear's earnings and an accelerated Apple modem timeline that devastates Qualcomm's near-term outlook.

**MaxLinear implosion.** MXL reported Q2 revenue of $92M (-50% y/y, missing the $100M estimate) and guided Q3 to $70–90M versus consensus of $111M. Adjusted operating margin came in at -21% versus +16.2% a year prior, and the company swung to negative operating cash flow. The author is 99.9% confident the US government revoked MaxLinear's license to sell to Huawei, and suspects MXL was routing product through shell companies that have now been blacklisted, gutting its wireless infrastructure revenue. Beyond export issues, MXL is losing share across its entire broadband/connectivity portfolio — DOCSIS, WiFi, PON — getting squeezed between Broadcom (AVGO) at the high end and MediaTek (2454.TW) at the low end. Management's characterization of share losses as a mere "failure to attach" WiFi to broadband platforms is called out as corporate obfuscation. MXL's PAM4 optics DSP for AI networking is described as probably inferior technology but potentially a cheap optionality play given the large TAM; Marvell (MRVL) holds ~80% share and AVGO ~20% in that segment.

**Apple modem acceleration.** Based on a Ming-Chi Kuo report, Apple has gotten its modem working much earlier than anticipated. The author's prior base case had QCOM retaining 100% iPhone modem share in 2025, dropping to 80% in 2026, then below 10% in early 2027. The new scenario has QCOM at only 40–50% share in 2025 and under 20% in 2026. The author views this as catastrophic for Qualcomm: incremental gains in Auto and Laptop/PC will be more than offset by the Apple hole. A large short position in QCOM was initiated on this news. A large short in AMD was also recently opened. Cash was redeployed into AVGO and held for dip-buying in favored names.

**Portfolio positioning.** Bears: AMD, QCOM. Bulls: NVDA, AVGO. The author targets outperformance versus SMH by year-end, or account blow-up trying.
