[[semianalysis]] argues that xAI's Colossus 2 project is not a one-time wonder but a repeatable and accelerating capability — and that xAI is on track to build the first gigawatt-scale AI datacenter in the world, surpassing Meta Superintelligence and Anthropic in total single-cluster compute by Q3 2025.

**Construction speed.** The Colossus 2 project launched March 7, 2025 with xAI acquiring a 1 million square-foot warehouse in Memphis, Tennessee. By August 22, 2025, 119 air-cooled chillers were on site — roughly 200MW of cooling capacity, enough for ~110,000 GB200 NVL72. That build took six months versus the 15 months it took Oracle, Crusoe, and OpenAI for comparable capacity.

**Power strategy: Tennessee + Mississippi.** Memphis faced local opposition to on-site turbines, so xAI's key move was acquiring a former Duke Energy power plant across the state line in Southaven, Mississippi, where regulators granted temporary approval to run gas turbines for up to 12 months without a full permit. Seven 35MW turbines (245MW) are operational there. Tesla Megapacks and medium-voltage power lines connect the Mississippi plant to the Memphis datacenter.

**Solaris Energy Infrastructure partnership.** To scale power at speed, xAI relies heavily on NYSE-listed Solaris Energy Infrastructure (SEI). Solaris owns a 600MW fleet of rental gas turbines, of which ~400MW currently serve xAI. xAI represents ~67% of SEI's 1,700MW orderbook (1,140MW). The two formed a joint venture (50.1% Solaris / 49.9% xAI) to own the Southaven turbines. Approximately 460MW is installed or under construction; the JV spent $112M in capex in Q2 2025. Solaris expects >1.1GW fully operational by Q2 2027, with potential to reach 1.5GW+ if xAI pulls remaining allocation.

**Financing.** Colossus 2 will require tens of billions in CapEx. xAI's external revenue is minimal — most of the reported nine-digit ARR consists of inter-company transfers from X.com. A ~$40B fundraise is in progress at a ~$200B valuation, with Saudi PIF expected to play a leading role. A reciprocal datacenter build in Saudi Arabia is likely as part of the deal. Beyond external investors, Elon Musk can leverage Tesla and SpaceX equity to fund xAI directly.

**Competitive framing.** The post briefly covers xAI's unique RL training methodology (paywalled) and talent/product developments (Grok, Ani), but the public section focuses on the infrastructure thesis: that xAI's speed-to-power advantage is structural, not accidental.
