A quarterly roundup from [[irrationalanalysis]] covering every notable name in the SMH semiconductor ETF plus selected "++ names" outside the index. The author benchmarks their trading account against SMH.

**The "Relatively Safe" AI Trio — NVDA, AVGO, TSM.** All three are designated blind-buy-and-hold names over a 2+ year horizon. The only incremental update is on Nvidia's Rubin CPX: the author endorses SemiAnalysis's bullish take, noting the chip is technically straightforward for Nvidia to build (GDDR7 PHY already exists for gaming, simpler package design, existing PCIe PHY). The author is baffled that hyperscalers and AI hardware startups have not produced an equivalent prefill-optimized chip.

**Intel — Heavily Biased Patriotic Pitch.** The author holds Intel shares and call options and is explicitly biased. Intel 18A is described as "completely unviable for 3rd-party use" (PDK missing device models, high variation forcing designers to overbuild, negating area gains from GAA+BSPD). Intel 18AP is assigned a ~70% chance of being "tolerable-to-decent" for low-priority external products. The author argues Intel's entire valuation should rest on its leading-edge fab network — worth at least 2x GlobalFoundries — and that Trump's political support has placed a floor around 1.2x book. The author makes a fiduciary-duty argument directly to American chip design companies: moving low-priority products to 18AP is in shareholders' long-term interest as a hedge against TSMC pricing power. Lip-Bu Tan is seen as capable of executing the turnaround with government support.

**Micron.** A re-iteration of a post from two days prior. The author is constructive but explicitly says SK Hynix (000660.KS) is a better risk/reward — especially on price/book — for any institution with Korean equity access. Author holds Micron because they are retail and unwilling to use the EU-listed derivative.

**Qualcomm.** Broadly negative. The author owns a Snapdragon X Elite laptop (bought at launch) and reports it trails a competing Intel Meteor Lake machine on battery life, CPU, GPU, and everyday app performance. The 5G/mmWave laptop pitch is dismissed. The in-package DRAM approach on Qualcomm's X2 is criticized as a repeat of Intel's Lunar Lake mistake — it destroys OEM margin on memory upsells. The author will not buy an X2, instead targeting an Intel Panther Lake laptop. Tone is skeptical to bearish on the PC ramp narrative.

**EDA Majors (CDNS, SNPS).** Both are solid long-term investments with "rich valuations." The author recently liquidated a large Synopsys position to de-lever and book profit on concerns about an overheated market. Cautions against holding either in a leveraged account.

**Teradyne.** Four separate industry sources have told the author that Teradyne has gained share from Advantest for Nvidia Rubin ATE — characterized as potentially huge. Also cited as a robotics play by Citrini Research.

**Aehr Test Systems.** Described as a "bubble stock like Astera Labs" and "the GameStop of semiconductors — but with actual substance and AI exposure." The author holds a long position and has watched multiple short-sellers get squeezed 20–40%. Acknowledges it is toxic to institutional investors who cannot justify going long, and many are short. The author frames it as a high-risk/high-reward moonshot.

**Apple Modem Basket (++ names).** A long/short basket around "Apple Modem Acceleration" is described but details are deferred to a separate section on disclosures. Named adjacent stocks include SiTime and Verizon as thematically correlated names the author wanted an excuse to discuss.
