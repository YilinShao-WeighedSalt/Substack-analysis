## Summary
Covers Cerebras's first earnings call post-IPO. Q1 revenue $193.4M (+94% YoY), FY26 guide $855–865M (beat estimates). Author calls transcript a "trainwreck" but maintains LONG conviction. Revised WSE-3 rack price to $700K–$850K (from prior $300K assumption) and token generation to 700/sec/user (from 3,000). Models packaging yield at 20% — the central financial variable — implying ~42% gross margin; improvement to 50% yield would unlock ~72% margins. KV cache competition with model weights in SRAM is a critical architectural constraint due to limited chip-edge I/O. Three investment catalysts: WSE-4 must support FP8 (currently FP16-only), face-to-face I/O (two sources report this in development), and packaging yield investor day. Author claims third corroborating source on Nvidia/Groq acquisition being "highly linked" to Cerebras's inference threat.
