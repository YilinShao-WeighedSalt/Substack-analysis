[[irrationalanalysis]] provides a sweeping review of Hot Chips 2024, rating every presentation from Excellent to Dumpster Fire, then drawing explicit long/short conclusions for public and private companies.

**Standout bullish call — AVGO:** Broadcom's co-packaged optics (CPO) presentation was the conference highlight. The author moved his CPO timeline from 2028 to 2026 for high-volume production. Broadcom solved reliability by externalizing the laser to a pluggable front-panel module; all photonic and electrical components use silicon-based processes with fan-out wafer-level packaging and strong yields. Real production-quality FEC data was shown publicly, including unsolved transmitter linearity issues the team expects to resolve within ~12 months. The author names AVGO the public winner and explicitly names MRVL's oDSP business as the casualty: "Marvell DSP revenue goes bye bye in 2026."

**Bearish call — MRVL:** Marvell is the public loser. The short-term outlook is framed as temporarily strong (Amazon orders, oDSP, telco recovery), but the author issues a hard warning: "DO NOT INVEST IN THIS COMPANY LONG TERM. PLAN TO BE OUT BY SUMMER 2025." The CPO displacement thesis drives the medium-term bear case.

**Bearish call — INTC:** Intel Lunar Lake is manufactured almost entirely on TSMC N3B, pushing COGS up 80–100% versus Meteor Lake. Sell-side models gross margins rising sequentially, which the author views as completely wrong. The post bluntly states "Lunar Lake might bankrupt Intel" and names INTC the public loser runner-up.

**Bullish call — ARM:** Named as the single highest-conviction trade: "Long ARM for the most concentrated alpha trade of all time," positioned as the primary beneficiary as cloud hyperscalers abandon Ampere and go vertical with ARM-licensed designs.

**Bullish (runner-up) — NVDA:** Named public winner runner-up for AI-driven chip design tooling and the widening software moat (Quazar/MX micro-scaling). Blackwell presentation contained little new content but the underlying moat commentary is positive.

**Bearish mention — QCOM:** The Qualcomm on-device AI tutorial is dismissed as "copeium advertisement for QCOM irrelevance in the genAI era." Author argues sub-10B LLMs are inferior, on-device inference drains battery, and his own Snapdragon X Elite laptop barely activated the NPU.

**Macro framing:** The OpenAI infrastructure presentation (Trevor Cai) is labeled "Terrifying." The author draws explicit parallels to dot-com and tulip mania, arguing the genAI capex bubble will eventually produce massive compute overcapacity. Moore's Law's economic dimension is declared dead; chiplets and advanced packaging continue density scaling but the cost case for moving every product to the latest node is gone.
