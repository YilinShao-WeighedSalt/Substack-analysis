[[irrationalanalysis]] reviews 1Q CY2025 earnings across ten semiconductor and optical names, offering blunt post-call verdicts grounded in supply-chain and architecture analysis.

**Intel (INTC):** The author holds long calls as a "dismemberment" volatility trade, not a bull thesis. Falcon Shores GPU is cancelled — the right call given Nvidia's dominance and hyperscaler custom silicon from Broadcom/Marvell. Clearwater Forest is massively delayed; management blames packaging but the author suspects 18A parametric yield is so bad Intel lacks capacity to ramp it alongside Panther Lake. Intel Products is rumored to be moving programs to TSMC N3/N2, an indictment of IFS. The author believes Intel is "pretty much dead" as an integrated device manufacturer and expects asset/organ sales to drive further stock volatility. Various rumours circulate about a GloFo–IFS merger or CEO changes.

**AMD:** The author made money on the short side going into the print. Datacenter GPU revenue fell sequentially; management stopped disclosing segment detail, which the author reads as intentional obfuscation ahead of MI350 competing against Nvidia GB300. AMD has no rack-scale-up solution until UALink, estimated 2026–2027. The verdict: wait for MI350 to prove dead-on-arrival before AMD becomes a "compelling CPU story."

**Qualcomm + ARM:** QCOM numbers were fine but the stock sold off. On Snapdragon X, enterprise adoption is weak due to compatibility issues, and Huawei compliance is zero, capping QTL growth. The ARM lawsuit outcome was a major reveal: Qualcomm's ALA rate is only 1.1% (vs. the assumed 2.5%) and does not expire until 2032, which is a significant blow to ARM's royalty upgrade thesis. v9 royalty revenues are not growing. ARM is in structural trouble; Qualcomm plans to sell datacenter CPU chiplets that directly compete with ARM Compute Subsystem offerings.

**Fabrinet (FN):** Stock fell on fears of Nvidia multi-sourcing transceivers. The author is not concerned long-term — co-packaged optics complexity and IP requirements should favor Fabrinet — but notes excessive supply-chain speculation volatility makes this a stressful hold.

**Coherent (COHR):** The author admits to misplaying this position. A surprise data point: a hyperscaler other than Google is buying optical circuit switches, which the author had previously dismissed as a niche. Flags it for deeper investigation.

**SiTime (SITM):** Results were solid and guidance was uneventful, yet the stock dropped sharply. The author attributes it to a pod fund overreacting to a one-month iPhone SE launch pull-forward. Bought the dip aggressively.

**Qorvo + Skyworks (QRVO, SWKS):** Low/mid-tier Android RF content is structurally shrinking. Qorvo Apple content won't grow. Skyworks lost Apple share, which the author believes went to Broadcom — whose superior filters compensate for Apple's in-house modem decode performance. RFFE as a category is "radioactive."

**Onto Innovation (ONTO):** The author holds ONTO in multiple accounts. The notable call: ONTO's commentary on HBM ramp trends confirms SK Hynix and/or Micron accelerated capacity while Samsung stalls. NAND CapEx is rising, keeping the layers race alive. The author is long Micron calls.
