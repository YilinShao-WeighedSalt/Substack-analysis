[[irrationalanalysis]] lays out a bifurcated H1 2025 view on the two major datacenter GPU players: constructive long-term on Nvidia despite near-term manufacturing noise, and structurally bearish on AMD due to gross-margin compression the sell-side has not yet priced in.

**Nvidia (NVDA).** The author's largest position remains Nvidia stock — no shares sold. The concern du jour is GB200 NVL72 manufacturing issues, blamed largely on signal-integrity problems with the passive copper cables Nvidia chose to hit 200G NVLink speeds: impedance mismatches creating reflections the SerDes equalizer cannot fully correct. The author is not worried about the long-term and expects GB300 (H2 2025) to resolve the issue via improved SerDes (more FFE taps, stronger CTLE), citing enough real-world channel data for Nvidia engineers to correct course. Near-term, the risk is mild sell-side panic and multiple compression; the response would be to aggressively build a call-option position on any dip. The ambition of NVL72 — 72 GPUs in a single NVLink domain — is viewed as worth the growing pains, especially for enabling superior reasoning-model inference.

**AMD.** No economic position held at time of writing (prior put position taken to profit). The bearish thesis centers on sell-side gross-margin models that show margins rising as MI300X/MI325X become a larger revenue share — which the author argues is flatly wrong. Key pressure points: (1) Nvidia's vanilla HGX Blackwell eliminates AMD's TCO advantage on per-GPU performance; GB200 NVL72 obliterates AMD in flagship use cases. (2) AMD's software stack remains weak, confining it to niche internal hyperscaler workloads. (3) Meta (largest AMD GPU customer) and Microsoft are extracting steep volume discounts, undermining the revenue bull case. The November 2024 4% workforce reduction is interpreted as a signal that AMD realized DC GPU revenue would miss without severe price cuts, so OpEx reduction became the EPS lever. The author forecasts a massive DC GPU revenue air-gap in Q2/Q3 2025, with further downside risk in Q4 if MI400X launches late or poorly.

## Calls

- [[NVDA]] — LONG — px@n/a — holds largest position in shares; temporary GB200 NVL72 signal-integrity issues do not change the long-term thesis; GB300 fixes expected in H2 2025; would add calls aggressively on any panic-driven dip
- [[AMD]] — SHORT — px@n/a — sell-side DC GPU gross-margin models are wrong; aggressive discounting to Meta and Microsoft, weak software, and Blackwell competition create a revenue and margin cliff in Q2/Q3 2025
