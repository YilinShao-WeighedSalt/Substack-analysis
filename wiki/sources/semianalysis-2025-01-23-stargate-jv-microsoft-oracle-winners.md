[[semianalysis]] dissects the real capital structure and infrastructure reality behind the Trump-era OpenAI Stargate announcement — a claimed $500B AI infrastructure commitment — separating what is genuinely underway from marketing hyperbole, and mapping out the project's clear winners and losers across hyperscalers, GPU vendors, and technology partners.

**The $500B number is mostly TCO, not committed equity.** The full figure represents Total Cost of Ownership over four years — encompassing capex for datacenters, servers, networking, power, and debt/financing costs — not upfront capital commitments. Only the first phase ($100B) is actually certain, and even that is partial. SoftBank and OpenAI each contributed ~$19B in equity for 40% ownership stakes; Oracle and UAE sovereign fund MGX each contributed ~$7B. Musk's claim that SoftBank had "well under $10B secured" was partially right; Altman's counter that construction was already underway was also accurate.

**Phase 1 is entirely the Oracle–Lancium–Crusoe Abilene campus.** The entirety of the initial $100B deployment is directed at the Lancium Crusoe Abilene Clean Campus in Texas, which SemiAnalysis had documented four months earlier in their Multi-Datacenter Training report. The campus has 1GW of power energized in 2025, with another 1GW unlocked for a total of 2.2GW supply and ~1.8GW of critical IT capacity. Eight buildings are planned with up to 50,000 GPUs each, deploying GB200/B200 clusters. OpenAI pays roughly ~$2.80 per GPU hour for the first GB200 cluster (TCO of ~$2.38/hr at full scale). Nvidia's GPU revenue from Stargate Phase 1 is estimated at ~$37.5B, with total IT equipment capex around $49B.

**Microsoft is the clear loser.** The Stargate venture is the culmination of OpenAI's deliberate diversification away from Microsoft's Azure. Talks for Microsoft to co-fund a $100B, 5GW mega-datacenter fell apart in 2024 after Microsoft grew cautious about subsidizing a growing rival. OpenAI subsequently signed large compute deals with Oracle, CoreWeave, Nscale, Amazon, and Google. Microsoft was left out of the Stargate equity group entirely — a structural shift in one of tech's most important partnerships.

**Arm's inclusion is labeled "fake news."** While Arm was listed as a Stargate technology partner in the announcement, SemiAnalysis argues this is a branding exercise with no substantive capital or technology commitment — Arm is not an investor, not a chip supplier for this phase, and its presence on the partner list is characterized as misleading.

**Oracle is the infrastructure winner.** Oracle secured the anchor infrastructure role for Phase 1 through an existing deal with OpenAI, cementing a long-term hyperscaler relationship with the industry's most prominent AI company. This is a structural win for Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) against AWS, Azure, and GCP.
