[[irrationalanalysis]] opens the post by acknowledging that the three "safe" semiconductor investments are TSM (2330.TW), AVGO, and NVDA — but argues that outsized market returns require more dangerous ideas.

## The Great HBM Trade

The central thesis is built on Samsung DS-Memory's persistent failure to qualify HBM3E for Nvidia. The author explains that HBM requires Through-Silicon Via (TSV) stacking, resulting in roughly 3x wafer intensity vs. DDR5. SK Hynix uses MR-MUF packaging; Samsung and Micron use TC-NCF. Samsung DS-Memory has repeatedly failed Nvidia's HBM3E qualification — while simultaneously supplying AMD MI300/325X and Amazon Trainium 2 — because Nvidia requires tighter power/performance specs. The author argues Samsung's failures mirror Intel's 2016–2021 decline and are unlikely to reverse, given a culture of short-term executive pressure over product quality.

For Micron, the author is cautiously long via deep-OTM, long-dated call options, but is small-sized due to fear of CXMT (Chinese DRAM maker) ramping commodity DDR5/LPDDR5 supply and triggering a conventional memory down-cycle in H1 2025. The HBM trade ratio thesis — that HBM production consuming wafers creates structural DDR5 undersupply — is called "hopium" in the face of CXMT. Samsung Electronics (005930.KS) trades below book value (P/B < 1), which the author frames as the market pricing in terminal failure. Institutions are dared to go long 005930.KS vs. short MU; conversely, long 000660.KS (SK Hynix) vs. short MU is presented as a cleaner pair trade given valuation.

## Photonics Fabs

Tower Semiconductor (TSEM) is preferred over GloFo (GFS) for photonics exposure, primarily because GloFo has a history of forcing wafer-purchase agreements on customers. Both benefit from the co-packaged optics ramp, multi-datacenter training interconnects, and higher-data-rate wireline demand.

## Other Ideas

**Marvell (MRVL)**: The author is skeptical of Marvell's 224G SerDes claims at TSMC N3, questioning whether the IP shown supports LR/KR spec (40 dB insertion loss) needed for Trainium 3 — or only VSR/XSR/C2M. Staying out for now, possibly trading in H2 2025.

**Astera Labs (ALAB)**: Still tempted to short; might go long as a trade if it corrects to $85–100, citing PCIe7/UALink as a potential catalyst.

**Credo (CRDO)**: Holding and possibly adding. The "deepening customer relationships" language is decoded as months of work qualifying LRO (linear receive optics) chips with a major customer. Dangerous given momentum-driven shareholders.

**Coherent (COHR)**: Hyper-bullish on low-phase-noise laser moat for high-modulation-order QAM optical systems. Stock is overbought; wants to buy at ~$80.

**SiTime (SITE)**: Upgraded to high-reward/medium-risk. Engineering thesis: SiTime's 0-PPM system design enables wins in every active electrical/optical cable and any hyperscaler system running slightly out-of-spec standards. Nvidia NVL72 design win and Apple iPhone re-entry rumors cited. Downside risk from momentum funds that don't understand the engineering.

**MediaTek (2454.TW)**: Google TPUv7E (small inference) design win confirmed. Even with a further 6-month delay, risk/reward is favorable. TPU revenue is margin-accretive as it is datacenter and ARM-royalty-free.
