On October 22nd, 2024, Bloomberg reported that ARM had cancelled Qualcomm's v8 Architectural License Agreement (ALA) — two months before a trial ARM was widely expected to win. [[irrationalanalysis]] likens this to the Chernobyl nuclear disaster: a catastrophic self-inflicted meltdown driven by impatience and hubris.

**Background**: Qualcomm holds an ALA dating back before its Nuvia acquisition. ARM separately moved to cancel the Nuvia ALA. The lawsuit centered on two orthogonal questions: (1) whether Nuvia IP was transferable under Qualcomm's existing ALA, and (2) whether ARM had grounds to cancel Qualcomm's ALA for breach of contract. The author believed ARM was on track to win question #1 at trial.

**Why ARM panicked**: The author's best theory (crediting Ravi_711) is that Oryon v2 — the new CPU core in Qualcomm's Snapdragon X Elite — appears to be a clean-sheet design developed after the Nuvia engineers joined Qualcomm. If true, Oryon v2 IP is theoretically not covered by the Nuvia lawsuit, meaning tens of billions of dollars of smartphone chips will ship at the lower v8 ALA royalty rate (~2–2.5%) rather than the v9 TLA rate (~4–5.5%). ARM seemingly realized winning the trial would be a pyrrhic victory and escalated to the nuclear option: canceling the ALA outright to force question #2.

**Impact on Qualcomm**: Business operations are expected to be unaffected unless ARM obtains an injunction. The author views an injunction as unlikely given the trial is only two months away, but notes it would be devastating if granted (revenue for affected jurisdictions goes to zero). The author initiated a small QCOM call option position before the news broke and faces a likely ~60% drawdown at open.

**Impact on ARM**: The author views this as a historic strategic blunder. Every ARM licensee (except Nvidia and Apple) now has strong motivation to accelerate RISC-V migration. ARM had a near-monopoly in CPU ISA with 98% gross margins — a durable earned monopoly the author believes has been irreparably damaged by this decision.

**RISC-V**: The author notes RISC-V adopted the RVA23 standard on the same day, addressing its fragmentation problem. He calls ARM's self-immolation an "incredible gift" to RISC-V startups and urges them to seize the marketing moment aggressively.

**ARM position**: The author holds a tiny ARM stake (<0.2% of long-only accounts), expressing he will watch what happens to those shares over 10 years, implying long-term structural concern.
