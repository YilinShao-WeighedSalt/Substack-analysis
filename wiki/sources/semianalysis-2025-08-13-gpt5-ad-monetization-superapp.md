[[semianalysis]] argues that GPT-5's most strategically significant feature is not raw model capability but its **query router** — a system that dynamically dispatches user queries to appropriately-sized models based on complexity, tool needs, and commercial intent. This architecture is the foundation for OpenAI's move from subscription revenue toward transaction-based monetization of its 700M+ free users.

## Core Thesis: The Router as Monetization Infrastructure

Unlike prior LLM deployments where every query consumed flat compute, GPT-5's router enables variable cost allocation keyed to query value. A trivial query ("Why is the sky blue?") is routed to a cheap mini-model; a high-intent commercial query ("Find me the best DUI lawyer near me") justifies $50+ of compute, agentic research, and outreach on the user's behalf. This unlocks a take-rate model — OpenAI monetizes through transaction fees rather than CPM advertising, sidestepping the ad-quality problems that plagued Perplexity's early attempts at display monetization.

## Leadership Signal: Fidji Simo

OpenAI hired Fidji Simo as CEO of Applications in May 2024. Her career centered on monetizing Facebook's mobile, video, and gaming surfaces. Her prior role at Instacart, which already deployed agentic checkout, is cited as directly relevant experience. The hire is interpreted as a clear organizational bet on transactional revenue.

## The SuperApp Vision

ChatGPT already ranks as the 5th largest website globally, having surpassed X/Twitter, Reddit, and Wikipedia — and it is among the only major platforms still growing its user base. The authors draw a direct line from this distribution advantage to the potential to collapse the entire consumer purchasing funnel: research → comparison → checkout → fulfillment, all inside a single AI assistant. Agentic partnerships already in place include Stripe, Visa, PayPal (payments), Shopify and Instacart (commerce), Booking.com (travel), Salesforce, Intercom, and Zendesk (enterprise SaaS), and Snapchat (social).

## Competitive Implications

The primary casualty is Google Search. High-intent commercial queries — the ones that fund Google's advertising model — are precisely the queries OpenAI is targeting for agentic capture. If merchants can acquire customers through ChatGPT at lower CAC via take-rate, ad spend migrates. Meta and Amazon face analogous pressure in their respective commerce and advertising surfaces, though the article treats Google as the most direct exposure.

## Remaining Uncertainties

The near-term implementation path likely begins with affiliate fees before evolving into full API-level agentic checkout. The timeline and depth of integration with airlines, telecoms, and major retailers remains speculative. Sam Altman has publicly gestured toward "transaction revenue and flat fees" while distancing the product from intrusive advertising formats.
