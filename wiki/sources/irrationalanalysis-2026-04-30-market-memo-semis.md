[[irrationalanalysis]] covers a wide range of semiconductor and adjacent names in this market memo, framed around a turbulent options trading period and a broader macro concern about the "stupidity war" (US-China trade/tariff conflict).

**Intel (INTC):** The author is extremely bullish. Intel 18A had catastrophic parametric yield problems due to inexperience and poor timing, but a conference paper showing ~30% tightening of skew corners for Intel 18AP suggests the next node will be competitive with TSMC N3P (or at minimum N4). A Google $1B prepayment for IFS capacity appeared in an SEC filing. The thesis: Intel needed to survive until 2027 for 18AP to ramp for external customers — they have made it. Finance analysts are not bullish enough.

**Qualcomm (QCOM):** The author sold all QCOM RSU+ESPP at ~$135 in Sep 2022 and rotated into NVDA at an average ~$13.9/share post-split. Despite grudgingly conceding a potential datacenter CPU opportunity (CPU shortage lowers the bar, QCOM has spare wafer capacity from imploding smartphone market), the author remains bearish. He rejects QCOM's claim that smartphones bottom next quarter, sees persistent headwinds from the memory price spike, macro demand destruction, Apple legal war risk in March 2027, and continued CPU talent drain to startups. Also skeptical of 6G datacenter pitch. The "custom silicon" announcement is guessed (~70% confidence) to be an optical DSP/transceiver chip leveraging Alphawave SerDes on Samsung SF4X, similar to Maxlinear.

**Analog/Power Semis (WOLF, ON, NVTS, BE):** Author is growing more bullish on power semis as they relate to 800V EV architecture and Bloom Energy fuel cells. Holds small positions in Wolfspeed, On Semi, and Navitas. Plans to run LTSpice simulations using public datasheets (including a partial Wolfspeed 10KV datasheet) to decide which positions to size up.

**Semicap (ASML, TER, AXTI/AIXA.AS, 268A.T):** Broadly bullish on niche semicap. Rigaku (268A.T) is a top pick for advanced packaging x-ray inspection and future logic inspection as GAA and backside power proliferate — actively buying. Aixtron (AIXA.AS) is bullish for InP/optics MOCVD equipment — Semianalysis institutional note got the Aixtron thesis right. Teradyne (TER) liked for CPO test (double-sided EIC+PIC simultaneous) but waiting for $280 entry after management guided conservatively. ASML is being studied for DRAM exposure; suspects Intel may be pressuring TSMC with High-NA.

**Optics (LITE, COHR, TSEM):** Hyper-bullish LITE for superior laser noise performance in InP. Bearish COHR — their 6-inch InP process has poor wafer uniformity (input goods), hurting yield despite the process node claim. Tower Semi (TSEM) bullish for SiN PIC process (COUPE competitor); conspiracy theory that Nvidia will migrate significant CPO volume to Tower for technical reasons related to SiN vs Si waveguide loss, Q-factor, and system-level NRZ vs PAM4 tradeoffs.
