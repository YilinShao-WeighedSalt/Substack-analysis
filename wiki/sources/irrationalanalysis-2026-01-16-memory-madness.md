A brief mid-January update from [[irrationalanalysis]], following up on the Dec 21st 2025 "Irrational Ideas" post after a volatile first two weeks of 2026. The central theme is that rising DRAM prices are shifting economic value from fabless SoC vendors (especially Qualcomm) toward memory makers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron), while also compressing smartphone OEM willingness to pay for premium silicon.

**Qualcomm bear case deepens via KeyBanc data.** The author is a self-described Qualcomm perma-bear but calls the KeyBanc slide "worse than my wildest bearish fever dreams." Qualcomm has introduced a binned-down 8G5 (cut 1/3 of GPU cores, cache slashed from 18 MB to 2 MB, modem downgraded from x85 to x80, CPU clock cut from 4.6 GHz to 3.8 GHz) and a similar binned-down 8G6 "lite." KeyBanc claims the 8G6 ASP is up 20% over 8G5, yet many Chinese OEMs are choosing the cheaper 8G6 lite, and Qualcomm is reportedly considering a 5-10% price cut on the 8G6 flagship. The author argues this collapses the premium-content narrative Qualcomm management has been pushing — rising TSMC N2 wafer costs plus customers de-speccing creates gross margin pressure that management has not credibly addressed. The root cause: higher DRAM prices are forcing OEMs to cut SoC spend, transferring value to Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron.

**Micron insider buy as a conviction signal.** Mark Liu — former TSMC co-CEO and current Micron board member — purchased ~$8 million of MU on the open market (23,200 shares at ~$337, Jan 14–15 2026), the largest open-market purchase since at least 2023. The author treats this as a strong cycle-bottom signal given Liu's decades of semiconductor cycle experience. After six months of watching the Micron chart from the sidelines, the author re-entered MU at small size with covered calls for protection, and initiated a similar position in Sandisk (SNDK).

**Portfolio updates.** Author bought both Camtek (CAMT) and Onto Innovation (ONTO), citing ONTO's correlation to the Samsung HBM4 ramp. Plans to buy Semtech (SMTC) once the wash-sale rule period clears (sold MACOM too early). Also initiated a long in Arista (ANET) described as "on a whim."
