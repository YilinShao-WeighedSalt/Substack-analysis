[[globalsemiresearch]] covers Oracle's Q3 FY2026 earnings, calling the result "truly impressive" and the clearest sign yet that Oracle has found the right direction in its cloud and AI transformation.

**Financial highlights.** For the first time in 15 years, Oracle posted 20%+ growth in both organic total revenue and non-GAAP EPS in dollar terms — a milestone the author emphasizes is more meaningful given that Oracle's business is now far larger and more complex than it was in 2011. The company also closed its books just 10 days after quarter-end, faster than most S&P 500 peers, which the author attributes to Oracle Fusion being used internally — a sign of product confidence.

**Cloud application business.** Cloud app revenue grew 11% in constant currency, reaching a $16.1B annualized run rate. All segments contributed: Fusion ERP +14%, SCM +15%, HCM +15%, CX +6%, NetSuite +11%, and industry-vertical SaaS (hotels, construction, retail) +19% combined. Deferred revenue from cloud apps grew 14%, ahead of recognized revenue, suggesting deal sizes are expanding and the business is re-accelerating.

**AI integration in SaaS.** The post pushes back on the "AI will kill SaaS" narrative, arguing that Oracle's experience shows AI enhances existing applications. Oracle has embedded over 1,000 AI agents into its products — described as practical tools rather than demos — and the strong SaaS growth is cited as evidence that AI is a tailwind, not a disruptor, for the installed base.

**Major transactions.** In January 2026, Oracle closed the acquisition of a 15% equity stake and a board seat in the newly independent TikTok US data company (spun out of ByteDance). The transaction is accounted for under the equity method with no impact to existing technology service revenue; gains will hit non-operating income/loss in Q4 FY2026. In February, Oracle announced plans to raise up to $30B via investment-grade bonds and mandatory convertible preferred stock; the order book was oversubscribed, and the "market-price" equity tranche had not yet been initiated at time of writing.

**Thesis.** The author is clearly bullish: the combination of re-accelerating cloud apps, AI agent embedding, a landmark TikTok deal, and strong capital markets reception all point to Oracle entering a new phase of durable, scaled growth.
