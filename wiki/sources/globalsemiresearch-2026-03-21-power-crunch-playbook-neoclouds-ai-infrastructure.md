[[globalsemiresearch]] examines how acute power constraints in U.S. data center build-out are reshaping the competitive dynamics among hyperscalers, neoclouds, and enterprises — and why neoclouds are positioned to capture a disproportionate share of AI infrastructure demand through 2027.

**The core thesis** is that electricity shortages have created a 2-3 year structural window for neoclouds to gain ground on hyperscalers. Power scarcity is the binding constraint on new data center capacity, and hyperscalers are disadvantaged because their large, purpose-built campuses require long lead-time grid connections that are now severely backlogged. Neoclouds, by contrast, can exploit **stranded power capacity** by repurposing legacy industrial or telecom facilities that already hold grid interconnects, bypassing the queue that hyperscalers face.

**Three compounding advantages** underpin the neocloud edge in this environment:

1. **Stranded power access** — repurposed facilities (former manufacturing plants, legacy data centers) often carry existing grid connections that are too small for hyperscalers but well-suited to neocloud-scale deployments. Flexible procurement agreements allow neoclouds to lock in capacity on shorter timelines.

2. **Contract model fit** — enterprises experimenting with AI workloads favor short-term, flexible cloud contracts rather than multi-year hyperscaler commitments. Neoclouds' default operating model — shorter contracts, lower lock-in — maps well onto enterprises' willingness-to-pay during the exploration phase of AI adoption.

3. **Improving security posture** — historically a weak point for neoclouds vs. hyperscalers, security capabilities at leading neoclouds have matured to a level that is now competitive in key enterprise verticals, removing what had been a structural objection to neocloud adoption.

**The window is time-limited.** Hyperscalers have been making substantial long-term power infrastructure investments — new substations, dedicated transmission lines, nuclear and gas-peaker offtake agreements — that are expected to come online in 2027-2028. Once that capacity lands, the power disadvantage reverses and hyperscalers' cost scale and breadth of services will likely reassert market share dominance. The neocloud opportunity is therefore front-loaded: the next 2-3 years are the period to establish durable customer relationships and differentiate through software services (orchestration, observability, fine-tuning tooling) before the power dynamics rebalance.
