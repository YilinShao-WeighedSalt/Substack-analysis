A rapid 15-minute stream-of-consciousness note from [[irrationalanalysis]] covering Oracle and Synopsys earnings, with a detour into SoftBank/ARM's related-party ecosystem and Intel Foundry's contract collapse.

**Oracle / Stargate CapEx wave**: Oracle massively beat on RPO (remaining performance obligations), signaling huge booked contracts and imminent CapEx acceleration. The author flags Bloom Energy, Broadcom, Nvidia, and Vertiv as beneficiaries of this spend. The sleeper call, however, is ARM. SoftBank is a major Stargate investor and therefore has direct influence over where CapEx dollars flow. Oracle and Larry Ellison were large Ampere Computing shareholders before Masa/SoftBank bailed them out; Ampere pays ARM high v9 royalty rates; and SoftBank (not ARM the listed entity) is now acquiring Ampere. ARM has extremely low float and Masayoshi Son refuses to sell his stake, almost certainly holding massive loans collateralized against the shares. The author's prior dismissal of the ARM/SoftBank/Izanagi related-party complex — which he called "Graphcore (LOL)" — has softened: he now says "hop on Masa's rocket-powered merry-go-round" while warning the bubble will eventually pop and ARM could drop 50% in a single day.

**Synopsys / Intel Foundry collapse**: Synopsys fell 20% after-hours on earnings, then another 10% the following morning. The author had been asked by a friend whether to buy the dip; he warned of another ~20% leg down. The hit stems from Intel Foundry. It was widely understood that both Intel Foundry and Samsung Foundry paid massive NRE contracts to EDA majors Synopsys and Cadence. With 18A third-party activity essentially dead (all interest has shifted to 18AP) and analog IP taped out on 18A incompatible with 18AP customer designs, the IP contract between Intel Foundry and Synopsys appears to have been cancelled or severely scaled back. The author paraphrases Lip-Bu Tan's implicit message: "we're going bankrupt and not paying you to port IP to a node nobody is going to use." Despite being massively long Intel via both shares and "degen options," the author explicitly does not extend that bullishness to EDA majors' IP departments.
