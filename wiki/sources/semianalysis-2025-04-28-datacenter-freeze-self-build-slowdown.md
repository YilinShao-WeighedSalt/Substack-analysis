[[semianalysis]] corrects widespread market misreadings of Microsoft's datacenter pullback, arguing that the headline "2GW of lease cancellations" conflates non-binding letters of intent (LOIs) with firm contracts — and that the real story is a deliberate 1.5GW near-term self-build slowdown, not a wholesale retreat from AI infrastructure.

**The LOI vs. contract distinction.** The widely cited "2GW cancellation" figure represents abandoned non-binding LOIs spread across multiple quarters — not terminated binding leases. Microsoft retains approximately 5GW of pre-leased capacity under firm contracts with delivery dates spanning 2025–2028. SemiAnalysis frames the LOI walk-backs as options Microsoft never exercised, not commitments it broke.

**The 1.5GW self-build freeze.** Microsoft has paused roughly 1.5GW of near-term self-build datacenter projects originally scheduled for 2025–2026. On-the-ground evidence includes construction shells frozen mid-build, halted orders for cooling and electrical equipment, and slowed development pacing across multiple US campuses that had already secured power interconnects and permits. Despite securing energy approvals, these sites show "underwhelming construction progress."

**Scale of prior dominance.** To contextualize the slowdown, SemiAnalysis documents the scale of Microsoft's prior buildout: the company represented more than 60% of all new leased turnkey capacity from Q1 2023 through H1 2024. By June 2024, Microsoft's pre-leased pipeline exceeded the combined total of Meta, Google, Amazon, and Oracle. The pause is significant precisely because the baseline was so elevated.

**Leasing halt.** In addition to the self-build freeze, Microsoft stopped all new lease signings after its 2023–2024 acquisition spree. The lease slowdown does not become material to revenue at colocation operators until 2027 and beyond, when the current binding contracts roll off.

**Vertiv orderbook timing.** A key analytical error flagged for Wall Street: much of Microsoft's 5GW pre-leased portfolio has not yet translated into Vertiv equipment orders. The binding lease commitments do not automatically populate VRT's orderbook on signing; procurement lags by 12–24 months as construction phases are activated. Analysts modeling Vertiv off headline lease cancellations are double-counting the negative signal and ignoring the latent demand from already-contracted capacity.

**Implication.** Microsoft's pause reflects demand recalibration rather than existential pullback — the company is digesting prior over-commitment while OpenAI and Azure workloads ramp. The 5GW under binding contract provides a multi-year demand floor for datacenter operators and equipment suppliers alike.

## Calls

- [[MSFT]] — NEUTRAL — n/a — pausing 1.5GW of self-build and halting new leases, but holds 5GW in binding contracts through 2028; slowdown is recalibration, not retreat
- [[VRT]] — MENTION — n/a — analyst forecasts misread lease cancellations as orderbook hits; latent demand from 5GW pre-leased pipeline not yet in Vertiv's orderbook, creating potential upside vs. consensus
