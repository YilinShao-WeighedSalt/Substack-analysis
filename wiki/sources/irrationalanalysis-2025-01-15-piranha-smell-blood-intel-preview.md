[[irrationalanalysis]] challenges a leading investment bank's CPU market share model (updated January 10, 2025), arguing it systematically underestimates Intel's losses and understates share gains by competitors across desktop, laptop, and datacenter segments through 4Q2026.

**Desktop:** The bank models non-AMD, non-Intel share as flat — but this ignores Apple's continued growth plus nascent contributions from Nvidia and Qualcomm, both ramping from zero. Even holding AMD flat, Apple + Nvidia + Qualcomm alone should capture an incremental 2–5% share by 4Q2026. The model showing Intel regaining share over the next two quarters is dismissed as implausible given product quality differentials.

**Laptop:** Intel share is again modeled flat from 3Q2024 through 4Q2026. The author sees this as a fantasy: Qualcomm's X Elite laptops, while not exceptional, are gaining traction from a 0.2% base toward mid-single digits (with upside on good execution). Apple continues taking share — the author notes they would have switched already if not for dependency on Windows software. Nvidia is entering with strong brand equity and functional GPU drivers, expected to ramp faster than Qualcomm. Intel's flat share assumption is unjustifiable.

**Datacenter (server CPU):** Intel market share loss is modeled as too slow. Hyperscaler ARM CSS chiplet solutions (the "Other" category) are growing far too slowly in the bank's model. AMD's Turin generation is described as dramatically superior. Nvidia is expected to gain mid-single digit merchant silicon share over time. As evidence of Intel's talent exodus and competitive deterioration, the former Chief Architect of Intel Xeon was just hired by Qualcomm's third datacenter CPU design team — a group with two prior disbanded teams under the same CEO.

**Governance critique:** The author criticizes Intel's board chair (Yeary) and calls for bringing Gelsinger back as chairman while installing a new CEO with better execution. The IDM 2.0 strategy is defended as correct; execution is the failure.

## Calls

- [[INTC]] — SHORT — px@n/a — losing desktop, laptop, and datacenter CPU share faster than sell-side models; talent exodus accelerating
- [[AMD]] — LONG — px@n/a — Turin-driven datacenter share gains and desktop wins undermodeled by the Street
- [[NVDA]] — LONG — px@n/a — entering laptop and datacenter CPU markets with brand power and driver quality advantages
- [[QCOM]] — MENTION — px@n/a — gaining laptop share but serial datacenter CPU failures under current CEO raise execution doubts
- [[AAPL]] — LONG — px@n/a — continuing to take desktop and laptop share as Windows quality deteriorates
