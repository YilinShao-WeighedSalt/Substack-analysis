[[irrationalanalysis]] covers a packed earnings week across semiconductors and optics, with standout moves from SiTime and AMD, a stumble from ARM, and continued skepticism on Coherent and Coreweave.

**SiTime (SITM):** The headline call of the week. The author holds ~$104K in long-only accounts at 398% P&L and believes SiTime has finally killed Quartz timing crystals. New parts released ~4 months ago dropped jitter from 130+ fs to ~60 fs, closing the remaining gap against high-quality quartz (~35 fs). The key differentiator is PPM sync via an integrated fractional PLL — SiTime can precisely correct intrinsic frequency offset in ways quartz cannot replicate. This capability directly improves SerDes BER at system level. Gross margins expected to expand significantly. Bull thesis: Quartz is structurally dead as a competing technology.

**AMD:** Strong earnings driven by the CPU business. Author regrets not buying calls pre-earnings. AMD's chiplet strategy enables it to re-ramp older N5 node CPUs at high yield and widening margins; even "outdated" EPYC x86 beats ARM Neoverse (the chips ARM designs itself) on performance and performance/$. With a shortage in place, competitive dynamics are moot — AMD simply prints and ships.

**ARM:** Stock had been running on sympathy with Intel and broader CPU momentum, but ARM does not have its own fabs and only recently began taping out proprietary designs. The earnings call revealed insufficient supply. Author views this as an obvious and avoidable miss; frames it as AMD winning the actual supply/yield game while ARM has no manufacturing leverage.

**Coreweave (CRWV):** Author held shares as a trade based on a hedge fund thesis centered on neocloud leverage. Exited before the conference call after a bad feeling. Post-print, management gave contradictory answers on whether higher component costs were passed through to customers. Author considers the stock "platinum dogshit" and does not hold a structural view.

**GlobalFoundries (GFS):** Covered via Investor Day. Author is deeply skeptical — no meaningful technical disclosures, word-salad slides, and no S21 data for ring modulators on the new "SCALE" platform. GloFo's prior monolithic fusion node appears to have been abandoned (Ayar Labs and Lightmatter both fled to TSMC COUPE). Author believes Tower Semi and TSMC hold superior CPO Silicon Photonics platforms via hybrid bonding.

**On Semi (ON):** Interested in vertical GaN but disclosures are generic. Treo analog parts look more like a fab-capacity-utilization play than a technical step-change. Positive on management execution (Hassane).

**Navitas (NVTS):** Author broadly agrees with CEO's framing that GaN and SiC are complementary; pushes back on the idea that customers cannot mix-and-match suppliers across topologies.

**Wolfspeed:** Covered briefly — noted as recently un-bankrupt with negative gross margins. Watching their TOLT packaging for AI/datacenter power semis.

**AAOI:** Skeptical. CEO widely disliked in the industry; stock described as a "hedge fund knife fight." AAOI declined to share linewidth and RIN specs for its new high-power laser. Plans to move to 6-inch wafers only by end of 2027. Author notes AAOI is not differentiated on custom automated equipment relative to Chinese competitors.

**Lumentum (LITE):** Highest-conviction long. Has publicly disclosed linewidth and RIN data for ultra-high-power CPO lasers — no competitor does. Greensboro fab described as a structural moat. Lumentum is reportedly reducing cavity length to boost capacity, indicating it is so far ahead it can afford to push the manufacturing envelope. ELSFP opportunity also flagged as a large high-margin revenue stream (requires only CW lasers + SiPho, no DSP or TIA/driver).

**Coherent (COHR):** Skeptical. Believes 6-inch InP ramp is struggling, particularly for high-value devices (100 mW+ CW, 200G EML). Industry joke that Coherent is "only yielding photodiodes" on 6-inch noted. Gross margin disclosure showing SiPho and EML at parity is flagged as suspicious — EML should carry better margins in a capacity-constrained InP environment. VCSEL at 200G per lane declared flatly impossible on reliability grounds (GR-468).

**Fabrinet (FN):** Call described as boring; no substantive commentary.
