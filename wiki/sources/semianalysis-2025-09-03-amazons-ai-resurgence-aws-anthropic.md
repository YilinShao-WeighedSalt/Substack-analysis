[[semianalysis]] argues that AWS is entering a structural AI resurgence, driven by a deepening partnership with Anthropic and a multi-gigawatt buildout of Trainium2-based infrastructure — reversing AWS's relative underperformance versus Azure and Google Cloud in the GPU/XPU transition era.

**AWS's underperformance and the turning point**: AWS historically dominated cloud but stumbled in the CPU-to-GPU transition. Azure pulled ahead on quarterly cloud revenue growth; Google Cloud narrowed the gap. The inflection point is Amazon's cumulative ~$8.25B investment in Anthropic (initially $1.25B expandable to $4B in September 2023, plus $4B in November 2024), which has made Anthropic AWS's most consequential AI tenant.

**Anthropic's explosive growth**: Anthropic grew annualized revenue from roughly $1B to $5B in 2025, achieving "clear outperformance" in the GenAI market. However, Anthropic's cloud infrastructure spending is still approximately 2x smaller than OpenAI's (which spends >$10B annually, all on Azure), and a meaningful portion of Anthropic's workloads still runs on Google Cloud — a legacy from its earlier relationship. AWS has not yet fully captured the Anthropic revenue tailwind.

**The Trainium2 buildout**: AWS has over 1.3 GW of IT datacenter capacity in final construction stages dedicated to Anthropic training workloads. The largest campus alone holds nearly one million Trainium2 chips. Additional gigawatt-scale campuses are breaking ground. The scale is comparable to hyperscaler GPU deployments for first-party AI labs, but backed by largely unproven custom silicon.

**Trainium2 competitive position**: Nvidia GB200 holds a 3.85x FP16 FLOP advantage (2,500 vs. 667 TFLOP/s) and a 2.75x memory bandwidth advantage (8,000 vs. 2,900 GB/s). Despite this, Trainium2's edge is cost-efficiency — superior memory bandwidth per total cost of ownership (TCO) — making it attractive for Anthropic's reinforcement learning roadmap. Anthropic's involvement in Trainium chip design constitutes a hardware-software co-design partnership analogous to Google DeepMind's role in TPU development.

**Roadmap**: New Teton PDS and Teton Max systems launch in 2026. NeuronLink v3 introduces all-to-all scale-up networking converging toward Nvidia's NVLink architecture. Yield issues during assembly remain a near-term risk; meaningful revenue contribution is not expected until late 2025.

**AWS profit context**: AWS contributes roughly 60% of Amazon's group operating profits, making this infrastructure bet central to Amazon's earnings trajectory. A successful ramp of Anthropic workloads onto AWS would be a multi-billion-dollar revenue catalyst.

**Competitive framing**: The post implicitly frames this as a three-way race — Amazon (Trainium + Anthropic), Microsoft (Azure + OpenAI), and Google (TPUs + Gemini/DeepMind) — where each hyperscaler is vertically integrating a frontier AI lab onto custom silicon. Amazon is the latest mover but has the most capital committed per training partner.
