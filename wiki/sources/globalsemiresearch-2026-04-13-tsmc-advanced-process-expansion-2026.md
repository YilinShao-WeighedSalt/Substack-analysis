[[globalsemiresearch]] provides an in-depth analysis of TSMC's capital expenditure strategy and advanced process capacity layout for 2026-2027, framing it as one of the most aggressive investment programs in semiconductor industry history.

The headline figure is TSMC's 2026 capex guidance of $60 billion in the baseline scenario, with a potential upper bound of $110 billion across 2026-2027 combined if demand continues to outpace supply. The post frames this investment intensity as historically exceptional — unprecedented in scale and speed relative to prior semiconductor cycles.

The core demand driver is the explosive growth of AI chips. The post identifies multiple demand vectors simultaneously in play: data center GPUs, edge device NPUs, large-scale language model training infrastructure, and the emerging AI PC segment. This broad-based demand across the AI stack is presented as qualitatively different from prior demand cycles, which were tied to more discrete product transitions such as smartphone adoption.

A key structural argument is TSMC's de facto monopoly in advanced process technology. Because no credible competitor exists at the leading edge (2nm and below), TSMC can pursue aggressive capacity expansion without the typical risk of building into a competitive oversupply scenario. This monopoly position gives management high confidence in the capital commitments.

The post dedicates significant attention to EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet) lithography equipment as the critical bottleneck and planning variable. Detailed tables (paywalled) break down EUV equipment procurement by year and by FAB class, linking specific tool orders to projected wafer capacity additions. EUV tool counts serve as a forward indicator of how quickly TSMC's N2 and subsequent nodes will reach volume production.

The overall thesis is bullish on TSMC's long-term competitive position: dominant technology, captive AI-driven demand, and willingness to invest at scale before competitors can respond.
