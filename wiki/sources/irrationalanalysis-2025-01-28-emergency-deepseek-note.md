[[irrationalanalysis]] published this emergency note on January 28, 2025, in the wake of the DeepSeek-driven market selloff that obliterated AI-related semiconductor and hyperscaler stocks in a 3-4 sigma intraday event. The author, running 2.2-2.5x leverage, survived only by aggressively selling in the Sunday overnight and premarket sessions; without that action, the account would have been force-liquidated with $170-200K in losses.

The post's central argument is that the market's conclusion — that DeepSeek signals the end of the AI infrastructure trade — is "extremely wrong." Instead, the author contends that cheaper, more efficient AI models will accelerate datacenter buildout with particular torque for energy, inference, and memory. The logic: DeepSeek running locally on an RTX 4090 is entirely memory-capacity and memory-bandwidth bound, not compute bound. The GPU barely stresses; the bottleneck is DRAM. This makes memory the structural winner of the DeepSeek "vibe shift."

The author frames the broader AI trade not around consumer chatbots or revenue, but around labor replacement and OPEX dilution — the real driver of corporate AI investment. Zuckerberg is cited as someone increasingly saying this "quiet part out loud": replacing employees with AI agents at a fraction of the cost will dramatically expand META's EPS.

On the selloff mechanics: today's carnage was attributed primarily to margin calls from over-levered funds and pods, not fundamental reassessment. ALAB (Astera Labs) was specifically called out as a victim — Gavin Baker's largest position — hit hard because its PCIe switch business is directly levered to Amazon CapEx and GB200 ramp. The author implies the valuation was always dependent on that specific capex cycle continuing.

Bullish trades executed on the day:
- Added to TSM: tariff-insensitive because TSMC can pass costs through to customers via price increases.
- Tactical long MSFT into earnings: Satya Nadella commentary on CapEx "reduction" could be a positive catalyst; shares described as "4x leverageable and safe-ish."
- Doubled down on MU call options: memory is the biggest beneficiary of the DeepSeek efficiency shift; described as "extremely bullish" with heavy risk warning.
- Held every share of NVDA: GB300 NVL72 expected to dominate reasoning model inference; sold all call options but kept equity.
