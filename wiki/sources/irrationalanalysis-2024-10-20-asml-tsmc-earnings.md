Written in October 2024 after ASML's earnings miss and TSMC's strong beat, [[irrationalanalysis]] uses both reports as a lens to survey the entire semiconductor landscape sector by sector.

**ASML miss:** The earnings disappointment is attributed primarily to Samsung Foundry and Intel Foundry delaying fabs, not weakness in auto/PC as management implied. China export control headwinds were already baked in. The author treats the "two dynamics" narrative as a dodge.

**TSMC dominance:** TSMC posted a strong quarter driven by N3-family ramp and surging HPC/AI logic demand. The author attributes TSMC's durable edge to disciplined engineering and superior PDK quality — foundry competitors fail largely because of cultural problems that produce systematically misleading PDK documentation, which collapses parametric yield. Samsung Foundry is on its fourth attempt at a 3nm-class node; detailed internal yield leaks point to deliberate misrepresentation of process corners.

**Semicap headwinds:** Despite TSMC raising CapEx guidance, the author is cautious on semicap. TSMC holds strong negotiating leverage and is expected to demand price cuts on high-NA EUV equipment and service contracts. Intel and Samsung Foundry spending is structurally impaired. A further 10–30% decline in major semicap names over the next six months is plausible.

**Memory:** Only one thing matters — whether Samsung fixes HBM yield. Until then, SK Hynix and Micron (MU) are the beneficiaries. Samsung's HBM3E qualification failures with Nvidia mirror the same PDK/yield culture problems as logic foundry.

**AI logic:** Bullish indefinitely. Nvidia is a good investment through H2 2026, after which hyperscaler custom silicon may take inference share. MRVL is a short-term tactical long on Trainium 2 ramp. ALAB is a short — PCIe retimer revenue will plunge with Blackwell, and Amazon warrant distortions inflate apparent margins.

**Smartphone:** The author covered a QCOM short at a loss after PRC stimulus and Samsung killing Exynos 2500 revived the end-market outlook.

**Other long-term ideas:** GEV benefits from AI data center power grid strain. KEYS is a low-risk screaming buy. CIEN is the author's top current trade ahead of an upcoming optics post.
