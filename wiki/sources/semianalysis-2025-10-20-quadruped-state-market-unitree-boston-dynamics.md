[[semianalysis]] provides a comprehensive survey of the quadruped robotics market, mapping hardware vendors, software ecosystems, and near-term commercial applications across a fast-moving competitive landscape.

**Market structure**: Unitree (China) commands roughly 70% of global quadruped sales by volume, producing approximately 200 Go2 units per day at peak. Boston Dynamics leads in premium/enterprise deployments in the West, while ANYbotics and DEEP Robotics occupy focused niches. The market is bifurcating: low-cost Chinese platforms dominate research and price-sensitive verticals, while Western OEMs compete on compliance, safety certifications, and ecosystem maturity.

**Why quadrupeds win**: Relative to drones, wheeled, and tracked alternatives, legged robots navigate cluttered and narrow industrial spaces, cross ~30 cm gaps, carry meaningful payloads for hours (vs. 30-45 minute drone flights), and provide flexible multi-sensor mounting — making them the platform of choice for automated inspection.

**Unitree cost moat**: Three structural advantages drive Unitree's price leadership. First, quasi-direct-drive (QDD) actuators with low gear ratios (~10:1) cut friction, cost, and enable backdrivability; actuators represent 50-70% of BOM. Second, domestic LiDAR suppliers (Robosense, LIVOX) price at a few hundred dollars versus ~$2,000 for Western equivalents. Third, the A2 achieves 5+ hours runtime (3+ hours at 25 kg payload) — roughly twice Western competitors — reducing total cost of ownership.

**Emerging software ecosystem**: The application stack is fragmenting into model vendors (FieldAI, Skild AI providing autonomy stacks), system integrators (Chironix, IntuitiveRobots, SUPCON), and orchestration/fleet-management layers (Lattice, Formant). Western OEMs benefit from mature APIs today, but this advantage narrows as third-party tools become platform-agnostic.

**Five-year opportunity verticals**: Datacenter inspection (downtime ~$1M/day creates ROI urgency), last-mile campus delivery (~$2.50/delivery vs. ~$9 human), and security patrol (replacing $250K-$450K/year human guards with ~$10K/month RaaS) are the near-term wedges.

**Security overhang on Unitree**: NERC compliance requirements restrict Unitree from U.S. utilities; semiconductor fabs resist Chinese hardware. This creates a durable Western demand channel for Boston Dynamics and ANYbotics in regulated or data-sensitive environments despite their higher cost.
