This memo covers two major themes: Intel's 18A process node collapse and an aggressive Apple modem ramp trade. [[irrationalanalysis]] writes from a position deeply invested in semiconductors.

**Intel 18A: Parametric Yield Disaster**

The author has been warning for over a year that Intel's 18A node suffers from a fundamentally broken PDK, resulting in catastrophic parametric yield — not merely defect density (D0) problems as Intel bulls claim. The memo takes this as validated by two sequential delays: Clearwater Forest (server) already slipped, and now Panther Lake (client PC) is delayed, missing the critical holiday shopping season. The author dismisses Intel Foundry Services (IFS) as "omega fucked."

The Trump administration's decision to route US semiconductor investment through TSMC rather than Intel further cements 18A's irrelevance. TSMC announced a $100B US investment package including a new R&D fab and advanced packaging facility (on top of the previously announced $65B commitment). The author views this as a decisive win for TSMC management, who avoided the operational disaster of taking over Intel's fabs configured for incompatible nodes.

The memo calls for firing Intel's entire board and rehiring Pat Gelsinger, mocking Chairman Frank Yeary's ability to "unlock shareholder value."

**SiTime iPhone Content Confirmed**

SiTime's oscillator content in the latest iPhone was independently identified by teardown analyst FomalHaut and subsequently confirmed by SiTime's CEO at the Morgan Stanley TMT conference. The author had previously found the quartz crystal but missed the clockgen marking. SITM's UCIe (chiplet interconnect) clock-forwarding advantage is highlighted as a unique datacenter performance differentiator beyond mobile.

**Apple Modem Aggressive Ramp — Macro-Neutral Trade**

The author expresses strong conviction that Apple will ramp its internal C1 modem much more aggressively in the iPhone 17 cycle (September 2025), potentially reaching 80% internal modem share — a scenario not priced in by sell-side. The proposed macro-neutral trade: long CEVA, SITM, QRVO, SWKS (Apple modem ecosystem beneficiaries) vs. short QCOM (Qualcomm has no offsetting revenue to absorb Apple share loss beyond the ~20% already telegraphed). The author systematically dismisses Qualcomm's C1 modem marketing comparisons (sub-6 bandwidth, CA, mmWave) as cope, arguing real-world network constraints make these spec differences invisible to 98%+ of users.
