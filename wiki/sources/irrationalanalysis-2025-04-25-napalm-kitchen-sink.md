[[irrationalanalysis]] delivers a scathing post-earnings breakdown of Intel's Q1 FY25 results, framing the quarter as a "napalm kitchen sink" moment under newly installed CEO Lip-Bu Tan (dubbed the "Napalm Dragon"). The author holds INTC shares and argues there is speculative hope in Lip-Bu's aggressive restructuring despite the company's dire operational state.

**Foundry and 18A Process:** The central technical concern is Intel Foundry's 18A node. The author, citing firsthand semiconductor industry knowledge, asserts that 18A parametric yield is critically poor. External customers are staying in test/R&D mode and waiting for the improved 18AP node, expected around 2027. Intel Foundry is described as financially self-sufficient for 12–18 months, buying time but nothing more. The author floats a theory that Clearwater Forest (server CPU) has been intentionally delayed because Panther Lake consumes all available 18A capacity, given how bad yields are — dismissing Intel's stated packaging excuse.

**Intel 7 Capacity Anomaly:** A major red flag flagged in the post: Intel wrote off the bulk of Intel 7 factory equipment just two quarters prior (booking a ~$17B loss), with CFO David Zinsner characterizing that capacity as worthless. Yet Q1 management now says Intel 7 is capacity-constrained. The author finds this contradiction deeply suspicious and signals ongoing investigation.

**Client CPU Competition and ASP Pressure:** Sell-side analyst Stacy Rasgon's question is highlighted: older Intel products are outselling newer Lunar/Meteor Lake. The author floats several explanations — AMD competitive pressure destroying ASP headroom, share losses to AMD in premium and enterprise segments (including AMD's new vPro competitor), TSMC price hikes making Lunar Lake economically unviable, and MediaTek/Nvidia client PC share gains materializing in H2.

**Management and Culture Reform:** Lip-Bu Tan's shareholder letter is quoted approvingly. The author celebrates Tan's war on "empire builders" — a middle-management class (Director through VP) that grows headcount without producing engineering or business value. Intel is contrasted with well-run peers (Nvidia, Broadcom, TSMC) where such behavior is controlled. Tan's OKR reset and cultural overhaul is seen as necessary and overdue.

**Mount Evans / Google:** The author briefly notes that the Mount Evans (Google TPU-adjacent) ASIC was reportedly a disaster due to upper management failures, with Google dissatisfied by the C5 revision process.
