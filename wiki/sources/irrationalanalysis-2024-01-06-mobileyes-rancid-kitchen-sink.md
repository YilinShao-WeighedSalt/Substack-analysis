On January 4, 2024, Mobileye ([[MBLY]]) issued a pre-announcement ahead of its January 25 earnings call, disclosing a severe revenue miss for FY2024 driven by excess inventory at Tier 1 automotive customers. [[irrationalanalysis]] dissects the press release with pointed skepticism about both the numbers and management's credibility.

**The inventory problem.** Mobileye revealed 6–7 million units of excess EyeQ SoC inventory sitting at customers, accumulated during 2021–2022 supply-chain panic-buying and compounded by weaker-than-expected OEM production in 2023. The author suspects China macro deterioration as a key driver. As a result, Q1 2024 revenue is guided down ~50% year-over-year — an extraordinary contraction for a supposed growth company — with FY2024 revenue guided at $1.83–$1.96B vs. ~$2.08B in 2023 (implying roughly 37M → 31–33M EyeQ unit shipments).

**CFO kitchen-sinking.** The author flags a suspicious accounting pattern: Q4 2023 operating income came in ~50% above expectations while revenue was roughly in line with guidance. This divergence is a classic "kitchen sink" — CFOs shuffling revenue recognition from the current quarter into a future one to make the current quarter look better while sandbaging the next. The author calls this "CFO shenanigans" explicitly.

**Contradictory guidance.** Management claimed the inventory issue would be largely resolved after Q1 2024, with Q2–Q4 2024 expected "roughly flat to up mid single-digits" versus prior-year periods. However, the author notes that simple arithmetic from the FY2024 guidance range implies multiple quarters of YoY revenue contraction — a direct contradiction that management never addressed.

**Broader auto semi read-through.** The author distinguishes MBLY's situation from the broader automotive semiconductor industry. ON Semiconductor ([[ON]]), STMicroelectronics ([[STM]]), and NXP Semiconductors ([[NXPI]]) are described as "sick with the flu" — suffering cyclical pressure but manageable. MBLY is characterized as "having a seizure on the floor... muttering strange/contradictory statements." The author liquidated a small ON Semi position and a SiTime ([[SITS]]) position in response and signaled intent to stay out of auto semis until H2 2024, then look at MEMS and power semiconductors.
