# The AI Trade Just Shifted From Buildout to Monetization

Global Semi's read on the same Meta-cloud news (mostly paywalled after preview). Opposite framing to SemiAnalysis's rebuttal: Meta offering to sell excess compute reads, **to a nervous market, as an admission that capacity may be outrunning demand** — the shift from a "buildout" phase (rewarding companies for securing GPUs/power/data centers on the scarcity thesis) to a "monetization" phase where that assumption is tested.

Market reaction cited:
- Meta **+~10%**; Micron **−~10%** in US.
- Next day Asia: **SK Hynix −14.6%** intraday, **Samsung −9%** (briefly halted Kospi trading); both recouped part Friday (Samsung +3%, Hynix up) as analysts called it overdone.

Nuance: Meta is **not really trying to become AWS** — big gap between renting excess compute and building a real cloud platform. But the *signal* (most aggressive buyer has spare cycles) matters more than the strategy for near-term sentiment. Note: this is a tonal shift toward caution for a publication that has been a consistent 2026 memory bull.
