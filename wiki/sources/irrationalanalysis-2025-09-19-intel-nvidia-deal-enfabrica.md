[[irrationalanalysis]] shares a rapid-fire stream-of-consciousness reaction to two surprise semiconductor deals announced around September 19, 2025.

**Intel trade recap.** The author went long INTC via stock and options after a "Trump deal" on the thesis that political pressure would coerce customers onto Intel 18A process (18AP), generate news-cycle momentum, and that near-book-value pricing capped downside at roughly 20%. The trade worked. INTC remains the author's third-largest position in the trading account after AVGO and TSEM; some trimming occurred to de-lever and cover pre-paid taxes. The position is held into year-end on the expectation of additional announcements — possibilities cited include a Microsoft AI ASIC semi-custom deal, an Amazon NVLink Fusion CPU, or an actual 18AP customer win.

**Intel + Nvidia deal — three components.** (1) Nvidia to use IFS advanced packaging (Foveros) as a second source for CoWoS-L style packaging — viewed as a needed win for IFS, since advanced packaging is the easiest customer entry point. (2) Intel and Nvidia to co-develop an x86 NVLink Fusion CPU for future AI racks (possibly Rubin Ultra) — rated a "HUGE win." The rationale: Nvidia Grace (ARM Neoverse V2-based) is a serious CPU bottleneck preventing GPU kernels from launching fast enough, earning the moniker "world's most expensive memory controller." The x86 project with Intel is positioned as a hedge against Nvidia's in-house Vera custom CPU cores, given the historically poor track record of Nvidia's Denver/Carmel ARM CPU designs. (3) Nvidia graphics chiplet on Intel CPU for laptops — the author is largely dismissive; views it as a deal sweetener, a way for Lip-Bu Tan to eventually wind down Intel's standalone graphics division, and a path to potentially dominant laptop silicon via hybrid bonding. Nvidia took a 4.9% stake in Intel, paid for in cash (unlike the Trump equity conversion).

**Nvidia + Enfabrica acquisition.** Enfabrica makes a hybrid switch combining Ethernet and PCIe into a "super-NIC-switch." The author is surprised and skeptical of the strategic logic — replacing all NICs with a single super-NIC kills network topology flexibility. The author speculates it might become meaningful in a future "Feynmann" AI rack if the entire system is designed around the concept, but admits uncertainty and solicits reader input.
