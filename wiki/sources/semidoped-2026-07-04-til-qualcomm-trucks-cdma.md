# TIL: Qualcomm Bet the Company on Trucks Before It Bet on Your Phone

Semi Doped, ~600 words. A history essay with a present-day hook.

## Summary
- Founded 1985 by seven ex-Linkabit engineers (Irwin Jacobs, Andrew Viterbi); name = "Quality Communications."
- Early revenue came from **OmniTRACS** — satellite terminals on truck cabs for fleet tracking. In 1989 one customer (**Schneider National**) was **half** of Qualcomm's $32M revenue. That trucking cash funded the risky bet.
- **CDMA** (Code Division Multiple Access): let all callers transmit at once, each wrapped in a unique code, untangled at the receiver. Industry (committed to rival TDMA) called it unworkable in 1989; WSJ warned of billions in losses ("Holy Wars of Wireless").
- FCC let carriers pick their standard; Qualcomm sold CDMA straight to operators. Became a standard in 1993, underpinned 3G; Qualcomm's patents/modems rode into 4G/5G.
- **Present-day hook:** as [[QCOM]] "reforms itself into an AI datacenter company," could its **HBC (High Bandwidth Compute)** — DRAM stacked on a logic die, avoiding the standard HBM+CoWoS route (from its 2026 Investor Day) — be the next impossible-sounding bet that pays off?
