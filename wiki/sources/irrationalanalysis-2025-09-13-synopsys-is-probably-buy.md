[[irrationalanalysis]] argues that the sharp selloff in Synopsys (SNPS) following its earnings miss is an overreaction driven by "SaaS tourists" who misunderstood the semiconductor industry dynamics, and that the stock is a buy on the dip.

## Background: Intel 18A NRE Contract Collapse

The core thesis rests on understanding why SNPS sold off. Porting silicon IP from one foundry process node to another requires substantial engineering OpEx — the author estimates over $7M per analog IP block. Because Samsung Foundry and Intel Foundry lack the large external customer base that TSMC enjoys (TSMC provides complimentary MPW shuttles to IP vendors in exchange for a rich IP catalog), IP companies like Synopsys would never voluntarily port their catalogs to Intel 18A or Samsung's nodes. Consequently, both foundries historically paid large NRE contracts to IP vendors to de-risk the porting work.

Synopsys had publicly committed to porting much of its IP catalog to Intel Foundry 18A and 18AP. The author infers that Intel signed a multi-stage contract worth potentially $100M+ in NRE fees to cover Synopsys's porting costs — work that was already underway when Pat Gelsinger was fired and replaced by Lip-Bu Tan.

## Lip-Bu Tan's Leverage Play

Intel 18A has zero external customers — Intel investor relations has publicly confirmed that focus has shifted to 18AP and 14A for external wins. Porting from 18A to 18AP requires a new tape-out and meaningful design changes (roughly equivalent to porting from TSMC N5 to N4P), meaning the existing 18A IP work has zero commercial value.

Lip-Bu Tan, former CEO of Cadence, holds outsized leverage: Intel Design is Synopsys's largest EDA customer with nearly 100% wallet share, an unusual all-in arrangement. Tan can credibly threaten to shift EDA spend to Cadence. Combined with Intel's precarious financial position (the author bluntly states "Intel is fucking going bankrupt") and the political sensitivity of any litigation against a company with Trump as a ~10% stakeholder, Synopsys had little recourse. The author concludes Tan effectively told Synopsys to sue him — and walked away from the contract.

## Earnings Call Confirms the Story

The author translates Synopsys management's careful corporate language from the earnings call: the IP team is being redirected to nodes "that actually have customers" (TSMC and Samsung), the 18A IP is effectively worthless, and the EDA revenue concentration in Intel is a known risk. Management guided that most of the revenue hit is already baked in and is largely a one-off tied to Intel 18A's failure, with China impact minimal.

## Investment View

The author holds a position in SNPS and frames the selloff as a buying opportunity: the impairment is a one-time event caused by an unprecedented contract cancellation, not a structural deterioration in Synopsys's EDA or IP business. Cadence (CDNS) is mentioned as a potential beneficiary if Intel shifts future EDA contracts, but the author's primary call is long SNPS.
