[[irrationalanalysis]] covers three topics: a critical take on Qualcomm's 2024 Investor Day, a swing-trade thesis around the ARM v. QCOM trial, and a debunking of Cerebras benchmark claims.

**Qualcomm Investor Day — Bearish.** The stock dropped 7% the following day. The author's core critique is that Qualcomm's diversification revenue (PC, automotive, IoT, industrial edge AI) is structurally dilutive to gross margins. Apple modem revenue carries 65%+ gross margins; everything Qualcomm is replacing it with sits at 30-45%. The $4B automotive revenue target by 2029 is dismissed as inflated, with one DB analyst noted as calling out management credibility in Q&A. The PC opportunity is undermined by a poor software ecosystem (no native ARM Discord, broken anti-cheat for games, stale GPU drivers), limited battery-life advantage, and fierce competition from MediaTek (which won the Samsung Galaxy S25 FE socket) and soon Nvidia. The author's personal experience on a Snapdragon X Elite laptop was so poor that they plan to switch to Intel Panther Lake next year. Meta switching AR/VR chip supply from Qualcomm to MediaTek is highlighted as a pivotal signal. The 2029 revenue diversification targets are called "believable" only because handset share losses to Apple, MediaTek, Google, Huawei, and Xiaomi will drag the base down, not because the new verticals are genuinely strong. At no price would the author invest in QCOM.

**MediaTek as the superior play.** 2454.TW (MediaTek) is described as an objectively better version of Qualcomm — outperforming across smartphone content growth, edge AI, and datacenter AI exposure — and is cited as the preferred long for investors seeking those themes.

**ARM v. QCOM Trial Swing Trade.** With the trial starting in mid-December, the author holds a small ARM call option and QCOM put option. The thesis, developed with contributor Ravi_711, is that royalty-rate hikes barely dent QCOM's economics but are hugely accretive to ARM's revenue; settlement is likely on the eve of trial (consistent with how the prior Apple lawsuit resolved), making it a clean event-driven swing trade.

**Cerebras Benchmarks.** Cerebras released Llama 3.1 405B inference benchmarks that the author considers misleading. The number of wafer-scale engines powering the benchmark was undisclosed; the author suspects gross margins on the inference cluster are deeply negative at current utilization.

## Calls

- [[QCOM]] — SHORT — n/a — gross margin destruction from Apple modem loss, dilutive diversification revenue, and accelerating share losses to MediaTek
- [[ARM]] — LONG — n/a — swing trade ahead of December trial; royalty hike is highly accretive to ARM and settlement is likely on the eve of proceedings
- [[2454.TW]] — LONG — n/a — MediaTek is the superior alternative to QCOM for smartphone content growth, edge AI, and datacenter AI exposure
