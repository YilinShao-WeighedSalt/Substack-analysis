# Tungsten Hexafluoride (WF6): The Semiconductor Gas Nobody Can Get Enough Of

Supply-chain deep dive (partly paywalled) on **WF6**, the gas used in CVD to deposit tungsten word-lines/contacts inside every advanced memory and logic chip. No substitute at scale (copper + tungsten only; aluminum exited). Prices up **5x since Jan 2026**; supply short by ~a quarter of demand.

Causes: (1) **China export controls** — China controls 80%+ of raw tungsten; aimed mainly at Japan's military industry (Feb 2025 dual-use list + early-2026 Japan-specific restrictions), it starved Japanese WF6 makers of high-purity tungsten powder (Japan's China tungsten imports −50% in April 2026). (2) **Fire** at Kanto Denka's Shibukawa plant (Aug 2025) damaged an NF3 line. (3) Both Japanese majors — **Kanto Denka Kogyo** (~1,200 t/yr) and **Central Glass** (~1,000 t/yr) — notified Samsung/SK Hynix/TSMC that WF6 output ceases **permanently from July 1, 2026**; Mitsui Chemicals already exited Feb 2026. Combined loss ~2,200 t ≈ ~a quarter of ~12,000 t global capacity vs ~11,000+ t 2026 demand.

Killer economics: WF6 is **<1% of a memory chip's cost** but absolutely required — "specialty gas is to a fab what oxygen is to a human." A 70%+ gross-margin HBM fab absorbs any price. New (mostly Chinese) capacity expected to close the gap by ~mid-2027, so it's a *time-limited* monopoly. Related fluorine chemicals (HF acid, fluorocarbons) also up 2-3x. Concentrated investment thesis (Chinese producers) behind paywall.
