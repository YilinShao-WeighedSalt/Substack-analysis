## Summary
Exhaustive deep dive on CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies), China's largest DRAM maker preparing for what could be China's biggest semiconductor IPO. Technology originates from Qimonda patents (7,000 patents, 2.8TB docs licensed 2019). Hefei government funded ~80% of $2.5B Phase 1. Financial trajectory: FY2025 revenue ~$8.6B, Q1 2026 $7.3B (700% YoY), Q1 operating margin ~70%. SemiAnalysis projects 2026 revenue could exceed $50B, but growth is driven by soaring DRAM prices (ASPs +57% QoQ) not market share gains (bit shipments only +11% QoQ). CXMT has <1% HBM revenue with ~25% combined yield on HBM3 8-stack; may skip HBM3 for HBM3E targeting 2027. Capacity trajectory: ~350K wafers/month by end-2026 (vs Micron ~385K), potentially ~500K by 2028 (~17% global DRAM). IPO governance: parent holds ~30% economics but 73–75% voting via state-linked entities. Alibaba Cloud is anchor customer and ~4% shareholder. Reports HBM smuggling via third-country re-export channels. Key conclusion: even CXMT at max expansion cannot break DRAM shortage through 2028; DRAM undersupplied by high-single to low-teens percentages.
