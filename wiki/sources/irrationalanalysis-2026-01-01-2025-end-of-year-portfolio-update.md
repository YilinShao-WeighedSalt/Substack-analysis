[[irrationalanalysis]] reviews the full-year 2025 trading account performance on the last trading day of the year. The author finished +332% on the year, outperforming the benchmark [[SMH]] by 283 percentage points despite being down 47% at the trough on April 22nd. The original goal was roughly +300% over SMH, narrowly missed.

On a risk-adjusted basis, the single best trade of the year was [[TSEM]] (Tower Semiconductor). The author describes it as "free money" and "so obvious" — the thesis being that VCSEL was dead at 200G/lane, that there was a massive EML (electro-absorption modulated laser) shortage, that silicon photonics was booming, and that GlobalFoundries was incompetent. TSEM was uniquely positioned to benefit from the silicon photonics ramp given its specialty process capabilities.

The two worst positions in 2025 were [[BE]] (Bloom Energy) and [[SITM]] (SiTime). Both were characterized as the author's highest-risk, most speculative ("degen") holdings. The author's risk management framework calls for liquidating the most speculative names first when sentiment deteriorates. Both BE and SITM were sold in panic de-leveraging moves during the April drawdown. As of year-end the wash-sale window has not yet cleared; the author intends to buy back into both positions in January.

The author also provides full trading records (stocks and options) in spreadsheet form and monthly brokerage statements for January through November 2025 as transparency disclosures. Long-only accounts are shown separately. The author acknowledges that the 2025 level of outperformance is not repeatable consistently, and sets a more modest 2026 aspiration of outperforming SMH by 30%–100%.

The post closes with a disclosure philosophy: the author accepts no subscription revenue, paywalls nothing, discloses all positions, and refuses all paid arrangements in order to maintain editorial independence. The "pledge" feature on Substack is described as a contingency fund in case the author is fired from the day job (semiconductor engineering).
