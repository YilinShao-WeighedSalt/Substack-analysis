[[irrationalanalysis]] publishes a Q1 2026 consolidated holdings and performance update, substituting a CSV of all year-to-date trading records (stocks and options) for a missing March brokerage statement. Two months of clean PDF statements (January and February) are also attached.

The author frames Q1 as a difficult period: the post is written on the eve of a potential Middle East ground invasion and amid acute uncertainty over U.S. tariff policy ("TACO" — Trump backing off). Either scenario — market crash or lasting peace deal — would, in the author's view, cause a significant re-grossing of the portfolio. The day prior to publication was described as "not fun" and the author acknowledges being "very out of position."

The stated portfolio benchmark is SMH, the VanEck Semiconductor ETF, with a self-imposed performance target of outperforming it by at least 30%. As of the post, that goal is described as barely being met. The author holds SMH 370-strike put options that would profit from a sharp market selloff triggered by a Trump tariff shock; if the market instead rallies on a tariff reversal, those puts expire worthless but the broader portfolio re-grosses.

The author flags a significant tax misstep: a large overpayment in Q4 2025 estimated taxes is still awaiting refund, and no Q1 2026 estimated tax pre-payment was made — explicitly framed as "learning from the best." The portfolio is described as heavily concentrated in the semiconductor industry, consistent with the author's long-running thesis that the sector is the central driver of returns.

A passing reference to Gavin Baker potentially having "bottom-ticked the market" suggests the author was caught leaning bearish or under-invested near the Q1 low, contributing to the underperformance relative to goal.
