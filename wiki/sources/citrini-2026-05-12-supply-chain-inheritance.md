[[citrini]]'s second installment in the Semis Memo series, guided by analysts Zephyr and Jukan, applies the same framework introduced in January 2026: find sectors where estimates still reflect non-AI headwinds and assess whether AI demand can overwhelm them. The issue covers five areas — analog and power semis, CPUs in the agentic era, neoclouds, AI materials bottlenecks, and Korea — and closes with updates on prior ideas.

**Analog and Power Semis: Supply Chain Inheritance.** The highest-conviction section argues that the AI data center capex wave is directly inheriting the supply chain built for the EV and solar buildout — a concept Citrini terms "Post-Traumatic Supply Disorder." Companies like **Texas Instruments (TXN)** and **NXP Semiconductors (NXPI)** accumulated heavy capex during the EV boom, were burned by the subsequent demand collapse, and are now choosing to raise average selling prices (ASPs) rather than flood the market with new supply. Crucially, Nvidia's May 2025 technical blog on 800V DC rack architecture explicitly credits EV and solar industries as the source of that power-delivery technology — meaning the capex that once looked stranded is now exactly the infrastructure AI data centers need. Discrete power semiconductors (MOSFETs, diodes), capacitors, and inductors see the clearest secular uplift. **Murata Manufacturing (6981 JP)**, **Vishay Intertechnology (VSH)**, and **Samsung Electro-Mechanics (009150 KS)** are flagged as early movers as the market begins pricing in an imminent MLCC shortage. The rack-external power semis story is characterized as straightforward: restrained capex intensity + rising AI-driven demand = ASP expansion.

**CPUs in the Agentic Era.** The memo argues that CPUs are seeing renewed demand in an agentic compute paradigm where always-on inference workloads create a different compute profile than the GPU-dominated training era. Specific names and price targets are behind the paywall.

**Neoclouds: The Inference Shortage.** Citrini flags a developing inference capacity crunch among neocloud operators as agentic AI workloads ramp. The framing suggests this is a distinct and underappreciated bottleneck separate from training compute.

**AI Materials Bottlenecks and Korea Unlocked.** These sections address upstream material supply constraints relevant to semiconductor fabrication and a potential re-rating of Korean semiconductor names as AI demand accelerates through their supply chains.
