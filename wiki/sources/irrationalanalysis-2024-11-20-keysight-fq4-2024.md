[[irrationalanalysis]] reviews Keysight Technologies' ([[KEYS]]) fiscal Q4 2024 earnings, characterizing it as an "excellent print" and rating the stock "Low-risk, Medium-reward, High-conviction."

**Gross margin beat driven by high-speed test equipment.** The author highlights a notable gross margin beat attributed to strong demand for Keysight's premium test and measurement hardware — high-end oscilloscopes, BERTs (Bit Error Rate Testers), VNAs/PNAs, network analyzers, and spectrum analyzers — all critical for the ongoing ramps in 224G Ethernet and PCIe Gen 7. These are technically demanding protocols requiring new, high-bandwidth test gear, making the upgrade cycle a structural tailwind rather than a one-time event.

**Competitive moat and pricing power.** The author draws on direct hands-on experience with Keysight products and competitors. Anritsu is dismissed as poor on products and support alike. Rohde & Schwarz is credited with a few competitive products in high-end spectrum analyzers but is rated mediocre in BERTs and oscilloscopes. Keysight's monopoly-like status in multiple categories — earned through sustained R&D investment — gives the company substantial pricing power that competitors cannot easily erode.

**Software as a durable revenue layer.** Keysight's SaaS/software attach model is flagged as a significant and underappreciated part of the thesis. A single high-end box (oscilloscope, spectrum analyzer, or BERT) can generate software/SaaS revenue equivalent to 1–3 full-time engineer salaries. The author notes that while ~2,000 lines of Python could replicate ~80% of a given Keysight software product's functionality, the UI quality, responsiveness, support, and feature richness make the premium justifiable in practice.

**Optical communications and protocol layer upside.** Management noted that an optical communication boom is not yet reflected in results — the author views this as a future catalyst. Additionally, CEO optimism around emulation platforms and protocol layer traction suggests hyperscalers may be developing proprietary protocols, with CXL cited as another potential driver.

**Wireless risk acknowledged but contained.** Wireless is described as stable, and the CEO expressed confidence it cannot deteriorate further — the author treats this as the one real risk, now largely de-risked.

**Overall stance.** The author describes KEYS as "one of the safest positions in my portfolio" and reiterates a strong buy conviction at current levels.
