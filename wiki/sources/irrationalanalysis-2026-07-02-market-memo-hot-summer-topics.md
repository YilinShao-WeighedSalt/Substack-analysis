# [Market Memo] Hot Summer Topics

Five disjoint engineering-driven notes.

**[1] Applied Materials DRAM/packaging "master class"** — Liked DRAM content: EUV intensity rising in DRAM (a reason author owns [[ASML]]); AMAT bringing SiGe epi to DRAM = believable/valuable; stricter etch benefits AMAT. Savaged AMAT's optics claims as "nonsense numbers" — implied 0.05 pJ/bit CPO (real SiPho CPO 2–3 pJ/bit ideal, VCSEL CPO ~1 pJ/bit); AMAT depicted fiber attach "in the wrongest way possible" (laser is ~always NOT in the PIC; waveguides don't go vertical — need grating coupler). Calls AMAT "optics tourists." Notes thinner DRAM dies push out hybrid bonding (relevant to AMAT's BESI stake).

**[2] Erock (S-1)** — Fast-response natural-gas generators for datacenters. "Dogshit gross margins," thin filing, one patent (engine w/ tall vertical cooling stack). Conveniently omits [[BE]] (Bloom) from comparisons. Story = recurring service revenue.

**[3] HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) — softened** — Long-held hatred (endurance) softened after feedback that SLC (not TLC/QLC) will be used, eliminating most concerns. Remaining problem: integrating NAND control logic into base die → ~10W heat with no public progress shown. [[SNDK]] and [[000660.KS]] are the two working on HBF. Stance: "I hate HBF" → "could work but thermals need solving."

**[4] VCSEL CPO — watching** — Nvidia split: Santa Clara pro ring-modulator SiPho (won, roadmap locked 2–3 yrs across Nvidia/Broadcom/Innolight/Eoptolink/Accelink/AMD/MSFT/AMZN); Israel/Mellanox pro VCSEL. **InP CW laser supply "an unmitigated disaster"** — 3 entities in panic to secure supply. But senior Nvidia figure (ex-SiPho camp) publicly backed VCSEL — a signal for the future. VCSEL leaders: [[AVGO]] "king", [[COHR]] close 2nd, [[LITE]] + Furukawa ([[5801.T]]) decent. 50G NRZ the sweet spot; integration/packaging matters more than VCSEL quality. No investment-view change yet.

**[5] 800V DC practical limits** — Back-of-envelope: ~150m max run for <10% resistive loss from on-site generators (Bloom) to datacenter. 800V direct disproportionately benefits GaN and [[NVTS]] (Navitas), though "Navitas SiC is dogshit" and mgmt over-sells its dual GaN/SiC story. Refs prior "High Voltage, Low Competence" SiC/GaN deep dive.
