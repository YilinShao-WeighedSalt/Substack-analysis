[[irrationalanalysis]] covers two major semiconductor failures in this post: Marvell's botched optical DSP execution and ARM's earnings guidance miss.

**Marvell (MRVL) — Optical DSP implosion.** The author's central thesis is that Marvell botched the port of their 224G SerDes from TSMC N5 to N3P. Supporting evidence includes: 3+ sources reporting poor stability on Trainium 2/2.5, with both Anthropic and Amazon unhappy; public DesignCon and OFC demos showing underwhelming post-FEC results even under favorable/unrealistic channel conditions; multiple hedge-fund contacts confirming Marvell channel-stuffed oDSP inventory; and oDSP customers broadly dissatisfied with performance. Marvell subsequently delayed their investor day, and several senior executives departed — read as corroborating signals. The author expects Broadcom (AVGO) to capture significant oDSP share as a direct result. Current rough oDSP share split is Marvell ~80%, Broadcom ~20%, Maxlinear negligible; the author sees Broadcom taking a large chunk and Maxlinear potentially reaching high-single-digit share on the back of competitive power numbers and Samsung Foundry (SF4X) wafer pricing. The Broadcom call is explicitly bullish off Marvell's misfortune.

**ARM Holdings (ARM) — Guidance miss and royalty stall.** ARM reported a significant guidance miss attributed primarily to the legal defeat against Qualcomm, which the author says was decisive. The key structural concern: V9 royalty growth is stalling because Qualcomm will remain on V8 architecture before eventually pivoting to RISC-V, removing the upgrade royalty uplift ARM had been counting on. The author is also skeptical of ARM's Stargate "technology partner" positioning, characterizing it as a collateral-driven publicity stunt to let SoftBank/Masa lever up — noting that ARM's actual Stargate involvement is simply the Grace component of Grace-Blackwell, which was already well-known. ARM management's evasive behavior on the call regarding disclosure of V9 metrics is flagged as a red sign.
