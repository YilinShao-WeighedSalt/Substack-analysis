[[semianalysis]] analyzes the landmark US-Middle East AI infrastructure deals announced in May 2025, framing them as a strategic triple-win: economic, geopolitical, and infrastructure — while acknowledging meaningful security tradeoffs.

**UAE Deal (G42).** The US granted UAE state-backed AI firm G42 an annual quota of 500,000 top-tier Nvidia chips. G42 retains 20% for its own operations (via its GPU cloud arm Core42/Neocloud); the remaining 80% is reserved for US hyperscalers led by Microsoft, which invested $1.5B in G42 and holds a board seat. A 5 GW AI datacenter campus is under development, with a 1 GW first phase already underway. Core42 already operates a 10 MW datacenter in Fort Worth TX and a 70 MW campus in upstate New York (potentially expanding to 200+ MW in 2026). Power will come from a mix of solar, gas, and nuclear.

**Saudi Arabia Deal (HUMAIN / $600B package).** Within a broader $600B US-Saudi economic package: DataVolt (Saudi colocation developer) commits $20B to US datacenter investments; Oracle commits $14B over ten years; the newly formed Saudi AI firm HUMAIN plans 500 MW of AMD systems (~$10B) plus 500 MW of Nvidia systems over five years, with the initial 2026 tranche consisting of 18,000 Nvidia GB300 units. AWS is partnering on a $5B "AI Zone" accord. Total tech capital commitments could approach $1 trillion.

**Strategic rationale.** SemiAnalysis argues these deals serve three US interests simultaneously: (1) economically, they bypass prior export controls while funneling Gulf capital into US-based GPU deployments; (2) geopolitically, they lock UAE and KSA into American technology stacks, blocking Chinese competitors (Huawei, etc.) before they can gain regional market share; (3) infrastructurally, Gulf energy abundance and sovereign capital resolve the US datacenter power bottleneck — Middle East AI capacity is forecast to exceed 6 GW operational by 2030.

**Security framework.** Four layers are described: cybersecurity matching CoreWeave standards with Kubernetes isolation; hardware controls with secure enclaves protecting model weights and egress monitoring; physical security via unannounced GPU location inspections; and human intelligence with insider-threat programs and intelligence coordination. Strict Know-Your-Customer requirements are mandated, though the piece acknowledges implementation challenges given past semiconductor industry breaches.

**Winner analysis.** Nvidia is the biggest direct beneficiary — anchoring both the UAE (500K chips/year) and Saudi (18K GB300s in 2026 alone, 500 MW over five years) deals. AMD scores a significant win with HUMAIN's 500 MW AMD commitment (~$10B), its largest single sovereign AI contract. Microsoft benefits as G42's preferred hyperscaler customer and capital partner. Oracle secures a $14B ten-year revenue stream. GlobalFoundries is indirectly relevant given Mubadala (UAE sovereign fund) owns 82% of GFS.
