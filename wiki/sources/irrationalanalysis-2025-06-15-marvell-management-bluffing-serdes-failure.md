[[irrationalanalysis]] publishes this post as a direct rebuttal to Marvell management accusing the author of "making up nonsense." The core argument is that Marvell's 3nm optical DSP/SerDes is demonstrably worse than its 5nm predecessor — an engineering regression backed by customer test data shared at a sell-side conference.

**The Irrefutable Proof:** A scatterplot from a major optical transceiver manufacturer shows Marvell's 3nm DSP underperforming the 5nm chip on two key metrics: OMA (optical modulation amplitude, ~0.5 dB worse) and TDECQ (~1 dB worse, averaging 2.6 dB vs. Broadcom's 5nm CPO at ~1.1 dB). The author argues that porting a SerDes from TSMC N5 to N3P should yield free performance gains — not regressions — making this a serious engineering failure. The practical consequence: transceivers using the 3nm DSP lose 0.5–2 km of reach depending on laser wavelength.

**NVLink Fusion as Admission:** The author frames Marvell joining NVIDIA's NVLink Fusion ecosystem as an outright admission that its internal SerDes is uncompetitive. NVLink Fusion's chiplet is effectively a SerDes retimer, making it a direct substitute for Marvell's offering. Questions for Marvell's upcoming Custom AI Investor Day include why the 3nm DSP has lower reach, and what differentiation remains if Alchip and MediaTek can do the same job cheaper via NVLink Fusion.

**Broader Competitive Landscape:** Multiple hedge fund contacts report channel stuffing of Marvell oDSP, customer dissatisfaction, and share shifts toward Broadcom oDSP. Credo's continued success is read as indirect evidence that Marvell's Alaska AEC DSP is underperforming, forcing copper cable companies to wait for Broadcom. MediaTek's own 224G SerDes is apparently failing (NVLink Fusion join seen as confirmation), delaying Google TPU production. Broadcom is the clear beneficiary across both optical and AEC SerDes. Coherent is long due to its monopoly on 6-inch Indium-Phosphide wafer production, though CPO is a content headwind. Semtech has a speculative swing-trade setup (downside ~$30, upside $60–100) following Nvidia's NVL36x2 cancellation. Qualcomm's Alphawave acquisition is treated as sarcastic condolences. BESI is a conviction long on COUPE/hybrid bonding. Micron: the author briefly shorted, lost money, and holds degenerate calls after Samsung failed to improve HBM competitiveness.
