[[irrationalanalysis]] covers six 3Q2024 earnings reports across semiconductors and server hardware, with a bullish slant on ARM and optics, a bearish slant on Astera Labs and Supermicro, and extreme caution on Wolfspeed.

**ARM (ARM):** The key insight is that Qualcomm recently regressed from ARMv9 TLA (Total License Agreement) to v8 ALA (Architecture License Agreement), a headwind the sell-side failed to model, causing an initial stock selloff. The author is strongly bullish near-term, believing ARM will win its lawsuit against Qualcomm and force v9 ALA royalty rates — a scenario with massive earnings torque given v9 rates are materially higher. The CSS (Compute SubSystem) adoption story is also called out as partially overhyped: the author disputes ARM's claim of 50% future CSS smartphone market share, arguing only MediaTek and possibly Xiaomi will adopt CSS, while Apple, Qualcomm, and Huawei will not. Despite long-term doubts (ARM sending a license cancellation notice to Qualcomm is labeled "incredibly short-sighted"), the author sees potential for a 2–3x move in 12–18 months on litigation and royalty rate dynamics.

**Qualcomm (QCOM):** Beat estimates but the stock reversed the after-hours gains in regular trading. The author suspects a Xiaomi/Oppo/Vivo pull-forward of PRC revenue ahead of potential tariffs as the driver of both the beat and the suspicious reversal, though this is labeled an "unsatisfying" explanation. No clear directional stance beyond noting the situation as murky.

**Astera Labs (ALAB):** The author was short going into earnings and was forced to cover at a loss after ALAB ripped. Two thesis errors are acknowledged: underestimating Amazon Trainium 2 ramp (which needs PCIe switches for every connection) and underestimating demand for local PCIe storage in AI servers (KV-cache offload, FSDP-NVMe offload, model weight swapping). Still, the author labels ALAB a "bubble stock" with fake gross margins from Amazon warrant-related related-party revenue, comparing it to Rivian. Estimates ASP for ALAB PCIe switches at $200–$350 using die-size back-calculation.

**Fabrinet (FN):** Reported good numbers; an initial stock drop was dismissed as algo-driven noise. The author aggressively re-initiated a long position on the dip, citing hyper-bullishness on optics demand driven by AI infrastructure build-out.

**Wolfspeed (WOLF):** Deeply bearish. Layoffs of ~20% of workforce, closure of the Durham fab, and 0% non-GAAP gross margin guidance. The author questions why WOLF receives CHIPS Act subsidies given the deteriorating business, and suggests the money would be better directed to Intel.

**Supermicro (SMCI):** Bearish on governance and fundamentals. The author describes SMCI as a "sheet-metal shitco" with 7–15% gross margins that are still deteriorating. Notes prior Nasdaq delisting in 2018, EY auditor resignation, cult-like CEO culture, and history of related-party revenue and improper booking. Believes Nvidia will redirect GPU allocations away from SMCI to avoid compliance risk.
