[[irrationalanalysis]] responds to a Citrini Research optics basket post, grading each ticker call as correct, partially correct, or wrong, drawing on deep engineering knowledge of photonics and compound semiconductors.

**[1] Aixtron (AIXA.DE) — Correct.** The author's favorite optics-focused semiconductor capital equipment name. Already up 45% on the position. Endorses Citrini's coverage fully and argues Aixtron should be the highest-weight name in any optics basket. The bull case rests on MOCVD equipment demand for compound semiconductor growth.

**[2] Furukawa Electric (5801.T) — Partially Correct.** Citrini's general thesis on Furukawa is right, but the CPO characterization is wrong. Furukawa's DFB lasers top out at ~70 mW, far short of the 300–400 mW required for co-packaged optics ELS applications; blind-mate connectors and ferrule products are fine, but the company does not have ultra-high-power DFB capacity for CPO. The author wants to buy but is constrained by Japan's 100-share minimum block (~$22K USD per block).

**[3] Nokia (NOK) — Correct (with caveat).** The 6G wireless narrative is dismissed as "loser brain rot," but Nokia's optical networking business — primarily Infinera — is genuinely attractive. Nokia/Infinera represents one of only two 6-inch InP fabs in the world, and price/book of ~1.8 makes it a "no-brainer" with significant upside. The key variable is whether they can improve yields beyond photodiodes to more complex photonic integrated circuits.

**[4] Soitec (SOI.PA) — Correct.** Previously on the fence, the author began buying after reading Citrini's coverage. Wants to add significantly more. No specific engineering pushback; the coverage is described as high quality.

**[5] AAOI (Applied Optoelectronics) — Wrong (Citrini bearish, author bullish).** The author sold pre-market to lock in profit — not because the bear case is right, but to front-run momentum traders who would blindly follow Citrini's negative view. Fundamental disagreement: AAOI has internal CW laser capacity for transceivers, and Nvidia CPO has no bearing on transceiver volumes until 2029 at the earliest. Author intends to buy back on chart weakness.

**[6] Himax (HIMX) — Completely Wrong.** Citrini and Hunterbrook sourced analysis from a purported former Lumentum engineer. The author finds the analysis superficially impressive (patents, diagrams) but lacking on critical metrics: no discussion of coupling efficiency, polarization extinction ratio, or alignment tolerance. The micro-lens assemblies appear designed for grating coupler attach, which has inherently high optical insertion loss vs. edge coupling — and no evidence is provided that the lenses compensate adequately.

**[7] POET Technologies (POET) — Completely Wrong (vaporware).** The connection between POET and Celestial AI is described as almost non-existent, particularly since Celestial is moving to COUPE. POET's IR and marketing materials use linear (not log/dB) scale on plots, obscuring poor SMSR performance; key laser specs (RIN, linewidth) are absent. The author characterizes POET as a trap for generalists unfamiliar with photonics fundamentals.
