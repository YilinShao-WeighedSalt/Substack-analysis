[[irrationalanalysis]] presents a long/short opportunity in semiconductor capital equipment centered on the advanced packaging inspection market. The core thesis is that Onto Innovation (ONTO) has been displaced by KLA in the advanced packaging inspection and metrology segment, specifically for CoWoS-L — TSMC's most advanced chip-on-wafer-on-substrate variant featuring embedded silicon bridges with tighter bump-pitch tolerances.

The post opens with a primer on CoWoS variants: CoWoS-S (simplest), CoWoS-R (adds redistribution layer), and CoWoS-L (embeds local silicon interconnects, analogous to Intel's EMIB). The key challenge in advanced packaging is aligning chips with extremely fine bump-pitch; failures are costly and inspection is critical, which is what initially drove demand for ONTO and CAMT tools during the AI boom.

The author argues Onto's Q1 2025 earnings call was revealing in its "copium" — management language is interpreted as implicitly confirming ONTO failed qualification for CoWoS-L inspection at TSMC. KLA, traditionally a wafer process-control tool maker, re-purposed its tools for advanced packaging metrology and won the business. ONTO now faces the unenviable position of trying to take process-control share from KLA — the dominant incumbent — after losing its own advanced packaging niche.

Camtek (CAMT), an Israeli pure-play advanced packaging inspection company, faces similar competitive pressure from KLA re-purposed tools but has a compensating advantage: it can sell high volumes into mainland China, where ONTO (an American firm with U.S. manufacturing) is effectively blocked by export controls.

The author's preferred trade is **long KLAC / short ONTO** as the balanced-risk version, acknowledging KLA is not a pure-play in advanced packaging. The more aggressive "degenerate" version is **long CAMT / short ONTO**, pairing two near-pure-plays with ONTO as a funding short. A sidebar notes Intel Foundry should buy more KLA machines to improve 18A yields — framed as both genuine advice and an insult.

## Calls

- [[ONTO]] — SHORT — px@n/a — failed CoWoS-L qualification at TSMC; KLA has taken advanced packaging inspection share and ONTO's pivot to process control faces KLA head-on
- [[KLAC]] — LONG — px@n/a — KLA re-purposed process-control tools dominate advanced packaging metrology; preferred long leg in the balanced-risk pair trade
- [[CAMT]] — LONG — px@n/a — advanced packaging pure-play that can offset KLA competition via large China volume; more aggressive long leg against ONTO short
