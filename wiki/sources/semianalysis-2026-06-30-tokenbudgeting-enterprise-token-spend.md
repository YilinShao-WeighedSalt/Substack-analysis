# TokenBudgeting: Our Conversations with Enterprises on Token Spend

Crystal Huang, Joey Brookhart, Dylan Patel. Interviewed 50+ enterprises (Slack/phone/Databricks AI Summit) to test whether the "token budget blowout" headlines mean AI demand is rolling over. Conclusion: **headlines overblown; enterprise AI spend keeps growing; no material risk to 2H26 AI budgets.**

Key data: viral cases (Meta 60T tokens/mo, "Claudeconomics" leaderboard; Uber burned annual Claude Code/Codex budget in 4 months → $1,500/mo/employee cap) stem from poor incentives, not AI failing. Even Meta (~$50k/yr/employee) is only a 3-5% Anthropic customer. Spend is concentrated: 99th-pct customers ~$90k/employee/yr, 90th-pct ~$7,300, median tech-forward ~$136, median Fortune 500 <$100 — huge runway. Budgets now normal but no convergence ($250/mo aerospace & defense → $2,000/mo Workday/Stripe); most employees never hit caps. **70%+ of Anthropic/OpenAI revenue is coding today** (Anthropic 90%+ B2B vs OpenAI ~60%); cyber and white-collar knowledge work expected to repeat the pattern. Conservation tricks: downgrade default model (one 800-engineer travel-tech firm moved everyone Opus→Sonnet; aerospace firm turned off Opus 4.8 + Fast-Mode), game free M365 Copilot allowance. TaaS market (Together/Fireworks/Baseten) >$4B ARR; AWS Bedrock estimate drives SemiAnalysis's AWS growth "well above street." Anthropic's own docs: avg Claude Code dev $150-250/mo, only 10% spend >$30/day.
