[[irrationalanalysis]] argues that CES 2025 exposed the terminal decline of Intel's product lineup and the collapse of the "Wintel" x86 moat, drawing on disclosures from Intel, AMD, and Nvidia at the show.

**Lunar Lake ramp is a disaster.** Intel disclosed 1.5 million Lunar Lake units shipped — the author estimates this represents roughly 3% of Intel's client volume in the launch quarter (Q4 2024). This inadvertently confirms SemiAnalysis reporting and contradicts management's claim of being "all-in" on AI PCs. Intel 10nm (rebranded Intel 7) is on par with TSMC N7, an obsolete node for power-sensitive applications. The author expects Intel to lose share at an accelerating rate to AMD, Apple, Qualcomm, and a fast-rising Nvidia/MediaTek combination targeting 5-15% of the PC market in 2025.

**Dell abandons Intel exclusivity in enterprise.** Dell — historically an Intel-only shop — has entered a significant partnership with AMD on enterprise laptops, the segment where Intel's vPRO (management engine) moat was strongest. The author reads this as a direct consequence of the Raptor Lake reliability disaster: OEMs have field failure data and no longer trust Intel. AMD now has a vPRO-equivalent management solution and Dell is helping sell it.

**Nvidia Digits kills the x86 developer platform.** Nvidia's ARM-based Digits workstation is described as the final nail in Intel's coffin. Combined with Apple Silicon MacBooks — now standard issue for most developers the author knows — the new AI/software development stack has no dependency on x86. The platform moat is gone.

**Intel's upcoming earnings expected to be a "circus."** Bloomberg consensus on gross margin and revenue are seen as too optimistic given Lunar Lake volumes and rumored ~10% parametric yield on Panther Lake CPU chiplets on the 18A node. Management's margin improvement narrative tied to Panther Lake ramp is viewed as unbelievable if yields are as bad as rumored. The author calls Intel a going concern that Wall Street has not yet priced correctly.

**DOJ actions against CXMT and Jensen Huang's comments on Samsung HBM3e** are flagged as unambiguously positive for SK Hynix and Micron: risk has cleared and the author holds approximately $12K in Micron options with intent to add.
