[[irrationalanalysis]] surveys 2Q24 earnings across six semiconductor names during what it calls a chaotic, volatile week, delivering bearish verdicts on most.

**Lattice Semiconductor (LSCC):** The CEO's abrupt departure weeks earlier now makes sense. LSCC makes low-end FPGAs into a weak demand environment. The company attempted to build a new toolchain and ecosystem to address its legacy software shortcomings — that effort has failed; the new toolchain still does not work well. Bearish by implication.

**AMD:** The author had shorted AMD weeks before earnings, captured a ~10% gain, covered on a gut feel that retail investors would chase MI300/325X guidance hype, and then re-shorted the stock post-earnings. The core thesis: AMD's datacenter GPU revenue is entirely dependent on MI300/MI325X, and those sales will implode the moment Nvidia ramps Blackwell. When that GPU story breaks down, the author plans to flip long on AMD's merit in client and datacenter CPU (where share gains vs. Intel remain strong) and in Xilinx FPGA technology (which the author considers best-in-class). Management's commentary on tight CoWoS supply into 2025 is dismissed as a pump.

**ARM:** ARM sold off 20% in a single session on earnings. The author sees the drawdown as noise — short-term weakness in laptop/PC market penetration (blamed on Qualcomm's botched Windows-on-ARM launch) and smartphone volumes is irrelevant to the structural datacenter opportunity. The stock reached the author's pre-drawn technical support level; they plan to add to their existing small long position.

**Qualcomm (QCOM):** Beat on handsets (strong current smartphone cycle; Xiaomi and Vivo outperformance) and crushed automotive (share gains from a competitor — foreshadowing Mobileye). IoT/PC was weak despite heavy "AI PC" marketing; the Snapdragon X Elite numbers simply were not there. Management repeatedly evaded analyst questions asking for PC ramp revenue guidance, citing a lack of sell-through data — a claim the author calls obviously false, since Windows 11 ARM activations provide instantaneous sell-through. Reads as management hiding bad data. Stock retraced all after-hours gains and closed down ~1.5%.

**Mobileye (MBLY):** MBLY was "annihilated and rightfully so." The author diagnoses a structural problem: Qualcomm's automotive solutions are superior and winning share from Mobileye. This trend will not reverse. MBLY is a trap; the author considers initiating a short.

**Intel (INTC):** Described as a "disaster of apocalyptic proportions." Positives: dividend finally suspended (long overdue). Negatives: hemorrhaging CPU share to AMD in both client and datacenter, losing datacenter share to Nvidia Grace, Google/Marvell Axion, Amazon Graviton, and ARM CSS-based designs for Microsoft. Running high-volume production in "hot lot" mode for Meteor Lake — an expensive, desperate measure to defend share at terrible margins. Intel Foundry pipeline is anemic; the 2018 10nm foundry fiasco has poisoned industry trust and current 18A engagements are viewed as low-priority trust exercises. The business outlook is bleak through H2 2026 at best, with a realistic turnaround not before high-volume third-party 18A production in 2027.
