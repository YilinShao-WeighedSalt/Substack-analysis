[[globalsemiresearch]] argues that indium phosphide (InP) substrates are entering a structural supercycle driven by AI computing's shift from copper to optical interconnects, and that AXT (AXTI) is the most direct beneficiary due to superior yield control, customer lock-in, and favorable regulatory positioning.

## Demand Drivers

Three application vectors are cited as the core demand thesis. First, EML (Electroabsorption Modulated Laser) chips for 100G+ optical modules require InP substrates as their foundational material. Second, CW lasers used as external light sources in silicon photonics solutions offer high chip output per substrate wafer, amplifying substrate demand per dollar of end-product revenue. Third, CPO (Co-Packaged Optics) — which integrates optical modules directly with processors — dramatically increases InP substrate consumption per system and represents the long-term structural growth driver as AI cluster density rises.

Lumentum (LITE) projected 85% annualized growth for the InP-dependent optical industry at OFC 2026. The author's industry checks suggest 2025 global InP shipments of only 600,000–700,000 wafers against demand of 1.5–2 million wafers — a supply deficit exceeding 70%. Order books reportedly extend through 2027 and beyond, with Chinese market prices rising ~20% YoY in early 2026 (from below RMB 2,500 to nearly RMB 3,000 by Q1 2026).

## Competitive Landscape and Moat

The global InP substrate market is an oligopoly: Sumitomo (Japan), AXT (U.S.), and JX Metals (Japan) control 80–90% of share. Chinese producers (Yunnan Germanium, Guangdong Xiandao, Dingtai) collectively hold under 10% at volumes an order of magnitude below the leaders.

The author pushes back on the common bear case that InP is a low-barrier industry because plant capex is only ~$60M per 400,000-wafer-equivalent. The real moat lies in yield, process know-how, and customer certification. Industry-wide single-crystal yields average just 20–25%, with even AXT and Sumitomo achieving only 15–20%; Chinese producers fall below 10%. The economics are severe: of 100 kg of high-purity indium feedstock, only ~10 kg becomes saleable product with minimal waste recovery. Each percentage point of yield improvement materially reduces unit costs and expands margins.

## Investment Thesis Summary

AXT is positioned as the primary beneficiary of the InP supercycle through a combination of: (1) deep yield expertise that Chinese competitors cannot replicate quickly; (2) customer certification lock-in that creates sticky, long-duration revenue; (3) capacity expansion underway; and (4) regulatory advantages (U.S.-based supplier in a geopolitically sensitive material). The post characterizes AXTI as the most direct pure-play on the InP substrate shortage.
