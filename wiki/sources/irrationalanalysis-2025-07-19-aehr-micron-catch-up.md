[[irrationalanalysis]] presents three quick catch-up ideas written under a self-imposed one-hour time limit.

**Aehr Test Systems (AEHR):** The author describes AEHR as a "shittiest of shitcos" — a silicon-carbide wafer-level burn-in meme stock with a pumper CEO and essentially no institutional coverage. Despite having previously shorted the stock, the author flipped bullish after re-reading the latest earnings call transcript. The catalyst: CEO Gayn stated on record that two hyperscalers are evaluating wafer-level burn-in of AI logic wafers. This matters because Aehr's traditional markets are specialty non-silicon/non-logic wafers (SiC, GaN, InP). Conventional logic reliability uses HTOL (High-Temperature Operating Life) testing — a one-time, 42-day minimum qualification run done once per design, not on every wafer. Wafer-level burn-in of logic wafers would be economically irrational under normal circumstances. The thesis is that hyperscalers (excluding Google, which has more mature ASIC processes) are so pressed for time-to-market on AI ASICs that they are skipping electromigration simulation and HTOL, instead burning in every logic wafer. CoWoS and HBM costs are so high relative to logic die COGS that the economics could justify it. The author believes this is real, acknowledges the stock is uninvestable for institutional buy-side given the CEO's credibility track record, and frames it explicitly as a degenerate, high-risk idea. Options chain is described as "revolting" — do not touch it.

**Micron (MU):** Samsung mass-produced HBM3e 12-hi inventory in anticipation of qualifying with Nvidia, then apparently misrepresented specs to Nvidia for the sixth time. The result is a large inventory write-off sitting on Samsung's books. The binary: if Samsung re-bins that inventory to actually meet Nvidia's power/parametric specs, some fraction (30–50%) could pass, partially reversing the write-off and directly hurting Micron's HBM market share. The author sees MU as a binary — either $160 or $90 — depending entirely on Samsung's decision. No strong directional call; the message is to tread lightly.

**Marvell (MRVL):** Flagged briefly as a bonus meme via a social media link, with no substantive thesis presented.
