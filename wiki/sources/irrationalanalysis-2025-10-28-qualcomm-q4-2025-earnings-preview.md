Written eight days before Qualcomm's Q4 FY2025 earnings, this post from [[irrationalanalysis]] is a blunt pre-earnings bear case on QCOM. The author's central thesis is that a last-minute "AI inference rack" announcement made during the pre-earnings quiet period is a publicity stunt designed to stoke excitement while dodging hard questions — no FLOPS specs were disclosed, and the PCIe and LPDDR revisions were not revealed, suggesting the chip is likely optimized only for INT4/INT8 throughput rather than competitive AI performance.

The author cites an October 2, 2025 SEC filing showing that CEO Cristiano Amon liquidated approximately half his personal QCOM holdings weeks after the author's prior deep-dive on Qualcomm's failed datacenter AI chip. The CEO also sold a small ESPP tranche at short-term capital gains rates — details the author reads as insider conviction in the stock's near-term downside.

On the core smartphone business, several headwinds are enumerated: (1) Apple's iPhone 17 cycle has been large but Apple has been quietly gaining modem share, with a possible stockpile of thin modems that could be used to "rugpull" Qualcomm in CY2026; (2) China exposure is deteriorating — Xiaomi showing significant weakness while Samsung Galaxy share gains may be running above 25%; (3) DRAM and NAND price trends are pressuring smartphone bill-of-materials in ways that will squeeze Qualcomm's blended ASPs.

The author explicitly discloses a short (put) position in QCOM and notes the implied volatility is cheap following the announcement, suggesting the market is not pricing in the downside risk. The author is considering quadrupling the put position before earnings. Long positions in AVGO and NVDA are disclosed as the author's preferred "real AI company" exposures.
