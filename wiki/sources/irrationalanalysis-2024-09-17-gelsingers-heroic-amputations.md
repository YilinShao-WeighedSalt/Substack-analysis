[[irrationalanalysis]] reacts bullishly to a cluster of Intel announcements that sent INTC up ~18% over five trading days, attributing the move partly to short covering and partly to genuinely positive news.

The author walks through four developments: (1) A new $3B in incremental funding on top of existing CHIPS Act money — characterized as the least interesting item. (2) An AWS AI fabric chip win — the author's best guess is that Amazon is building a high-bandwidth NIC for its Trainium/Inferentia AI ASICs (semi-custom silicon from MRVL, fabbed by TSM), and that Intel's contribution is PCIe PHY/PMA interface IP on 18A, since no other vendor has that IP available on that process node in time. (3) A custom Xeon 6 deal with AWS, interpreted as a modified Granite Rapids (not Sierra Forest), analogous to the Cooper Lake custom Xeon Intel built for META that added BF16 support to Cascade Lake. (4) Conversion to a wholly-owned foundry subsidiary — seen as a structural positive, one step closer to a full AMD/GlobalFoundries-style separation.

Additional observations: the EU fab expansion is effectively canceled despite being officially called a "2-year delay"; the halt to Malaysia advanced packaging capacity startup is read as confirmation of real third-party IFS demand, with EMIB and Foveros now having proper EDA flows; and the recent re-orgs are flagged as a standard precursor to layoffs, with a direct warning to Intel employees who have been rolled into larger organizations.

The overall tone is constructive on Intel's strategic direction — the "heroic amputations" framing suggests that painful but necessary cuts are putting the company on firmer footing.
