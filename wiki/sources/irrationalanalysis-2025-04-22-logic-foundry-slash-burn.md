[[irrationalanalysis]] argues that leading-edge logic foundries are in a strange position where engineering reality is diverging sharply from the investment narrative — and uses that divergence to build a contrarian bull case for Intel.

The author opens by disclosing that all TSM shares were liquidated after "Liberation Day" tariff announcements (booking long-term profits), with a small active INTC position that is actively traded with covered calls. Samsung Electronics is unowned due to lack of access to Korean equity markets.

**Intel 18A / Nova Lake dual-sourcing.** The post addresses the circulating rumor that Intel Products has reserved TSMC N2 capacity for Nova Lake (the Panther Lake successor, targeting H2 2026). The author frames this as confirmation that 18A in its current state is uncompetitive: 18A is not competitive with TSMC N3P today. It may reach parity with N3P in late 2026 via incremental PDK improvements, and 18A-P could compete with TSMC N2 on cost by 2027. The current PDK suffers from missing libraries, missing device characterizations, poor leakage, and high process variation.

**Process node comparison methodology.** Density numbers (e.g. SRAM cell area from conference papers or TechInsights) are called unreliable. The only valid comparison is porting a real IP block (CPU core, PCIe PHY, ASIC) or full product to the target node. The author walks through a hypothetical showing how a node with worse digital-logic switching frequency and higher analog variation can be net-negative in real area even if headline SRAM density improved.

**Samsung Foundry SF3/SF2.** SF3 is described as an "unmitigated disaster" with essentially zero design activity and PPA worse than TSMC N5. SF2 is characterized as a rebranded SF3 — not a true 2nm-class node — making it vaporware relative to TSMC N2 or Intel 14A.

**Bull case for INTC.** Intel's Price/Book ratio below 1x is the core catalyst. Lip-Bu Tan is expected to slash CapEx (avoiding value-destructive spending on 18A fabs before volume ramps), and to cut 40–50% of headcount — both actions preserving book value. Once 18A-P is proven, the stock can re-rate to 2–3x P/B; GFS and TSEM trade at 1.3–2x P/B today, and a resurgent Intel deserves a premium. The author wants to re-enter TSM but considers it not yet at the right price post-Liberation Day.
