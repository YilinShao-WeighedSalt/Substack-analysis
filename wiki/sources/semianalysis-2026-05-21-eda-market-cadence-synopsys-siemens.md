[[semianalysis]] presents a comprehensive primer on the Electronic Design Automation market, arguing that the Big-3 oligopoly (Synopsys, Cadence, Siemens EDA) is structurally permanent and that both leading pure-play vendors are well-positioned to compound earnings well above semiconductor R&D growth rates.

**Market structure.** The EDA+IP market totals ~$18B in 2025, with the Big-3 commanding 85%+ combined share. Synopsys leads at $8.0B revenue (including the $35B Ansys acquisition completed July 2025), Cadence at $5.3B, and Siemens EDA at an estimated $2.2–2.5B. The market is projected to reach $28–31B by 2030, with expanded TAMs from simulation (Ansys/Altair/MSC Software) lifting the addressable base to $29–32B.

**Why the oligopoly is permanent.** Six reinforcing lock-in layers — sequential tool dependencies (changing synthesis forces downstream re-runs of place-and-route, signoff, and physical verification), franchise tools at each layer (Synopsys PrimeTime at 90%+ signoff share, Cadence Palladium at 55–60% emulation, Siemens Calibre at 85%+ physical verification), design-start entrenchment (95%+ retention at advanced nodes), co-developed PDKs 24 months pre-production, trained-engineer switching costs, and proprietary design databases — make meaningful share shifts at 7nm and below effectively impossible. Customer retention by category: signoff/analog at 99%+, simulation at 95–97%, physical verification at 98%+.

**Cadence preferred organically in FY26.** While Synopsys reports 36% FY26 growth (Ansys-inflated), organic EDA+IP growth is decelerating to ~7–8% due to Intel foundry delays depressing IP revenue and China export controls cutting that segment from 16% to 12% of revenue. Cadence organic growth is accelerating toward double-digits, driven by digital full-flow momentum (36 new logos in 2024 vs. 10 in 2014), Cerebrus AI at 1,000+ tapeouts (5.6× in under two years), and 25%+ IP growth in FY25. Cadence margin expansion is structural: 53 percentage points over 15 years, with 50% incremental revenue drop-through to operating income delivered for 7+ consecutive years. FY26 backlog of $7.8B covers 67% of revenue pre-booking.

**AI tool monetization is early but compounding.** Cadence Cerebrus and Synopsys DSO.ai are growing exponentially in tapeout adoption, but full pricing capture awaits FY27–28 contract renewal cycles. Token/capacity-based licensing unlocks 30–40% utilization slack as pure vendor upside; AI tool premiums of 15–25% on renewals are already documented at Synopsys. The shift from seat-based to enterprise license agreements further obscures per-tool ROI, entrenching vendor pricing power.

**China is a structural headwind, primarily for Synopsys.** Export controls progressively tightened 2019–2025; Synopsys China revenue fell 22% in FY25. Local alternatives (Primarius, Empyrean) are credible at 28nm but remain 2–3 years behind at advanced nodes. ARM's Flexible Access model (70%+ of new agreements since 2019) represents a licensing model evolution worth monitoring for IP commoditization risk.

**Siemens EDA's Calibre blocking position is durable but insufficient.** TSMC, Samsung, and Intel mandate Calibre-clean tape-outs, ensuring permanent revenue regardless of competitive pressure elsewhere. However, without matching Synopsys-Ansys or Cadence-MSC multi-physics platform breadth, Siemens must extract value from the Altair acquisition ($5.9B, 2024) to remain competitive beyond physical verification.
