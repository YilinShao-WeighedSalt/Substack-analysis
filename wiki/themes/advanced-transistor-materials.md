## Narrative
SemiAnalysis planted the flag on this theme in early 2024, using IEDM 2023 as the launching point for CFET and next-generation transistor architectures as the axis along which leading-edge competition would be decided. Coverage deepened through 2024 with the GAA-vs-FinFET foundry clash, backside power delivery, and the materials bottlenecks constraining DRAM scaling, while irrationalanalysis contributed foundational framing on what knowledge was required to track the space. The February 2025 deep-dive on TSMC N2, Intel's EMIB, and 2D materials marked a step-change in analytical depth, treating CFET less as a future roadmap item and more as an imminent competitive differentiator. By early 2026 the conversation had shifted from transistor geometry to interconnect materials — specifically what replaces copper at sub-2nm — with semidoped and globalsemiresearch adding lithography and Huawei's own scaling attempts as parallel data points. The theme is maturing: the architectural questions are well-defined, the leading-edge foundry race is live, and attention is now on which materials and integration schemes will determine winners at the angstrom era.
