## Narrative
SemiAnalysis planted the energy grid theme in early 2024 with its AI datacenter energy race deep dive, framing power delivery and grid interconnection as a first-order constraint on AI infrastructure scaling. The theme lay dormant in broader coverage until mid-2025, when SemiAnalysis returned with the AI training grid blackout piece — the moment grid-scale power limits shifted from background risk to acute bottleneck. From late 2025 into 2026, IrrationalAnalysis took up the equity angle in earnest: LNG and gas peaker plays, high-voltage equipment, supercapacitor components, and the operational incompetence of utilities trying to serve hyperscaler load all appeared as investable theses. SemiAnalysis reinforced the structural side with analysis of electric bills at scale and power co-design requirements for next-generation rack architectures like Vera Rubin. The theme is maturing rather than consensus — publication frequency accelerated sharply in early 2026 and the investable perimeter is still widening, spanning transmission equipment, on-site generation, energy storage, and grid-adjacent components.
