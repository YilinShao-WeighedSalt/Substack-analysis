## Narrative
IrrationalAnalysis was the earliest voice on this theme, framing Micron's HBM ramp as a supply-chain event as early as March 2024, well before the AI infrastructure buildout drew broader analyst attention. SemiAnalysis deepened the forensics angle through 2024 by publishing detailed BOM teardowns of Blackwell systems, exposing component-level bottlenecks — power delivery, advanced packaging, and substrate supply — that constrained GPU shipments independent of TSMC yield. Through 2025 the coverage broadened: tariff-driven rerouting, TSMC overseas fab viability, and memory-type rotation (MR-DIMM vs. NAND) became recurring subthemes across all four publications. By early 2026 GlobalSemiResearch joined with high-conviction calls on memory supplier leverage and multi-year prepayment contracts, while IrrationalAnalysis and Citrini converged on the idea that HBM allocation and supply-chain inheritance now determine AI system roadmaps as much as chip design itself. The theme is maturing — the forensic methodology is established and widely shared — but the specific battlegrounds (CPO timing, China memory substitution, five-year LTAs) continue to generate differentiated calls.
