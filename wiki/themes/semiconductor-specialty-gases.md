## Plain-language explanation

Making a chip needs dozens of ultra-pure **industrial gases and chemicals**, most of which investors never hear about. They are cheap per wafer but absolutely required — if one process step lacks its gas, the whole wafer is scrap. The industry analogy: "specialty gas is to a fab what oxygen is to a human." Because these inputs are a tiny share of a chip's cost but non-substitutable, their prices have **almost no ceiling** when physical supply is short — a high-margin memory fab will pay almost anything rather than idle a line.

Key terms:
- **WF6 (tungsten hexafluoride):** gas used in **CVD** (chemical vapor deposition) to lay down the tungsten "wires" (word-lines and contacts) inside every advanced memory and logic chip. Only copper and tungsten compete at these scales; tungsten owns the contact/word-line layers.
- **CVD:** a manufacturing step where a gas decomposes inside a plasma chamber and deposits a thin metal film onto the silicon wafer.
- **Feedstock:** the raw input a producer needs — here, high-purity tungsten powder, ~80% of which is mined/refined in China.

## Narrative

The theme opens in June 2026 with Global Semi Research's WF6 deep dive — the sharpest specialty-materials dislocation since krypton/xenon spiked during the Russia-Ukraine war. Trigger: **China's tungsten export controls** (aimed mainly at Japan's military industry, since tungsten also makes armor-piercing munitions) cut Japanese gas makers off from feedstock; a 2025 fire at Kanto Denka compounded it. Japan's two majors (Kanto Denka ~1,200 t/yr, Central Glass ~1,000 t/yr) ceased WF6 output **permanently from July 1, 2026**, erasing ~2,200 t — nearly a quarter of ~12,000 t global capacity — into rising AI-driven demand (~11,000+ t in 2026). Related fluorine chemicals (HF acid, fluorocarbons) also up 2-3x. Winners: Chinese producers consolidating the market (mostly unlisted). Caveat: new capacity is expected by **mid-2027**, making this a *time-limited* monopoly, not a durable moat. The theme is the materials-side mirror of the geopolitics theme: the West restricts China's access to chip *tools*; China counters by squeezing the *raw inputs* (rare earths, tungsten, fluorine) everyone depends on. It also feeds the memory-inflation story (Apple price hikes) since these costs pass through in a >70%-margin HBM fab.
