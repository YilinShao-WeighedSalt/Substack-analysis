## Narrative
irrationalanalysis has tracked semiconductor IP and licensing as a recurring investment lens since early 2024, initially framing ARM as undervalued relative to its royalty stream before the market re-rated the stock. The ARM-Qualcomm licensing dispute became the defining flashpoint of the theme, receiving four mentions in a single October 2024 piece characterized as ARM's "Chernobyl moment," signaling how existential royalty conflicts could reprice the entire IP licensor category. Coverage broadened through 2025 to include CEVA's exposure to Apple's in-house modem ambitions, Synopsys as a pure-play EDA licensing compounder, and Tenstorrent's RISC-V positioning as a potential disruptor to ARM's dominance. The consistent cadence from 2024 through early 2026 — spanning ARM valuation, licensee disputes, and EDA toolchain moats — indicates the author treats IP monetization and licensing risk as a durable analytical framework rather than a one-off trade, with the theme now maturing as the key disputes and valuations have become more widely recognized by the market.
