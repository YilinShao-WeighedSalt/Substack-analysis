## Thesis evolution
Samsung's coverage arc is the most contested and volatile in the memory sector: citrini opened long in mid-2024 on generic DRAM/AI-phone exposure, but by late 2024 irrationalanalysis had turned outright short, citing structural deterioration in the DS-Memory division comparable to Intel's 2016-2021 decline, and semianalysis reinforced that view through mid-2025 by documenting specific HBM yield failures and a pattern of announcing leading-edge packaging technology while missing execution. The pivot came in early 2026 — semianalysis upgraded to long in February once Rubin HBM4 allocation (~30%) was confirmed, and globalsemiresearch became the most bullish voice by March, arguing that the combination of Groq LP30 foundry exclusivity, full-stack DRAM/NAND/SSD exposure, and incoming hyperscaler prepayments made Samsung the single largest GTC 2026 beneficiary. The residual bearish thread is semianalysis's April 2026 note that HBM4 yield remains roughly half SK Hynix's and that foundry wins came partly through pricing concessions — so Samsung's bull case rests on macro DRAM tightness and diversified exposure rather than on competitive HBM leadership, which stays firmly with 000660.KS. The irrationalanalysis May 2026 note added an unusual constructive angle: Samsung's in-house foundry and silicon photonics co-design capacity could give it a structural edge in the next phase of memory integration, partially rehabilitating the long thesis on differentiation grounds.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are recorded across any entry in this log, so direct price-performance verification is not possible. The thesis trajectory shows a clear V-shape: the short calls from late 2024 through mid-2025 were consistent with reported HBM share losses and a P/B below 1, suggesting those bearish entries were operationally correct at the time. The subsequent bullish pivot is validated at the operating-metrics level by the confirmed ~30% Rubin allocation, reported Microsoft prepayment of over $10B, and new foundry design wins — though Samsung remains a second-tier HBM supplier relative to SK Hynix. The live long view would be falsified by a further HBM4 yield deterioration that pushes Rubin allocation below 20%, by hyperscaler prepayments failing to materialize or being renegotiated, or by a DRAM oversupply event before multi-year LTAs lock in pricing; the smartphone drag thesis would be confirmed if consumer DRAM pricing decouples meaningfully from datacenter DRAM pricing.
