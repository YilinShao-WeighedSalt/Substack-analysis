## Thesis evolution
The view on MXL began as a clear structural short in July 2024, grounded in two compounding failures: a Huawei export license revocation gutting wireless infrastructure revenue, and broad share losses across broadband/connectivity markets (DOCSIS, WiFi, PON) as MXL was squeezed between AVGO at the high end and MediaTek at the low end. By December 2024 the bearish stance was reaffirmed with no change — core markets still declining and MXL's PAM4 oDSP was viewed as inferior technology facing entrenched competition from MRVL (~80% share) and AVGO (~20%). The May 2025 update introduced the first hint of a potential catalyst: Marvell's botched N3P SerDes port created an opening, and the author flagged MXL as a possible dark-horse reaching high-single-digit oDSP share on power efficiency and cheap Samsung SF4X wafer pricing. This softens the short from structural to watchful — the core bearish thesis on legacy markets is unchanged, but oDSP optionality has moved from negligible to non-trivial.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values exist for any of the three calls, so price-based verdict is not possible. The thesis trajectory shows the short has been directionally consistent — the core business (broadband/connectivity) has not recovered, and MXL has remained a troubled company through mid-2025. The live view hinges on whether Marvell's oDSP stumble translates into meaningful MXL design wins at hyperscalers; the short would be falsified by confirmed customer traction in oDSP (e.g., a named hyperscaler win on SF4X) or a recovery in broadband end-markets. Absent those developments, the structural bear case from the original July 2024 analysis remains intact.
