## Thesis evolution
Citrini introduced VSH in May 2026 as part of a broader "Supply Chain Inheritance" thesis: AI data center rack architecture (particularly 800V DC designs) is directly inheriting power-delivery technology and supply chains built for the EV and solar boom. VSH, as a maker of discrete passive components — resistors, capacitors, inductors, and power diodes/MOSFETs — sits squarely in that inherited supply chain. The bull case is structurally simple: incumbents like VSH exercised capex restraint after EV demand collapsed, so when AI-driven passive component demand accelerates, supply will lag and ASPs will expand. Citrini groups VSH alongside Murata (6981.T) and Samsung Electro-Mechanics (009150.KS) as early movers ahead of what it characterizes as an imminent MLCC and passive component shortage. No other publication has weighed in, so there is no cross-publication disagreement to note.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call value was recorded at the time of the call, so price-based scoring is not possible. The thesis is live and falsifiable on two conditions: (1) AI data center capex continues to expand and flows into rack-level power management components rather than being absorbed earlier in the supply chain, and (2) VSH maintains pricing discipline rather than chasing volume at lower ASPs. A sustained decline in AI infrastructure spending or an unexpected capacity addition by VSH or peers that suppresses ASPs would invalidate the call. The thesis trajectory has not yet been confirmed or refuted by subsequent publications.
