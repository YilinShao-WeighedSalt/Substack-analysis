## Thesis evolution
The single call on record originates from irrationalanalysis in March 2026, framing Cheniere as the primary beneficiary of a potential Qatari supply disruption. The logic is a supply-shock trade: if Qatar — the world's largest LNG exporter — experiences an outage, Asian buyers with no short-term alternatives would be forced to reroute demand toward US cargoes, directly lifting Henry Hub-linked export netbacks and Cheniere's realized margins. No bearish counterpoint from any publication is on record, leaving the view uncontested. The thesis is event-driven and contingent rather than a structural long.

## Outcome tracking
No price at call was recorded, so a quantitative assessment of the trade is not possible at this time. The thesis lives or dies on whether a meaningful Qatari supply disruption materializes and whether Asian spot LNG prices spike enough to widen US export spreads. The view would be falsified if Qatar supply remains uninterrupted, Asian buyers maintain diversified supply chains that absorb any shock without rerouting to US cargoes, or if US export capacity constraints prevent Cheniere from capturing incremental demand. Absent the triggering event, the call is in a neutral holding state.
