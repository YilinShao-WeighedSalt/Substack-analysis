## Thesis evolution
The sole published view on Yaskawa comes from SemiAnalysis in their March 2025 robotics piece, where the stock is framed as a structural short. The bear case has two interlocking legs: (1) Japan's accelerating demographic contraction is compressing the domestic industrial base that historically anchored Yaskawa's servo and motion-control demand, and (2) Chinese OEMs are rapidly localizing servo motors and robot controllers, eroding the pricing power and market share Yaskawa built over decades in mainland China. No bullish counterpoint from any tracked publication exists in the log. The conviction is held at medium rather than high because the demographic thesis is slow-moving and Chinese localization risk — while directionally clear — is unevenly priced across the market.

## Outcome tracking
No price-at-call value was recorded for the 2025-03-11 entry, so mark-to-market tracking is not yet possible. The bear thesis would be falsified if Yaskawa demonstrates accelerating order intake from non-Japan, non-China markets (e.g., India, Southeast Asia, or North American reshoring), or if Chinese localization stalls due to quality gaps or geopolitical restrictions on component supply. The thesis gains confirmation if Yaskawa's guidance cuts or margin compression in its motion-control segment outpaces broader industrial peers over the next one to two reporting cycles.
