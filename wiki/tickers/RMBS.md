## Thesis evolution
Irrational Analysis initiated a long on RMBS as one leg of a long MRDIMM / short NAND pair trade. The bull case rests on Rambus being the sole supplier of all five non-DRAM components in an MRDIMM (PMIC, temp sensor, SPD Hub, MDB, MRCD), giving it unusually concentrated leverage to the platform ramp. The deliberate sale of PHY IP assets to Cadence roughly a year prior is read as a strategic signal that management is betting the company on MRDIMM silicon rather than defending a declining licensing base. The residual IP/royalty business (~95% gross margins) is seen as flat-to-declining as Synopsys and Cadence bundle competing controller IP, while the product segment (~63% gross margins) carries management's own 42% CAGR projection. No other publication in the wiki has taken a conflicting view; the call stands unopposed as of the review date.

## Outcome tracking
No price at call was recorded for the 2025-01-05 entry, so mark-to-market assessment is not possible. The thesis is falsified if Intel Granite Rapids ramp stalls, if a competitor enters the MRDIMM component supply chain and compresses Rambus's pricing power, or if the 12800 MT/s Gen2 cycle is delayed beyond the anticipated 12–18 month window from the call date. The thesis gains further support if Rambus reports accelerating product-segment revenue in subsequent quarters as Granite Rapids volumes build.
