## Thesis evolution
The single published view, from irrationalanalysis in December 2025, is bullish. The core argument is structural: EQT's position as the lowest-cost natural gas producer in the US gives it a durable cost advantage that widens its margin when gas prices are depressed and protects downside when peers are squeezed. The secondary driver is secular — rising datacenter power demand is expected to lift domestic natural gas consumption as utilities burn more gas to meet AI infrastructure load. No bearish counterpoint from any tracked publication exists in the call log at this time, leaving the thesis unchallenged but also unconfirmed by a second source.

## Outcome tracking
No price at call was recorded, so quantitative P&L tracking is not possible. The thesis rests on two pillars: (1) EQT maintaining its cost leadership relative to Appalachian peers, and (2) datacenter-driven power demand translating into meaningfully higher natural gas demand. The long view would be falsified if EQT's unit costs converge toward industry averages, if datacenter buildout slows or shifts to non-gas power sources, or if structural oversupply keeps Henry Hub prices below EQT's breakeven for an extended period.
