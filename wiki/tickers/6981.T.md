## Thesis evolution
Citrini's May 2026 call positions Murata as the primary beneficiary of a structural MLCC shortage driven by surging rack-internal power delivery requirements inside AI server clusters. The argument is that as AI accelerator density increases, the passive component bill-of-materials per rack scales non-linearly, and Murata — as the dominant MLCC supplier by market share and process technology — is first in line to capture incremental volume and pricing power. There is only one publication on record, so no cross-publication disagreement exists yet. The view is unambiguously bullish, predicated on supply tightness rather than demand-side uncertainty.
