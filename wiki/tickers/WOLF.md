## Thesis evolution
The view on WOLF reversed sharply between late 2024 and mid-2026. Through early 2026, irrationalanalysis held a bearish structural view: zero non-GAAP gross margins, ~20% workforce reduction, Durham fab closure, and a bankruptcy exit into a crowded SiC market with Chinese competitors racing prices to the floor. The 10KV SiC announcement in April 2026 cracked that view — the author found the product genuinely interesting as a potential category-defining part, but the preliminary datasheet was incomplete, keeping the official stance at SHORT. The flip to LONG came within days via the April 30 market memo, driven by a broadening thesis around 800V power semiconductors for EV and datacenter grid applications, with WOLF as a small-starter position pending LTSpice validation. By May 17, irrationalanalysis upgraded conviction: post-simulation work, the 10KV SiC part was framed as uniquely enabling solid-state transformers (SSTs) for grid applications — a higher-margin, more defensible market than the EV race-to-the-bottom. SemiAnalysis corroborated independently on May 26, placing WOLF in its 800VDC datacenter revolution winners list and citing the 10KV MOSFETs (available March 2026) as a key enabling component for Phase 4 SST conversion stages with a projected $32B TAM by 2030. Both publications converge on the same product-level catalyst; neither disputes the company's difficult operational history.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are available across any of the five entries, so price-based outcome judgment is not possible. The thesis has undergone a qualitative reversal: the original short thesis (structural insolvency, overcapacity, zero margins) has not been falsified on the fundamentals — WOLF remains a post-bankruptcy, high-fixed-cost SiC manufacturer in a crowded market — but a new product-level catalyst (10KV SiC for SSTs) opened a potential path to a differentiated, higher-margin market. The live long view would be falsified if: the full 10KV datasheet reveals inferior temperature/switching performance versus competing high-voltage SiC parts; SST adoption in datacenter grid applications stalls or is delayed past NEC 2032 code support; or WOLF fails to translate product leadership into revenue given its cost structure. Low conviction reflects the coexistence of a genuine product breakthrough with a company that has not yet demonstrated operational recovery.
