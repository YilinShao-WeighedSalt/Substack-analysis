## Thesis evolution
SemiAnalysis initiated coverage on MP Materials in the context of a broader piece on the US labor economy and robotics supply chain, framing MP as a structural beneficiary of reshoring critical materials. The core bull case rests on MP being the sole vertically integrated US producer of rare earth elements — mining through magnet-grade NdFeB output — at a time when Western supply chain policy is actively hostile to Chinese rare earth dominance. No other publication in the related set has weighed in on MP, leaving SemiAnalysis as the lone voice. The 5-10 year ramp caveat signals medium rather than high conviction: the strategic logic is sound but the timeline to meaningful free cash flow is long, and execution risk around the Stage III magnet manufacturing buildout remains the key variable.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call value was recorded at the time of the call, so mark-to-market judgment is not possible from available data. The thesis is primarily structural and multi-year, so near-term price action is secondary to milestones such as commercial-scale NdFeB magnet output from the Fort Worth facility, offtake agreements with US EV or defense customers, and any policy developments (tariffs, ITAR, DoD contracts) that tighten or loosen the competitive moat. The view would be falsified if China floods the market with subsidized NdPr oxide at prices that structurally undercut MP's cost curve, or if Stage III faces sustained delays beyond the stated 5-10 year window.
