## Thesis evolution
The original 2024 stance was bearish: the Incal acquisition was called out as a contrived revenue bridge with zero engineering synergy, and AEHR was flagged as a potential short. The view reversed sharply in July 2025 when the CEO disclosed on an earnings call that two hyperscalers were seriously evaluating wafer-level burn-in of AI logic wafers — an irrational but economically defensible move given how cheap logic-wafer COGS are relative to the total CoWoS/HBM BOM. Through Q3 2025 the author remained a high-conviction long, characterising AEHR as the "GameStop of semiconductors" with real substance, noting repeated short squeezes as confirming evidence and describing the position as one he would size 2–3x larger with more risk appetite. By December 2025 the position was closed with no replacement, and the March 2026 mention was exploratory — rehashing the wafer-level burn-in mechanics and noting a rumour that the key hyperscaler could be AMD — without arriving at a new directional conclusion.
