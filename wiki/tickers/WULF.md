# WULF — TeraWulf

**What it does (plain language):** TeraWulf is a former Bitcoin-mining company that has pivoted into being an AI **"neocloud"** — a landlord that builds data centers and leases the space/power (and sometimes GPUs) to AI companies. It owns power-connected sites and converts them to AI compute capacity.

## Thesis evolution
First entry in the wiki (2026-07-10). TeraWulf enters the book as a beneficiary of the AI data-center capex/debt boom: a bitcoin-miner-turned-neocloud landing a marquee, 20-year, ~$19B contracted-revenue anchor tenant in Anthropic. Sits alongside CoreWeave ([[CRWV]]), Nebius ([[NBIS]]), and IREN ([[IREN]]) in the neocloud cohort that SemiAnalysis and Semi Doped track as the demand-absorption layer of the AI buildout (see [[ai-infrastructure-capex]] and [[buildout-vs-monetization]]).

No priced call yet (px@call n/a — live market feeds egress-blocked on the 2026-07-10 run; no prior wiki mark). The $19B/20-yr Anthropic lease is a large, long-dated demand commitment, but neocloud economics hinge on (a) the tenant's own creditworthiness and survival over 20 years, (b) financing costs on the build (initial capacity not live until H2 2027), and (c) execution on power/permitting. The bull case would be falsified by Anthropic-side credit stress, construction/power delays at Hawesville, or a broader "buildout→overcapacity" turn that re-rates the whole neocloud cohort. Conviction low pending a priced entry and a second corroborating source.
