## Thesis evolution
Global Semi Research initiated coverage on Kioxia with a bullish stance during a broader memory sector selloff in late March 2026, arguing the market was conflating NAND Flash with the DRAM/HBM names that face more direct displacement risk from KV cache efficiency gains. The core view is that KV cache algorithmic changes primarily reduce the memory bandwidth burden on DRAM rather than storage-class NAND, leaving Kioxia's end markets structurally intact. As of this writing, there is only one publication on record and no dissenting view; the thesis is nascent and has not yet been stress-tested by earnings or a competing analyst.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call was recorded at the time of the call, making numerical outcome tracking impossible at this stage. The thesis hinges on the proposition that Kioxia's NAND business is insulated from AI inference efficiency trends — this would be falsified if enterprise SSD demand softens materially as hyperscalers reduce storage attachment ratios, or if NAND ASPs deteriorate beyond what the broader memory cycle would imply. The live view remains intact until evidence of NAND-specific demand destruction from algorithmic changes emerges.

**Update 2026-07-04:** first priced call at ¥83,300 (semidoped LONG). Kioxia has become Japan's most valuable company (passing Toyota) — a vivid marker of how far the AI memory boom has re-rated a NAND-only maker spun out of Toshiba. An IwaiCosmo analyst puts Kioxia "2–4 years ahead" in NAND (wafer-bonding tech), and it began sampling 10th-gen BiCS10 with Sandisk. The bull thesis (NAND insulated from KV-cache DRAM-displacement) is broadly intact and now backed by extreme fundamental performance; the cycle-risk caveat (govt/CXMT capacity coming 2027+) applies to all memory names.
