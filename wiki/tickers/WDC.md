## Thesis evolution
Irrational Analysis has held a consistent and deepening short view on WDC across all three appearances. The original July 2024 note framed WDC as a sub-scale commodity NAND producer with corporate complexity (failed Kioxia merger, Bain IPO pressure) and no durable competitive moat. By January 2025, the short was formalised as a pair trade against RMBS, with new bearish catalysts added: industry dumping, WDC's self-inflicted credibility damage from prematurely declaring the NAND layer-count race over, and spin-off execution risk. The February 2025 note extended the thesis to the spun-out Sandisk entity, technically demolishing the "High Bandwidth Flash" concept and signalling the short would migrate from WDC into standalone Sandisk post-spinout. Citrini held the only bullish position — owning WDC in July 2024 as a broad storage/AI-device-cycle basket name — but did not develop a dedicated thesis, making it a shallow long versus Irrational Analysis's sustained structural bear case.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are available for any of the four entries, so price-based P&L cannot be assessed. The Irrational Analysis bear thesis has gained narrative momentum: each successive note added new corroboration (YMTC dumping confirmed by Micron commentary, rising NAND CapEx from ONTO earnings despite Sandisk pulling back on layer scaling, technical debunking of HBF). The live view as of the last review is a short on WDC transitioning to a direct short on standalone Sandisk post-spinout; the thesis would be falsified if the NAND supply overhang clears faster than expected (e.g., YMTC voluntarily cuts wafer starts) or if HBF achieves credible adoption that rerates the NAND business toward HBM-like economics.
