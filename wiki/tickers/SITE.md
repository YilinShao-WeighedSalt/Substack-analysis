## Thesis evolution
Irrational Analysis initiated a hyper-bullish LONG on SITE in December 2024, upgrading conviction from "high risk" to "medium risk / very high reward" on the basis that the market was mispricing SiTime's true TAM. The core engineering insight is that SiTime's 0-PPM MEMS oscillator technology unlocks content in every active electrical and optical cable and in every hyperscaler system running slightly out-of-spec proprietary Ethernet variants (AWS Elastic Fabric, Google modified Ethernet, etc.) — a TAM the hedge funds bidding the stock on Apple iPhone re-entry and Nvidia GB200 supply-chain rumors had not grasped. The March 2025 GTC note reinforced the thesis with a factual confirmation: SiTime's timing chip appeared on a discrete mini M.2 board inside the GB300 reference design, suggesting routing complexity required off-PCB clock distribution and implying sticky, architecturally significant content rather than a commodity win. No bearish voice appears across these two posts; the sole risk flagged by irrationalanalysis is demand-side: a reversal of Nvidia or Apple orders could cause a 50%+ drawdown given the hedge-fund momentum overlay.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values were recorded for either entry, so absolute return cannot be calculated. The thesis trajectory strengthened between the two publications: the December 2024 call was prospective and engineering-led, and the March 2025 GTC observation provided hardware confirmation of GB300 design-win content. The live view would be falsified by loss of Nvidia rack-scale timing content in a future GPU generation, failure of the 0-PPM active-cable TAM to materialize in volume, or Apple once again designing SiTime out of the iPhone; continued GB-series design wins and hyperscaler adoption of proprietary low-jitter clock architectures would validate it.
