## Thesis evolution
The ABB bull case enters the record in May 2026 through a single SemiAnalysis piece on the 800VDC datacenter power revolution. The thesis is that ABB is a first-mover industrial partner in the Nvidia-led push to raise datacenter bus voltage from 480V to 800VDC, co-developing the core power distribution hardware — busway, solid-state protection, and modular blocks — that the new architecture requires. ABB's strategic bet on DG Matrix, a solid-state transformer startup, signals that the company is positioning for the medium-voltage-to-rack power conversion layer as well, not just the distribution layer. Only one publication covers the name at this stage; there is no bearish counterpoint in the record.

## Outcome tracking
No entry price was recorded at the call date (px@call = n/a), so mark-to-market assessment is not possible. The thesis is early-stage and hinges on 800VDC becoming the dominant datacenter power standard in new hyperscaler builds; the key falsification events would be hyperscalers reverting to or extending 480V architectures, a competing supplier winning preferred-vendor status with Nvidia on the critical hardware layers, or DG Matrix failing to achieve commercial traction in a way that signals ABB misjudged the SST opportunity.
