## Thesis evolution
The short thesis initiated in March 2025 frames FANUC as a structurally vulnerable incumbent: alongside Yaskawa and ABB, it is grouped among the "Big Four" traditional robot makers characterised by low R&D intensity just as Chinese rivals cross 50% global market share and approach cost parity. The July 2025 piece does not contradict or reverse this view — FANUC's dark factory is cited admiringly as a benchmark for Level 0 scripted-motion efficiency, but the framing is descriptive rather than bullish; it illustrates where legacy automation excels while the broader piece argues that higher-autonomy levels (Levels 1–4) are the future growth frontier, areas where FANUC has no acknowledged edge. The publication (semianalysis) is therefore consistently bearish on FANUC's competitive positioning even as it acknowledges the firm's operational excellence in its current niche.

## Outcome tracking
Neither call recorded a px@call value, so mark-to-market assessment is not possible. The bear thesis would be falsified if FANUC meaningfully increased R&D spend relative to peers, recaptured Chinese market share, or demonstrated a credible roadmap into higher-autonomy robotics beyond its scripted-motion core. The July mention of the Zero Downtime monitoring system is a marginal positive data point for the franchise but insufficient on its own to shift the stance. Until a px@call is established or FANUC shows structural competitive improvement, the live view remains short at medium conviction.
