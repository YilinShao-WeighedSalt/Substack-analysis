## Thesis evolution
TSLA started as a peripheral concern in the AI infrastructure coverage universe — mentioned in August 2024 only as a governance risk tied to Musk extracting capital to fund xAI. By year-end 2024, IrrationalAnalysis disclosed a passive ROTH holding, framing it as an acknowledged irrationality rather than a conviction call. SemiAnalysis then developed the most substantive bull thesis in June 2025: Megapack's millisecond-scale frequency response and near-monopoly position in grid-scale BESS make it the structural beneficiary of gigawatt-scale AI datacenter buildout, where BESS is a non-negotiable grid-stability requirement. The two publications diverge sharply on Optimus — SemiAnalysis's June 2026 robotics piece treats Tesla as a laggard relative to Unitree, with zero external humanoid shipments, undermining the robotics optionality that many bulls price in.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are available for any entry, so directional assessment is based on thesis trajectory alone. The Megapack thesis from SemiAnalysis (June 2025) remains structurally intact: AI datacenter power demand has continued to scale, BESS requirements have grown with it, and no credible competitor to Megapack at hyperscaler volumes has emerged in the covered sources. The Optimus bull case, by contrast, has been materially weakened by the June 2026 SemiAnalysis finding that Tesla has zero external humanoid shipments while Unitree is already in productive commercial deployment at scale. The live LONG view on Megapack would be falsified by a credible large-scale BESS competitor entering hyperscaler supply chains, or by evidence that synchronous condensers or demand-response programs substitute for BESS at the datacenter level.
