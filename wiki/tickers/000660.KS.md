## Thesis evolution
The bull case on SK Hynix has been unusually consistent across publications and time: semianalysis established in March 2024 that MR-MUF packaging gave Hynix a durable yield and reliability advantage over both Samsung and Micron, and every subsequent update has reinforced rather than complicated that view. irrationalanalysis overlaid a relative-value angle in late 2024 — preferring 000660 over MU on valuation and explicitly recommending a long/short pair — and the HBM4 base-die revelation in September 2025 (using a logic-process node rather than DRAM) validated the technical thesis precisely when Micron's equivalent effort was reported to yield catastrophically. The sole bearish data point in the log is semianalysis's January 2026 observation that Hynix's V9 NAND lags Micron and Kioxia on cost-per-bit, but all publications treat NAND as a secondary business; the HBM franchise remains unchallenged in the coverage universe. By early 2026 the view had crystallized into Hynix as the default preferred name for institutions wanting HBM exposure, with Micron and Samsung as inferior alternatives.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are recorded across any entry in this log, so direct price-performance verification is not possible. The thesis trajectory is strongly self-consistent: the technical advantages claimed in 2024 (MR-MUF yield, packaging reliability) translated into the reported ~70% Rubin HBM4 allocation and $8B+ EUV commitment by 2026, suggesting the structural thesis has played out operationally. The live view would be falsified by a Samsung HBM4 qualification catch-up that rebalances Nvidia allocation materially away from Hynix, by a KV cache compression breakthrough that structurally reduces per-GPU HBM content, or by a DRAM oversupply event before agentic-AI capex absorbs the capacity being built.
