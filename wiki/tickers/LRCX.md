## Thesis evolution
The LRCX view began as a neutral, dual-edged mention in early 2024: semianalysis flagged it as a structural beneficiary of the 3D DRAM etch/deposition intensity ramp while simultaneously noting CXMT export-control risk, and irrationalanalysis grouped it generically among WFE beneficiaries without a strong directional call. Through 2024–2025 the mentions were largely contextual — China sanctions-evasion risk via Malaysia (a regulatory overhang), neutral memory exposure framing, and incremental process-technology roles at High-NA EUV and advanced DRAM layers — leaving conviction low and stance ambiguous. The view crystallized to LONG in January 2026 when semianalysis identified Lam as the dominant supplier of molybdenum ALD and cryo etch tooling for NAND, a concrete share-gain story at AMAT's expense as Mo wordlines displace tungsten; irrationalanalysis confirmed the position into the subsequent sector selloff, attributing the dip to market misreading rather than fundamental deterioration. Semianalysis is the primary bull; irrationalanalysis is a confirming voice. No publication has taken an explicit short or bearish stance, though the China regulatory exposure remains an unresolved overhang that neither publication has fully dismissed.
