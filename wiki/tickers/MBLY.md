## Thesis evolution
The view on MBLY originated as a tactical short at irrationalanalysis in January 2024, centered on an extraordinary pre-announcement — 50% Q1 revenue contraction, CFO accounting irregularities consistent with kitchen-sinking, and guidance language that arithmetically contradicted itself. By August 2024, the thesis had hardened from cyclical to structural: Qualcomm's automotive platform was actively winning share from Mobileye, and irrationalanalysis concluded the trend was irreversible. SemiAnalysis approaches MBLY from a different angle entirely — not as a standalone equity call but as an Intel asset-stripping exercise, recommending divestiture in December 2024 and noting by mid-2026 that Intel had partially monetized the stake but retains roughly 78%, limiting further capital optionality. There is no bullish voice across the tracked publications; the two outlets differ only in framing (fundamental short vs. corporate-restructuring liability).

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values were recorded across any of the four entries, so direct price-based validation is not possible. The structural bearish thesis — share loss to Qualcomm automotive — would be falsified by evidence of MBLY re-winning design-ins at major OEMs or sustained revenue recovery well above the 2024 trough. The Intel stake overhang identified by SemiAnalysis would be resolved if Intel fully divests the remaining ~78% position; until then it represents a persistent seller and a signal that Intel itself has no confidence in MBLY's standalone trajectory. The thesis has compounded over time rather than reversed, with both publications converging on a negative view through different lenses.
