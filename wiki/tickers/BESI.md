## Thesis evolution
The BESI long was initiated in June 2025 as a conviction play on hybrid bonding and COUPE adoption as secular packaging trends, with no qualification or hedging — it appeared alongside high-confidence semiconductor structural calls. By August 2025 the thesis sharpened around two concrete near-term demand drivers: Broadcom's hybrid-bonding 3.5D XPU platform as the primary catalyst and HBM4 as incremental upside, explicitly not the core case. The March 2026 update added a new supporting argument: Nvidia pushing HBM4 pin speeds to 11 Gbps (above JEDEC's 8 Gbps spec) is creating signal-integrity problems traceable to micro-bump parasitics, which hybrid bonding would eliminate — making industry-level signal-integrity pressure a near-term accelerant for adoption. All three publications are uniformly bullish; there is no dissenting view across the covered period.
