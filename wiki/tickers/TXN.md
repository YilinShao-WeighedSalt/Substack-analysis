## Thesis evolution
The initial view was purely contextual — TXN as a background analog power player. SemiAnalysis turned structurally bearish in early 2025, arguing TXN's high-margin analog franchise was being eroded by customized Chinese entrants in robotics, a thesis driven by supply chain localization rather than any TXN-specific misstep. By April 2026 the narrative had inverted: irrationalanalysis identified TXN as one of only a handful of vendors with competitive high-voltage GaN products suited for 800V datacenter power, rating its integrated driver architecture a genuine system-level moat. Citrini reinforced the bull case the following month, framing TXN's post-EV capex restraint as a setup for ASP expansion as AI datacenter demand inherits the EV power supply chain. The main counterweight is irrationalanalysis itself walking back its LONG one month later — not due to product deterioration but because TXN is too diversified to be a clean expression of the GaN/800V trade; the position was sold in favor of pure-play names like NVTS and IFX.
