## Thesis evolution
Both publications are bullish on SEI, though from different angles and with different depth. [[semianalysis]] built the most detailed structural case: SEI is xAI's primary power-scaling partner, with ~400MW of its 600MW rental turbine fleet already serving xAI and a 50.1/49.9 JV governing the Southaven, Mississippi gas plant; the $112M Q2 2025 JV capex and the >1.1GW target by Q2 2027 frame SEI as a quasi-captive infrastructure vehicle for xAI's Colossus 2 ambitions. [[irrationalanalysis]] endorsed the name three months later in a broader energy section but kept the thesis tighter — SEI earns its place as a "time to power" solution alongside Bloom Energy, with no mention of the JV mechanics or orderbook concentration, and with [[BE]] flagged as potentially superior on merit (800V DC direct-to-datacenter). There is no disagreement between the publications, but [[irrationalanalysis]]'s lighter treatment and explicit Bloom comparison suggest lower conviction than [[semianalysis]]'s structural deep-dive.

## Outcome tracking
Neither call records a px@call value, so quantitative outcome assessment is not possible. The thesis trajectory is intact as of the last review: the JV capex ramp and orderbook concentration around xAI were the bull case drivers identified in September 2025, and the December 2025 endorsement added a second publication's weight without surfacing any contradicting data. The live long view would be falsified by (1) a material reduction in xAI's share of the SEI orderbook, (2) xAI shifting its power strategy away from rental gas turbines toward grid or alternative on-site generation, or (3) Bloom Energy or another competitor winning displacing volume from the Solaris JV.
