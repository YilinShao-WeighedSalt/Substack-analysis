## Thesis evolution
Citrini introduced TDK in July 2024 as a prospective long within a broader "picks and shovels" basket for the on-device AI inference theme, grouping it with Murata (6981.JP) and Taiyo Yuden (6976.JP) as the Japanese RF/passive component triad best positioned to supply next-generation AI smartphones. The thesis is demand-pull: as iPhones and Android flagships require denser, more capable RF front-ends to support edge inference workloads and expanded connectivity, TDK's passive components and electronic components business becomes a structural volume beneficiary. Only one publication covers this name, so there is no cross-publication disagreement; Citrini's stance is unambiguously long but carries lower urgency than core owned positions (TDK is described as "high on list" rather than already purchased).

## Outcome tracking
No price-at-call was recorded for TDK, so direct mark-to-market tracking is not possible from the available data. The thesis depends on two sequenced events: (1) the iPhone 16/17 replacement cycle materializing at the scale Citrini projected (200%+ YoY growth in AI smartphones per IDC), and (2) TDK capturing meaningful unit economics from increased passive component content per device. The view would be falsified if the replacement cycle disappoints — for instance, if Apple Intelligence is delayed or excluded from key markets such as China, suppressing upgrade volumes — or if RF component intensity per handset fails to rise meaningfully. Monitoring Murata and Taiyo Yuden, which share the same thesis, provides useful triangulating evidence for whether the Japanese component trio is playing out as a group.
