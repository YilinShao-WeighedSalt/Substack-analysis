## Thesis evolution
Citrini's July 2024 piece positioned Foxconn (Hon Hai, the world's largest contract electronics manufacturer and Apple's principal iPhone assembler) as the central assembly beneficiary of the on-device AI replacement cycle. By mid-2026 the story had shifted decisively from *phones* to *AI servers*: Foxconn is the key assembler of Nvidia's rack-scale AI systems, and Semi Doped's July 2026 read flags that AI-server revenue has now **overtaken consumer electronics** as Foxconn's largest line. That makes 2317.TW one of the cleanest real-world proxies for how much AI hardware is actually being deployed — a hard-data vote on the [[buildout-vs-monetization]] debate. No bearish voice appears in the tracked publications; conviction stays medium because the name is a low-margin assembler whose fortunes ride on Nvidia's and the hyperscalers' capex holding up.
