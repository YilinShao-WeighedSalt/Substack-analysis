## Thesis evolution
The earliest mentions framed MPWR as a commodity-adjacent power-components vendor competing with Vicor — present in the ecosystem but undifferentiated. By November 2024 irrationalanalysis had formed a structurally bearish view: switching regulators and VRMs are commodities that a small design team can swap out within weeks, meaning MPWR has no durable pricing power and any premium valuation is unjustified. SemiAnalysis reinforced that bear case in December 2024 from a different angle — the transition to hyperscaler-direct VRM procurement for GB300/B300 racks structurally removes MPWR from the Nvidia supply chain, and the stock had already absorbed a 37%+ drawdown reflecting partial pricing-in of that risk. The view softened materially by mid-2025: irrationalanalysis acknowledged MPWR's regulators are best-in-class on datasheet completeness (A+ grade) and moved to a small long position, though the design-out risk and price competition were not dismissed — they were treated as sources of volatility rather than terminal impairment. The two publications thus converge on technical quality but diverge on whether that quality translates to investable moat; irrationalanalysis ultimately sided with a cautious long while semianalysis remains structurally skeptical of the supply-chain position.
