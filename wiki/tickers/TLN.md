## Thesis evolution
SemiAnalysis initiated a LONG view on TLN in March 2026 as part of a broader call on PJM-region independent power producers. Talen's thesis rests on two reinforcing legs: structurally elevated PJM capacity prices (the BRA cleared at a FERC-capped $329/MW-day, a near-10x increase over the prior year) that inflate merchant revenue for any PJM generator, and a differentiated datacenter co-location strategy that positions Talen to capture hyperscaler demand directly at the generation asset rather than through the grid. By May 2026 the co-location angle received concrete confirmation — SemiAnalysis cited Talen alongside Vistra as having contracted nearly 2 GW of new datacenter capacity in Indiana and Mississippi specifically for AWS — upgrading the thesis from a generic IPP capacity-price trade to an identified hyperscaler counterparty relationship. No bearish counterpoint appears in the record; both mentions come from SemiAnalysis and are directionally consistent, with the May note adding specificity rather than revising the view.

## Outcome tracking
Neither call date carries a recorded entry price (both px@call = n/a), so mark-to-market assessment is not possible. Thesis trajectory has been additive: the March 2026 macro tailwind (PJM capacity price spike) was subsequently joined by a named deal with AWS, which reduces the speculative component of the co-location growth leg. The live bull case would be falsified by a material decline in PJM BRA clearing prices (e.g., from FERC intervention or faster-than-expected new supply entry), cancellation or non-renewal of the AWS co-location contract, or a broader hyperscaler capex retrenchment that reduces contracted datacenter load.
