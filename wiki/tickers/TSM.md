## Thesis evolution
From 2024 through mid-2025, the view was near-unanimously bullish: irrationalanalysis treated TSMC as its highest-conviction long, anchored on the collapse of Samsung 3nm yields and Intel Foundry's structural failure leaving TSMC as the only viable advanced-node supplier; semianalysis framed TSMC as the apex predator extending dominance into the GAA era via N2, A16, and CoWoS packaging compounding its ecosystem moat. By late 2025 and into 2026, semianalysis began layering in short-framed arguments — first a hypothetical XRL domestic-fab displacement scenario, then the pointed observation that N3 is running above 100% utilization yet TSMC's pricing discipline is leaving substantial economic rent on the table, and most recently framing TSMC capacity saturation as a binding constraint on global AI buildout rather than a pure pricing windfall. irrationalanalysis remained structurally long but acknowledged TSMC's own pricing power as a systemic risk to the broader AI supply chain it anchors. The resulting mixed stance reflects a consensus on TSMC's irreplaceable technology lead alongside a growing debate over whether the company is monetizing that monopoly adequately — and whether capacity concentration in N3 creates systemic fragility rather than pure upside.

## Outcome tracking
The only price-anchored call in the log is irrationalanalysis's October 2024 mention at $131.58, at which point the author held a long position with highest-conviction framing; the subsequent LONG calls through early 2026 imply the position was held and added to through the DeepSeek dip and Liberation Day volatility. No exit prices are recorded, so precise P&L cannot be determined. The thesis trajectory has broadly played out on the technology side — N2 GAA ramp, Arizona yield parity, and Intel IFS failure all confirmed — but semianalysis's neutrals and shorts signal that the remaining upside question has shifted from "will TSMC maintain its lead?" to "will TSMC extract monopoly pricing?" which remains unresolved. The live view would be falsified by TSMC raising N3/N2 ASPs materially above current contract levels or by a credible second-source competitor emerging before 2028.

**Update 2026-07-04:** first fresh priced anchor since 2024 — $446.68 (Jul 1). Reported Q2 gross margin ~70% and Q3 revenue +10%+ q/q confirm the monopoly-pricing question is resolving in TSMC's favor. ECTC reinforced the CoWoS packaging moat (TSMC leads microfluidic direct-to-silicon cooling) while, for the first time, a credible packaging challenger (Intel EMIB-T, Google TPU v9-bound) appeared on the horizon — the one thing to watch. Separately, TSMC's public caution on ASML High-NA EUV ("30–50% better," maybe not until 2030s) cedes first-mover High-NA to Intel — a small strategic bet worth tracking.
