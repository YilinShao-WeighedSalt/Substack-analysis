## Thesis evolution
Both calls originate from SemiAnalysis and share a single root thesis: Caterpillar is a direct beneficiary of the "bring your own generation" (BYOG) trend in AI datacenter infrastructure, through two distinct product lines. The December 2025 piece introduced the more specific angle — Solar Turbines, a Caterpillar subsidiary, powered the xAI 500 MW+ truck-mounted deployment as 16 MW units rented to sidestep lead times, and management has guided to 2x engine and 2.5x turbine production by 2030, signaling that the company is actively positioning for this demand wave. The March 2026 piece broadened the framing, citing CAT as a backup generator supplier benefiting from the structural shift toward on-site dispatchable capacity accelerated by Winter Storm Fern and the DOE emergency orders that forced datacenters onto their own generation assets. No bearish view appears in the record; the conviction is medium rather than high because CAT is a large industrial conglomerate and the datacenter exposure, while real, remains a subset of a much larger business mix compared to purer-play names like GEV or BE.

## Outcome tracking
No entry prices were recorded at either call date (all px@call = n/a), so precise mark-to-market tracking is not possible. The thesis trajectory is directionally intact: the BYOG buildout that anchored the December 2025 call had grown rather than faded by March 2026, and Winter Storm Fern elevated the strategic value of on-site backup generation broadly, reinforcing both the Solar Turbines and generator product lines. The live bull case would be falsified by evidence that hyperscalers shift away from gas-based onsite generation — toward nuclear microreactors, grid-scale batteries, or fuel cells — before Caterpillar's guided production ramp materializes, or by margin compression within the Energy & Transportation segment that offsets volume gains.
