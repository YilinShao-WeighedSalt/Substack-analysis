## Thesis evolution
irrationalanalysis drove a high-conviction long thesis through most of 2024, anchored on the Grace CPU royalty ramp — projecting a step-function increase in datacenter royalty revenue as GB200 NVL72 drove Grace shipment volumes 10-20x above the GH200 baseline, with ARM's percentage-of-ASP royalty structure making the company an indirect beneficiary of even poor Grace yields. That bull case broke decisively after October 2024: ARM's decision to cancel Qualcomm's ALA, which the author called a "Chernobyl moment," was framed as a catastrophic own-goal that handed RISC-V a marketing gift and motivated every licensee except Nvidia and Apple to accelerate ISA migration. The December 2024 trial outcome confirmed the bear turn — ARM lost, the ALA rate was revealed at only 1.1% through 2032 (half or less of prior assumptions), and call options purchased for the event expired worthless. From early 2025 onward, irrationalanalysis settled into a structural short, adding Nvidia's design-out of ARM-RTL in Vera and SoftBank's float manipulation as additional negatives. semianalysis disagrees, maintaining a structural long based on hyperscaler custom silicon (Graviton5, Cobalt 200, Axion, Phoenix) displacing x86 at scale and generating a durable royalty uplift — a thesis irrationalanalysis partially acknowledged in September 2025 before reverting to bearish on supply constraints in May 2026.

## Outcome tracking
The one px@call data point is $92.30 (February 2024 NEUTRAL), after which the stock reportedly reached approximately $178 by July 2024 — validating the May 2023 high-conviction long that was established well below that level. The December 2024 trial outcome was a direct falsification of the core bull catalyst: the royalty rate locked in at 1.1% through 2032 rather than the assumed 2.5%+, and call options bought for the event expired worthless, confirming the bear turn. The structural short thesis — v9 royalty stall, Vera design-out, Qualcomm remaining on v8 — has been consistent since early 2025, but is contested by semianalysis's hyperscaler custom ARM thesis, which points to Graviton5, Cobalt 200, and Axion as compounding royalty growth drivers. The live view would be falsified for bears if ARM reports accelerating datacenter royalty revenue driven by hyperscaler CSS adoption despite the Qualcomm headwind; it would be falsified for bulls if further licensees renegotiate ALA rates downward or accelerate RISC-V transition following the Qualcomm precedent.
