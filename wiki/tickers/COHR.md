## Thesis evolution
Coverage began in early 2024 with consensus bullishness across irrationalanalysis and semianalysis: Coherent's low-phase-noise laser IP and OCS pipeline were seen as structural advantages in the 800G/1.6T transceiver cycle, with the author of irrationalanalysis explicitly targeting a ~$80 entry. The view fractured sharply in mid-2025 when irrationalanalysis pivoted to short on manufacturing quality concerns — specifically, that the monolithic InP MZI architecture suffers poor yield, that 200G VCSEL claims are physically impossible, and that management credibility trails Lumentum; this skepticism has been the dominant irrationalanalysis stance through mid-2026. The bullish counter-case, held by semianalysis/semidoped and globalsemiresearch, rests on Coherent's unique 6-inch InP wafer scale (two sites, strong reported yields), its US/EU tariff-resilient footprint, and the CPO external laser TAM expansion — with the most recent globalsemiresearch note raising CW laser unit guidance from ~40M to ~100M and citing confirmed CPO shipment ramp by 2027. The live debate is therefore a direct empirical dispute over 6-inch InP yield reality: irrationalanalysis says the yield data is implausible given EML sourcing from Lumentum and margin inconsistencies; semidoped and globalsemiresearch say the ramp is verified and unit economics are improving.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are recorded across any entry, so quantitative P&L tracking is not possible. The thesis trajectory shows that the early long thesis (OCS + low-phase-noise laser moat) gave way to a contested short/long split as the focus shifted to 6-inch InP yield execution — the key falsifier for the current irrationalanalysis short is a sustained improvement in Coherent's gross margin profile and direct evidence of CPO laser shipments at scale, which the globalsemiresearch June 2026 note claims is now occurring. Conversely, the long case (semidoped, globalsemiresearch) would be falsified if yield problems resurface in reported margins or if CPO ramp timelines slip beyond 2027. The OCS sub-thesis remains a secondary overhang: irrationalanalysis consistently flags insertion-loss disadvantage versus Lumentum MEMS, and this has not been resolved in the public record.
