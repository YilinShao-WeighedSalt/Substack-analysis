## Thesis evolution
The view on SKYT has been consistently long across both publications, held as a pure US-national-security positioning play rather than a technology quality bet — the author is explicit that SKYT's PDK and node quality are poor. The August 2025 entry frames it as a speculative long paired with Frequency Electronics (FEIM), treating both as military-niche timing/fab plays with captive DoD demand that insulates them from commercial competition. By December 2025 the framing tightened: SKYT is described simply as the only viable US military old-node (130/90nm) foundry option, with no competing thesis from any other publication in the source base. The conviction level is low given the acknowledged technology deficiencies; the bull case rests entirely on the absence of any domestic alternative for classified and trusted-foundry military designs.

No px@call values are recorded for either entry, so price-based performance cannot be assessed.
