## Thesis evolution
The thesis originated in August 2024 as a structural long on Keysight's wireline T&M franchise: its oscilloscopes and BERTs are described as best-in-class by a massive margin for 224G SerDes R&D, with competing products from Anritsu and R&S characterized as "outright unusable," and the add-on software/SaaS attach plus mandatory annual calibration create a durable recurring-revenue flywheel that scales with the installed base. Through late 2024 the author deepened conviction after the FQ4 2024 earnings beat — attributing the gross-margin outperformance directly to demand for high-end gear tied to the 224G Ethernet and PCIe Gen 7 upgrade cycle — and explicitly listed it as "one of the safest positions" in the portfolio, with wireless risk acknowledged but treated as largely contained. By 2025 the emphasis shifted toward KEYS as the safest derivative play on AI datacenter investment broadly: the optical/photonics ramp (CPO, LPO, 1.6T transceivers) is portrayed as a multi-year demand driver for Keysight's high-end instrumentation, the author added to the position with Verizon proceeds in November 2025, and the December 2025 annual ideas post concludes with KEYS as the "safest AI datacenter play" with competitors making "unusable dogshit." No publication in the covered set ever expressed a bearish or even cautious view on KEYS; the stance is uniformly long at every mention across 16 months.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values are available for any entry in the log, so quantitative P&L cannot be assessed. The thesis trajectory is strong: every predicted demand driver — the 224G/PCIe-7 hardware upgrade cycle, hyperscaler R&D intensity, and the optical/photonics buildout — has continued to materialize or accelerate through the covered period, and management's FQ4 2024 gross-margin beat was cited as direct validation of the premium-hardware demand thesis. The live view would be falsified by a sustained collapse in hyperscaler and AI ASIC R&D spending (reducing demand for high-end BERTs and oscilloscopes), a credible competitor closing Keysight's jitter/performance gap in wireline test gear, or a structural deterioration in the SaaS/calibration attach economics.
