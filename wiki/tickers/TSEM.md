## Thesis evolution
The view opened in late 2024 as a straightforward silicon photonics fab beneficiary: Tower's specialty SiPho process nodes gave it a structural lead over GloFo (GFUS/GFS) for PIC/CPO ramp, and GloFo's customer-stuffing reputation made Tower the credible alternative. The thesis briefly softened in Feb 2025 (RFFE drag dilutes the pure-play story) and flipped to cautiously short in Apr 2025 when TSMC's packaging lock-in policy appeared to threaten Tower's photonics TAM. That concern resolved quickly — by Aug 2025 the author had rebuilt a large long, anchored by the disclosure that Nvidia ported its ring-modulator SiPho design to Tower, confirming Tower as a critical node in the 1.6T transceiver supply chain. From Sep 2025 onward the author disclosed it as a top-two position and the thesis expanded to include pricing power (SiN PIC COUPE process), an Intel breakup fee tailwind, and GloFo secular weakness. The only remaining ambiguity as of early 2026 is CPO adoption speed; a Mar 2026 note shifted from bearish to neutral on near-term CPO share, acknowledging lower-speed PIC designs as credible, but this did not alter the long stance. In June 2026 Semi Doped flagged Tower and Marvell crossing 5 million coherent photonic ICs shipped — the first concrete volume data point, framed as a deliberate flex against GlobalFoundries.

## Outcome tracking
No px@call values were recorded across the first 18 appearances (all pre-2026-06); the June 2026 semidoped LONG at $287 is the first priced call and is open (< 30 days). The thesis trajectory is strongly self-consistent: the brief Apr 2025 short was reversed within four months and the author's subsequent position sizing (second-largest trading account holding by Sep 2025, "do not touch" core by Apr 2026) implies the long has been rewarding. Key falsifiers for the live long are: (1) a competitor (TSMC, imec fab partnership, or GloFo) wins a meaningful share of Nvidia's SiPho PIC orders away from Tower, or (2) CPO adoption stalls long enough that Tower's specialty node utilization falls materially before the SiN COUPE ramp offsets RFFE revenue decline.
