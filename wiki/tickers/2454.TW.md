## Thesis evolution
irrationalanalysis opened the coverage period constructively, treating MediaTek as the structurally superior smartphone SoC vendor versus QCOM and a credible low-cost path for Google to diversify its TPU supply away from Broadcom. The bull case peaked in February 2025 when the ISSCC 2025 SerDes presentation lifted the author's probability of a Google TPU V7E win from ~30% to ~80%, and MediaTek was recommended as a hedge for Broadcom longs. The thesis broke sharply in May 2025 when NVLink Fusion's ecosystem reshaping exposed the underlying SerDes execution failure: two failed attempts on both N4P and N3P processes, with roughly half the SerDes team reported to have defected to Nvidia. The November 2025 technical post reinforced the SHORT — MediaTek's dual DFE tap and ADC-interleave gain architecture generates burst-error vulnerability that makes it unsuitable for hyperscaler half-RS-FEC deployments. A partial rehabilitation theory emerged in December 2025 (Semtech ACC linear equalizers potentially masking the design flaws), but the author flagged it as unverified and speculative. The April 2026 SemiAnalysis mention is a design-strength positive on the consumer SoC side (xBIT SRAM, Dimensity 9500) but does not address the datacenter SerDes failure. Citrini's 2024 LONG was a basket position on edge AI/Android SoC exposure and was never updated to reflect the TPU SerDes outcome. In June 2026 Semi Doped introduced a new bullish vector: Google has reportedly committed to ~3 million TPUs designed with MediaTek and packaged using Intel EMIB rather than Broadcom/TSMC CoWoS, targeted for ~2028. If confirmed, this would rehabilitate the datacenter bull case that was destroyed by the May 2025 SerDes failure, though via a packaging-driven (EMIB) route rather than the original SerDes execution thesis. The stance is now mixed: the datacenter SHORT from SerDes failure remains unresolved, but the EMIB/TPU LONG from Semi Doped represents a credible new path.

## Outcome tracking
